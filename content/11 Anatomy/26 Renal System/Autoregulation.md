# Myogenic 

Arterioles and capillaries undergo ==vasoconstriction/vasodilation== based on ==changes in blood pressure== to maintain [[glomerular filtration rate]] 
Note: *only works when arterial BP within 80 and 180mmHg*
![[Pasted image 20250401220603.png|center]]
### Reduced blood flow
↓ blood flow/glomerular blood pressure → initial ↓ GFR → ↑ D$_{AA}$ + D$_{GC}$ + ↓ D$_{EA}$  → ↑ Blood input + ↓ Blood output → ↑ [[glomerular filtration rate#^4bfc38|Hydrostatic Pressure]] → ↑ GFR back to normal

### Rise in blood pressure

↑ renal blood pressure → afferent arterioles wall initial stretch → smooth muscles contract→ ↓D$_{AA}$ + D$_{GC}$ + ↑ D$_{EA}$→ ↓ blood flow




# Tubuloglomerular feedback

Involves combination of cells of [[Juxtaglomerular Apparatus]] at the [[Distal convoluted tubule]] 

[[Juxtaglomerular Apparatus#Macula Densa cells|Macula Densa cells]] detect change in salt levels at [[Distal convoluted tubule]] 
trigger [[Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System (RAAS)]] and [[#Myogenic]] Response

### Increased salt
Too much salt in body, signal to glomerulus → trigger [[cardiac hormones]] → ↑ [[glomerular filtration rate]] + excretion 


### Decreased salt levels
Too little salt in body, [[Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System (RAAS)]] → ↑ Reabsorption  + ↓ [[glomerular filtration rate]]
