
## Characteristics
**Blood Volume**:

Vol of fluid in person ~5L, ~8% body weight
Substances of blood:  Plasma > Erythrocytes(RBCs) > platelets > WBCs

### Plasma
- **Guiding questions**
	- What substances are contained in plasma?
	- Which are the most abundant?
	- What does plasma do?

**Function:** Medium for transporting nutrients and waste around body
**Traits:** 
- ==55% of Blood vol==
- ==Water(92%)== - solvent for other components
- Plasma Proteins(7%): ^564a64
	- Albumins (60%) - Contribute Osmotic Pressure(water balance) , Transport steroid, hormones ^4f10bd
	- Globulins (35%) - immune functions Transport ions, hormones, lipids
	- Fibrinogen (4%) - clotting system, converts to insoluble fibrin
	- Regulatory proteins (<1%) - enzymes, proenzymes, hormones
- Other Solutes(1%)
	- Electrolytes - ions for osmotic pressure e.g. Na⁺, K⁺, SO₄2⁻,
	- Organic nutrients - for adenosine triphosphate ATP(RBC energy source) production
	- Organic wastes - for excretion e.g. urea, uric acid, [[creatinine]]

### Erythrocytes
- **Guiding Questions**
	- What do they do?
	- What features enable them to perform their function?
**Function:** 
- transporting O₂ and CO₂ via Hemoglobin
- buffer pH of blood

**Traits:**
- Body's only fluid tissue
- pH: ==7,35~7.45==
- ==~45% of blood vol==, **avg ~5 million cells/mm3 of blood** , (4.5-6.5 mil(M), 3,9-5.6mil(F))
- Hematocrit → % of total blood vol occupied by ==packed RBCs in centrifuge== → ==avg 40% ==(~40 - 54% (M) , 37-47% (F))
	
Contains:
- Hemoglobin  → avg 14g of Hb/dL (14-17g Hb/dL (M), 12-16g Hb/dL (F)) ^ed39ff
- Carbonic anhydrase: CO$_{2}$ + H$_{2}$O → H$_{2}$CO$_{3}$ -> H$^{-}$ + HCO$_{2}^{-}$
- Lifespan: **100~120 days**
- produced via [[Erythropoiesis process and factors|Erythropoiesis]] 
			![[Pasted image 20250201135522.png]]
#### To facilitate O$_{2}$ Transport
 **Lack Nucleus & Organelles:**
	To carry more O$_{2}$  and reduce load on heart
	- No mitochondria → rely on ==anaerobic respiration(glycolysis)== fuel by ATP → does not consume O$_{2}$
	- No Nucleus/ribosomes → ==more space for Hemoglobin== → cannot repair itself, divide, or create proteins → produced via ==Erythropoiesis==

 **Biconcave Shape:**
	- For ==high surface area-volume ratio== -> Gases diffuse quickly
	- + flexibility for smooth transport in bloodstream

**Hemoglobin structure:**
	- **Globin** portion with ==4 protein chains==, each have 1 Heme(Fe) molecule
	- ==1 Heme== can bind==(not strongly)== to ==1 O$_{2}$ molecule==
