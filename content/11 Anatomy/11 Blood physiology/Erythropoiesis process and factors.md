## Process
- Guiding Questions
	- What are the crucial stages involved in the process?
	- When does Hb production start and end?

- Early:
	In bone marrow, Genes in myeloid cells are triggered to develop as RBCs
	==Start as Proerythroblasts ==→ once genes get expressed

- Middle:
	Basophilic Erythroblast  stage → Cell is largest Ribosomes start producing Hb
	Polychromatophilic Erythroblasts stage → ↑hemoglobin synthesis, ribosome content ↓

- Late:
	==Normoblasts(late)== stage → Cell is smallest → ==Hb accumulation finishing ribosome content much ↓==, ready to ==eject Nucleus==
	When Nucleus ejected → cell ==now called Reticulocyte== → small amt ribosome for final Hb synthesis → Once ==Leave Bone marrow== → ==mature Erythrocyte formed==

## Regulating Factors
- Guiding Questions
	- What substances affect erythropoiesis and where do they come from
- Erythropoietin(EPO)
	==Hormone== produced and secreted in Kidneys
	Liver also secretes (~10%)
	Causes homeostasis Reaction
	**Activating Stimuli:** ^1c038c
	- ==Hypoxia== (insufficient O2)
		- Due to altitude
		- Exercise
	- ==Hemorrhage== (bleeding) -> reduced RBC count in blood
	- Lack of Hb per RBC -> Iron deficiency 

- Androgen
	Hormone more prevalent in Males, higher RBC in M > F

## RBC Recycling + Turnover
- Guiding Questions
	- Where do RBCs go to be broken down?
	- What happens to the materials that are produced?

**Near end of erythrocyte lifespan:**
	Engulfed + broken down by macrophages in [[Spleen]]
	Separated into Heme and Globin components

**Heme: 2 components**

|                                                                                                        |                                              |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------- |
| Fe2+ which is ==stored in Ferritin==/Hemosiderin released into blood -> ==transported in Transferrin== | Ferro = iron Hemosiderin = group of Ferritin |
| ==Bilirubin==: Chemical substance, ==yellow pigment== -> sent to liver, excrete via bile & feces       | Bile = fluid mixture of waste products       |
|                                                                                                        |                                              |

**Globin:**
	Broken down into ==amino acids== -> sent to digestive track reabsorb in bloodstream -> can be recycled into new globin and erythrocytes
