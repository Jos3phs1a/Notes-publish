### Platelet Phase

	- What do platelets do when there is damage?

Platelets ==normally inactive:== Repelled due to glycoproteins on platelet surface

Upon wound detected → nearby platelets will:
1. Flow to injury site, bind to exposed collagen, form platelet plug, possess ==spike processes + stickier== → **Von Willebrand Factor (VWF)** assist in ==adhesion + activates platelets==

2. When activated:
	Platelets ==degranulate ≠ megakaryocytes break down to give platelets== → Release
	1st part of hemostasis
	- **Adenosine Diphosphate (ADP)**: enhance secretion and aggregation of other platelets (chain effect)
	- **Thromboxane A2**: enhance vascular spasm
	- Platelet Activating factor: an [[Inflammatory mediator]] 
	
	2nd part of hemostasis
	- **Calcium(F4)**: for Extrinsic and Intrinsic pathways
	- **Factor 13**: Fibrin stabilizer
	- **Platelet Factor 3**: Activation Thrombin ==(not one of the 13 factor)==
- Growth factors (not as involved in hemostasis)
	- PDGF: activate fibroblasts → stimulate proliferation(repair) of skin ^2aa5a6
	- VEGF: Stimulate **Angiogenesis** → growth of new blood vessels

1. ADP causes more platelets to undergo aggregation & secretion →  ==Positive feedback loop== → Platelet Plug forms
