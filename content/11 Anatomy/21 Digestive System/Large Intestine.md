## Structure
Components:
- Caecum - used to identify [[Appendix]]
- Ascending Colon
- Transverse Colon
- Descending Colon
- Sigmoid Colon
- Rectum

Flexures exist to 'turn' within the abdominal cavity, connecting the main portions of colon
**Right colic/hepatic flexure** → Ascending to Transverse
**Left colic/splenic flexure** → Transverse to Descending

**Characteristic features**
- Haustrations: bulge + depressions ^de1dee
- Appendices epiploicae: fat droplets
- Taeniae coli: 3 longitudinal muscles, helping w moving food substances


## Physiology
Function: primarily drying and storage organ for feces
Secretes: mucus + ions
Digestion: no digestion but bacteria acts on undigested food for vitamin K, B12, folate etc
Absorption: water + ions

#### Motility
1. contractions of [[Large Intestine#^de1dee|Haustra]] shuffle food back & forth
- slow + nonpropulsive
1. Mass movements propel feces long distances into distal portion for [[defecation reflex]]
	- Large segments of ascending & transverse colon contract simultaneously

#### Absorption
- absorbs salt & water, converting the luminal contents into feces
- small amt of absorption occurs but not as much as small intestine
- Na$^+$ actively absorbed
- Cl$^-$ follows down gradient
- water follows osmotically

#### Secretions
colon contains microbiota and microbiome that act on food, side effect producing :
Intestinal gases:
- flatus: aka farts
- swallowed air
- bacterial fermentation
Large intestine secretion is entirely protective
- colonic secretion consists of alkaline mucus solution
