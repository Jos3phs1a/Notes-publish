## Function
1. **M**astication → Mechanical breakdown of food into smaller pieces via action of teeth, tongue, palatal surface
2. **I**nitiation of swallowing (deglutition)
3. **S**aliva & mucus lubrication by mixing 
4. **T**aste Sensation
5. limited digestion of carbohydrates and lipids

## Accessory organs
6. [[Teeth]]
7. [[Tongue]]
8. [[Salivary glands]]


## Structure
**Roof**
	hard & soft palate 
	lined with keratinized squamous epithelium → endure wear & tear

**Floor:**
	- thin vascular layer of mucosa + geniohyoid + mylohyoid muscles, tongue sits on them
	- lined with stratified squamous epithelium which is non keratinized → thin layer for fast absorption e.g. medicine

**Lateral walls:**
	pads of fat + buccinator muscle
	not involved in compressing [[Oral Cavity#^e1ae3d|bolus]] → lined with non keratinized SE
**Uvula:**
	fleshy extension at back of the mouth
	the 'punching bag'
	contributes to [[Oral Cavity#Pharyngeal Phase|swallowing]]






## 4 phases of swallowing
BPES
#### Buccal Phase
- Bolus(ball of food ^e1ae3d) is formed, during chewing
- once swallowing begins, bolus compressed against hard palate
- Soft palate elevates close of nasopharynx
- Tongue retracts, push bolus posteriorly into oropharynx
- bolus enters oropharynx
#### Pharyngeal Phase
- bolus touches palatoglossal, palatopharyngeal arches & posteriors pharyngeal wall
- Larynx elevation + folding of epiglottis send bolus past glottis.
- Uvula + soft palate block backflow to nasopharynx
#### Esophageal Phase
- contraction of pharyngeal muscles, forcing bolus through esophagus
- peristalsis carries bolus down to stomach

#### Entering Stomach
- bolus triggers lower esophageal sphincter to open allowing it to enter stomach
