- serosa = serum, clear liquid
- Definition: thin membrane covering surface of internal organs
- Outer most layer of gut
- lubricating functions

## Peritoneum
- type of serosa membrane
- Layer of membrane lined with simple squamous epithelium similar to [[Anatomy of heart, great vessels, mediastinum structures#^0edada|pericardium]] 
- contains 2 layers:
	- visceral peritoneum - lines surface of organs
	- parietal peritoneum - lines internal surface of abdominal wall
	- peritoneal cavity - space between that contains serous fluid

Omentum: Double layer of visceral membrane superior and inferior to stomach 
Mesentery: Double layer of visceral membrane attaching to posterior abdominal walls located at small intestine and colon

intraperitoneal: organs within peritoneal cavity
retroperitoneal: organs outside peritoneal cavity ^a3ebb8


## Adventitia
- outermost layer of connective tissue covering organ
- anchors organs to surrounding tissue 
- covers [[Serosa#^a3ebb8|retroperitoneal]] organs oral cavity, thoracic esophagus, ascending colon, descending colon, and rectum
