
## mechanism

1: Production of Ion
	positive Ion produced via dissolving CO$_2$ convert using carbonic anhydrase
	$$CO_2 + H_2O ⇌ HCO_{3}^- + H^+ $$
	
2: H$^+$ - K$^+$ ATPase pump
	Use of active transport to swap high conc K$^+$ in stomach with low conc H$^+$ in parietal cell 

3: K$^+$ channel allow passive leak back
	K$^+$ diffuses through luminal K$^+$ channels back into stomach

4: Cl$^-$ - HCO$_{3}^-$  antiport
	HCO$_{3}^-$ ions leaves the parietal cell via channel, using secondary active transport, Cl$^-$ is taken in to maintain charge balance
	
5: Cl$^-$ channel
	In cell: Cl$^-$ conc high
	In gastric lumen: Cl$^-$ low
	Cl$^-$ exit via passive diffusion
	merge with H$^+$ to form HCl
