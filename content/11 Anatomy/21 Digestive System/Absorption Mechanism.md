# Nutrients
## Diffusion

### Simple Diffusion
- [[Nutrient Classes#^77798d|Monoglycerides + Fatty Acids]]  
- Are the only nutrients that avoid liver processing as they are transported by lymphatic system 
#### Mechanism
- transported by [[micelles]] onto brush border
- passive absorbed through plasma membrane
- reforms triglycerides and transported through lymph nodes in form of [[Chylomicrons]] which are water soluble
### Facilitated Diffusion
- [[Nutrient Classes#^2edfcd|fructose]](GLUT-5) + [[Nutrient Classes#^8bd8cb|Amino Acids]]
#### Mechanism
- ==Solute molecule(absorbed nutrients)== binds to carrier protein on walls of plasma membrane
- Carrier protein changes shape to send solute molecule to exit
## Active Transport

### Primary Active
- Sodium

#### Mechanism
Na$^+$ - K$^+$ pump
### Secondary Active
- [[Nutrient Classes#^2edfcd|Glucose & Galactose]]([[GLUT]]-2) + [[Nutrient Classes#^8bd8cb|Amino Acids + peptides]] 

#### Mechanism
- Inside lumen: Solute molecule ==low conc==, Driving ion(Na$^+$) ==high conc==
- Inside Cell: Solute molecule ==high conc==, Driving ion(Na$^+$) ==low conc==
- Symporter fueled by ==Energy from Na$^+$ gradient==, **not directly from ATP**
- Na$^+$ and solute molecule taken in by symporter, transported into the cell together

**Tertiary Active**
- H$^+$ Symport used to transport small peptides
Setup:
- Na$^+$- H$^+$ antiport used to transport H$^+$ out of the cell

# Water

- Active absorption of electrolytes & nutrients ==create osmotic gradient==, high water conc in lumen, low water conc in cell
- Duodenum: water flows into lumen due to hyperosmotic chyme
- Jejunum & Ileum, water is absorbed

# Vitamins

1. Water soluble:
	- Vitamin B & C complex
	- absorbed passively with water through carrier mechanisms
2.  [[Metabolism of different organic compounds#Fat-soluble|Fat-soluble]]:
	- Vitamins A, D, E, K 
	- carried in [[micelles]], absorbed passively with other lipids
3. Vitamin B12
	- Requires formation of complex by binding to gastric intrinsic factor
	- Absorbed by [[endocytosis#Receptor-mediated endocytosis|Receptor-mediated endocytosis]]
