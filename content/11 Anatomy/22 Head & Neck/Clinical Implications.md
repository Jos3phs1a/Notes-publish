
## Temporomandibular joint dislocation
- Anterior dislocation most common
- Mandibular head moves forward & out
- Held against the articular tubercle
- Aided by lax capsule and ligaments

## Bell's Palsy/Lower/ Upper Motor Neuron Lesions

Definition: paralysis of facial muscles due to [[lesions]] formed block the innervation to facial muscles
- The lower the lesion, the more it affects the body
- lesion affects contralateral side ie right side form lesion, left side body loses control
symptoms: face drooping to due ==loss of control of muscles==
	Lower → Bell's Higher(full blockage of nerves) Upper → Stroke/tumor/trauma (partial blockage of nerves)
