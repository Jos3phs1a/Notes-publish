## Creatine Phosphate
- ==Fast, Low efficiency==
- Obtained from food, stored in muscles

During metabolism

PCr → Cr via creatine kinase, phosphate group given to ADP → ATP

## Oxidative Phosphorylation
- aka **aerobic respiration**
- ==Slow, high efficiency==
- Occurs inin mitochondria:
	Glucose + O2 → pyruvate + ATP via Krebs cycle
	==NAD$^+$ used up becomes NADH==, required for transporting electrons used in membrane of mitochondria. O$_2$ + NADH undergo oxidation reaction, regenerates NAD$^+$

Who process quite slow, ==insufficient during high intensity training.==

Lipids and Glycogen can be used in krebs cycle to feed into energy stores
Protein unlikely→ sign of starvation
## Glycolysis
- aka **anaerobic respiration**
- ==Fast, low efficiency, produces [[lactate]]==
- Glucose → ATP + pyruvate acid (no O2)

NAD(Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) used up

Since **no O$_2$** :
to regenerate NAD, pyruvate + NADH → lactic acid + NAD (Lactic acid fermentation)
Lactic acid build up → [[Acidosis]] → soreness + fatigue



Note: in exercise, ==glycolysis is always involved in the initial portion==. In **aerobic exercise**, glycolysis still occurs, but ==oxidative phosphorylation will be dominant==
