Muscles can adapt based on demand placed on them

# Characteristics affected

### Oxidative capacity
more mitochondria available
primarily induced through [[Physiological adaptations to Aerobic exercise|aerobic exercise]] 
### Muscle cross sectional area 
aka muscle tone
Increase → [[hypertrophy]]  Decrease → [[muscular conditions#Atrophy|atrophy]]
Result of or lack of [[Physiological adaptations to interval training|Anaerobic]]/[[Physiological Adaptations to Resistance training|resistance training]]
Affected by testosterone levels: ↑ lvls promote myosin & actin synthesis

### Interconversion between fibre types
mostly genetic but small conversion can occur based on training
### Muscle repair
satellite cells(stem cells in skeletal muscle)  become myogenic precursor when there is muscle damage
### Number of muscle fibres
Hyperplasia/Myofibre splitting, testosterone, steroids, vigorous weight training ↑ no. of myofibres. Sarcopenia opposite ↓ no. of myofibres
