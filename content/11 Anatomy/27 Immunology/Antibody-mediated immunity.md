## Contribution to immune system
cannot directly destroy pathogens on binding with antigens
function

### Physically hinder antigens 
1) antibodies combine with pathogens by binding to surface, prevent viruses from entering cell
2) **agglutination** - multiple antibody molecules form cross links, cause target cells to bind together, prevent spread of infections
### Amplify innate immune response
1) Activating [[The Complement system#Classical pathway|Classical pathway of complement system]] by mediating the binding of C1 to antigen to form antigen-antibody complex 
2) Enhance [[phagocytosis]] especially IgG antibody
3) Stimulate [[Immunological surveillance]] via binding of antibody to antigen


## Primary response
first contact with new antigen, antibody response delayed until plasma cells formed ~ roughly 2 weeks
- mediated by IgM antibodies 
- Concentration declines after infection due to short lifespan of plasma cells


## Secondary
same antigen detected, memory cells able to respond in short time 
- Stronger and faster immune response
- memory cells ready, release high concentration of IgG antibodies compared to [[Antibody-mediated immunity#Primary response|Primary response]] 
- IgG has longer life span, concentration declines slower
