[[cytokines]] /small proteins released by activated [[White blood cells#Lymphocytes|Lymphocytes]] and [[White blood cells#Macrophages/Monocytes|Macrophages]] 
bind to receptors on normal cell and trigger antiviral protein in cytoplasm which prevent viral replication inside cell, slowing down infection

3 types:

### Type 1 IFNs
first to respond, primarily mediate the antiviral defense of the innate immune system.
produced by all cells with nucleus
- alpha 
	attract and stimulate [[Immunological surveillance|NK cells]]
- beta
	slows inflammation in damaged area

### Type 2 IFNs
involved in regulating immune responses and activating macrophages, crucial for adaptive immunity
- gamma
	secreted by [[Immunological surveillance|NK cells]] stimulate [[White blood cells#Macrophages/Monocytes|Macrophages]]
