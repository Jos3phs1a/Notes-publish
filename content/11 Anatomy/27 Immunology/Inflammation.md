nonspecific response to tissue injury [[phagocytosis]] main process of mediation

Function: 
- bring phagocytes to injured area to remove invaders + debris, necrotic cells and tissue resulting from injury
- initiate repair process
	- secretion of chemical mediators and growth factors
	- inflammatory cells & [[White blood cells#Macrophages/Monocytes|macrophages]]
	- growth factors for cell division during [[Healing Process#Proliferative|Proliferative]] stage

## Vascular event

- mast cells release ==inflammatory mediators== e.g. bradykinin, histamine, substance P → capillary dilation & permeability
	- ↑ Redness
	- ↑ Heat
	- swelling (↑capillary permeability & protein leakage)
	- Pain (↑ Pressure & nerve irritation by chemical mediators
- ==Can result in ↓ function==
- expansion in capillary walls to allow ==monocytes and neutrophils to pass through== to attack pathogens
	recall monocyte = immature macrophage in blood vessel
	macrophage = in tissue 
## Cellular event
- macrophages in tissue sense unwanted substances → activate and engulf + release [[cytokines]] that ==trigger or heighten inflammation==
	examples:
	- TNF tumor necrosis factor
	- Interleukin IL-1 IL-6 
	- chemotaxins IL-8
	- prostaglandins
	- Bradykinin
	- [[Function of platelets in Hemostasis]]  


- Stasis: subsequent slowing of blood flow due to ↑ RBCs clumping together → pushes [[White blood cells]] towards the side of blood vessels
- Margination: Leukocytes adhere to vessel wall with the aid of protein receptors
- Diapedesis: Leukocytes squeeze out towards interstitial fluid
- Chemotaxis: Chemicals guide migration of WBC towards injury site
