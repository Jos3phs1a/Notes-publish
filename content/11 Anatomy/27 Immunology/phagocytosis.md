3 main steps

# Recognition

- Pathogens with presenting proteins/antigens are scanned and recognised by leukocytes
#### 1: Chemotaxis
- Chemotaxis bring the pathogen and leukocytes closer together

#### 2: Adherence
- cell surrounds the pathogen, sticking it to membrane 

# Engulfing

- surrounded pathogen is transported into cell via phagosome

# Elimination

#### 4: Digestion
lysosomes containing acidic/toxic chemicals transported to phagosome with pathogen
mix together and phagosome begins digestion

#### 5: Killing
pathogen is digested by enzymes/chemicals
leaving only residual body of indigestible material
