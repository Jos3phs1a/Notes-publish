destroy virus-infected cells and cancer cells by releasing chemicals that directly lyse the membranes on first exposure

## mechanism
### 1: Recognition
NK cells detect antigens on abnormal cells (not as selective compared to other cells)
upon recognition, adhere to target cell

### 2: Realignment of Golgi apparatus
Golgi apparatus moves around nucleus to face target call
produce flood of vesicles containing proteins perforins
move towards target cell

### 3: Secretion perforin
Perforins released, diffuse to target cell

### 4: Lysis of abnormal cell
perforin molecules interact with membrane creating network of pores
cause overflow and rupture of cell
