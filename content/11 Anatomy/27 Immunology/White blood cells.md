# Types of leukocytes

### Neutrophils
- Most abundant in blood: ==~70%==
- size: ~12µm
- life span: ==1 day==
- ==first to arrive== during inflammation
- engulf and destroy unwanted materials e.g. bacteria via [[phagocytosis]]
- secrete toxic chemicals $H_2O_2$, $O_2^-$, Hypochloride ions 
### Lymphocytes
- Arise from [[lymphatic System]]
- In blood: ==20-35%==
- life span: ==months to decades== (related to vaccination)

#### B cells
antibody production ([[Antibody-mediated immunity]]) ^aea0ff
	- Derived and differentiated in bone marrow
	
#### T cells
directly destroy virus and mutant cells through chemicals ([[cell-mediated immunity]])
	- Derived from bone marrow but mature within [[Thymus]] 
		a) **Helper T cells (CD4+)**: regulate activity of immune cells (most abundant)
		b) **Cytotoxic T cells (CD8+):** kill virus infected or cancer cells^4c930d
		c) **Regulatory T cells:**   subset of helper cells, regulate immune response, prevent self attack after threat removed ^fc4a1b

#### NK cells
destroy virus infected or cancer cells via [[Immunological surveillance]]

#### Macrophages/Monocytes
- In blood: ==~5-8%==
- size: >10µm
- life span: ==7-10 days==
- Large, active mononuclear phagocytes, contain chemicals for [[phagocytosis]]
- Monocytes = immature macrophages: after produced leave blood vessels →  ==migrate into tissues, become mature==
- Differentiate into Kupffer cells & alveolar macrophages
#### Eosinophils
- In blood: 1-4%, 
- size: ~12µm, bi-lobed nucleus
- life span ~ 10 days
- contain proteases and collagenases
- secrete chemicals and destroy ==parasitic worms ==
