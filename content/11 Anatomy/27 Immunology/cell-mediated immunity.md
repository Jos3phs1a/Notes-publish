Good for viruses that hide within cells and [[Antibody-mediated immunity|antibodies]] and [[The Complement system]] cannot reach

Prerequisites:
- T cells must be in direct contact of target cells to eliminate them
- T cells only respond to antigens bound to glycoproteins on cell membranes, cells show the antigens via **antigen-presenting cells(APCs)**
- In order for antigens to be presented, [[Major Histocompatibility Complex(MHC)]] must be formed

#### TLDR analogy
- **MHC I**: A cell waves a **"Help, I’m infected!"** flag → **CD8⁺ T cell** comes in and eliminates it.
    
- **MHC II**: An APC waves a **"Here’s what the enemy looks like!"** flag → **CD4⁺ T cell** coordinates the immune army.
# Mechanisms
## Class 1 MHC


--- start-multi-column: ID_ov0u
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

1) virus enters cell
2) Proteins of virus broken down by cell into peptides and sent to endoplasmic reticulum(ER)
3) protease bind to MHC in ER, forming Antigen-Class 1 MHC complex
4) ER transports complex to cell surface via vesicles
5) Complex detected by [[White blood cells#^fc4a1b|CD8 cytotoxic T cells]] and target cell is eliminated

## Class 2 MHC


--- start-multi-column: ID_95lw
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: Right
```

1) Virus engulfed by APC
2) [[lysosomes]] within APC expose peptides of virus
3) ER produce Class 2 MHC protein
4) Antigen fragments bind to MHC forming complex
5) transported to cell surface 
6) Complex detected by [[White blood cells#^fc4a1b|CD4 Helper T cells]]

### After binding
- CD4 + second reaction on cell surface will be activated
- release cytokines activate other immune cells e.g. CD8 T cells
- CD8 activated will recognize any infected cell displaying antigen via [[cell-mediated immunity#Mechanism of Class 1 MHC|MHC class 1 mechanism]] and attack ==(will not attack APC)==
