Facilitated by 

- [[White blood cells#B cells|B cells]] via [[Antibody-mediated immunity]] 
- [[White blood cells#T cells|T cells]] via [[cell-mediated immunity]]

## Features
### Specific
- T and B cells only respond to 1 specific antigen
### Diverse
- body produces many types of T and B lymphocytes
- each recognize different antigen to combat specific pathogen
*no T/B cell can recognize multiple pathogens*
### Memory
- can memorize previously encountered pathogen
- responds quickly and effectively in the future
	(mechanism of vaccinations)
- can stay circulated for many years to provide immunity
