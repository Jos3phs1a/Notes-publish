3 membrane layers that cover & protect the CNS
Mnemonic: DAP

3 layers:
### Dura mater
- Brain: tightly adhered to the inner surface of the skull, no *real space* but *potential epidural space* if there is [[03 HSC/22 Head & Neck/Clinical Implications#^9a71a1|epidural hematoma]]
- Spine: not fused with vertebrae, exists *real* epidural space 
### Arachnoid mater
- In healthy person, Arachnoid mater and dura mater are in contact, exists *potential space* 
### Pia mater 
- directly in contact with brain gyri(ridges) & sulcus(grooves)
- directly in contact with spinal cord
- contains blood supply for CNS

### Epidural Space
- Real Space between vertebrae and [[Meninges#Dura mater|Dura]]
- contains ==areolar tissue, blood vessels, and protective padding of adipose tissue==
### Subdural Space
- **Potential Space** [[Meninges#Dura mater|Dura]] & [[Meninges#Arachnoid mater|Arachnoid]] Maters

### Subarachnoid Space
- Real Space between between [[Meninges#Arachnoid mater|Arachnoid]] & [[Meninges#Pia mater|Pia]] maters 
- contains [[Cerebral Spinal Fluid]]
