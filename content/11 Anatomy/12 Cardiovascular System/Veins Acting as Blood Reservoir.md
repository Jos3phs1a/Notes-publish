
### Features
Blood flow slow to heart
Low Resistance, elastic smooth muscle
Large capacity:
	Distensibility of walls and external pressure

### Factors affecting [[Control of Cardiac Output|venous return]]
**Gravity:**
	Vessels below heart subjected to gravity
	More force required to pump to heart
	Blood pools toward lower body
	Veins under hydrostatic pressure - capacity ↑, VR↓, cardiac output ↓, leads to fainting

**Valves:**
	Prevent ==backflow of blood==, maintains venous return^7c7085


**Respiratory Activity:**
	Chest cavity low pressure
	Creates pressure gradient for lower veins(LP) and chest veins(HP) 

**Cardiac Suction:**
	Ventricular contraction:
	AV valves closed + Atrial cavity enlarged - blood sucked in
	Ventricular Relaxation:
	AV valves open, enlarge ventricles - blood sucked in
