## Pericardial Sac
**Function:**
1. acts as shock absorber
2. Holds the heart in place
3. Prevent overfilling of the heart and excess dilation

**Components:**
- Pain sensitive
- Both layers innvervated by phrenic nerve

Fibrous pericardium
- Not considered the start of the heart muscles, just a protective layer
- Tough outermost layer of pericardium, made of connective tissue prevents heart from expanding too much.
- Flexible, but tends to not stretch

Serous Pericardium:
Produces pericardial fluid that lubricates heart as it beats
2 layers: ^0edada
1. Parietal layer
	-  Directly connected to fibrous pericardium
2. Visceral Layer (epicardium)
	- Start of the heart muscles layers
3. Pericardial Cavity:
	- Space between the 2 layers: holding pericardial fluid
4. Layers are lined with Mesothelium(simple squamous epithelium)

## Cardiac muscle + histology
**Muscles of the heart:**
Order of layers: **parietal** pericardium -> **visceral** pericardium(epicardium) -> myocardium -> endocardium
Myocardium: muscular walls forming chambers of the heart (does most of the pumping)
**Endo**cardium: covers ==inner surfaces== of heart, including heart valves, lined with ==simple squamous endothelium==
	types of endocardium in heart:
	Pectinate muscles(right atrium)
	Papillary muscles(right + left ventricles)
	Moderator band(right ventricle)

**Histology:**

Features allowing heart to contract in unison:

- Striations
- Branch patterns of muscles connected to one another
- Intercalated disc:
- Connect cardiac muscle cell together, electromechanical coupling allowing cells to contract in unison

## Landmarks of heart

### Outer layer:

**Sulcus:** Deep groove marking border between atria and ventricles
	Coronary sulcus(atrioventricular), Anterior + Posterior interventricular sulcus

**Atriums:**
	Auricles==(ear flap)==: muscular flap at edge of atrium
	Left + right atrium auricles

### Inner layer:
**Common**
	Interatrial septum: muscle layer separating right and left atria
	Interventricular septum: muscle layer separating right and light ventricles

**Atria:**
	Fossa ovalis(oval Fossa):
		- Separates left and right atrium, used during ==fetal development==, ==useless in adults==

**Right atrium only:**
	Crista terminalis:
	- ==Origin of pectinate muscles==, separate inner and outer walls

**Musculi pectinati** (pectinate muscles):
	Rough muscular ridges on walls of atrium
	Function:
	- create more ==forceful contractions== to pump low pressure blood to right vent.

![[Pasted image 20250201222511.png|center | 450]]

**Ventricles:**
	**Common:**
	Interventricular septum:
		- layer of myocardium
	**Function:**
		- house the AV bundle, separates right and left chambers
		Trabeculae carneae + Papillary muscles + Chordae tendineae:
		- during contraction, tense up, shorten, shuts AV valves, prevent backflow of flood
	
**Right ventricle only:**
		Moderator band:
			- musuclar ridge extending horizontally form interventricular septum, connects to anterior papillary muscles
			- Delivers stimulus for contraction to papillary muscles
**Cardiac Valves:**
	**4 valves**:
		2 atrioventricular:
			Bicuspid(mitral): Left chamber
			Tricuspid: Right chamber
		2 Semilunar:
			Aortic Valve: Left ventricle -> Aortic Arch
			Pulmonary Valve: Right ventricle -> Pulmonary artery


**Cardiac skeleton:**
- four dense bands of ==tough elastic tissue== surrounding heart valves,
- ==stabilise== heart valves and ventricular muscles
- acts as ==insulator between atrium and ventricle==, prevent mix signals and only way to receive signal is through AV bundle

## cardiac conduction system

- **Sinoatrial Node:**
	Location: Posterior wall of ==right atrium==, close to vena cava
	Function: Contains pacemaker cells, ==60-100 action potentials per min==, main generator of electric signals

- **Atrioventricular node**
	Location: near ==coronary sinus ==on interatrial septum
	Function: Backup generator ==40-60 action potentials per min==, can function if SA node shuts down
	Allows Atria to communicate with ventricles, holds signals sent by SA node for short time, allow all blood to flow properly

- **Bundle of His(AV bundle)**
	Location: ==interventricular septum==
	Function: conduct signals to ventricles(apex)

- **Purkinje Fibres**
	Location: fibres spread ==throughout surface of ventricle walls==
	Function: ensure synchronous action of contractile cells through intercalated discs
