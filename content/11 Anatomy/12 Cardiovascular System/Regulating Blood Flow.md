## Mechanism
Vasodilation: Passage of vessel expands, increase blood flow
Vasoconstriction: Passage constricts, reduce blood flow

E.g. during Exercise

### [[Anatomy of blood vessels#^31dfd4|Capillaries]]
Many capillaries not open under normal conditions
no smooth muscles → Blood flow regulated by ==pre-capillary sphincters (gates)==
During increased metabolic activity, ==sphincters open, increased blood flow to active tissues==

### Arterioles
2 areas to consider blood flow:
	==Active tissue== being targeted labelled **Local**
	==Non active== labelled: **Extrinsic**

But both areas can dilate/constrict depending on situation → ARTERIOLAR RADIUS

#### Extrinsic control (rest of body):
==All arterioles have a supply of sympathetic nerves== (==except brain== as blood supply must not be altered)
Influenced by:
1. [[02b Autonomic nervous systems#Sympathetic|Sympathetic]] activity ==(little influence by [[02b Autonomic nervous systems#Parasympathetic|parasympathetic]]  except penis + clit==)
	Inactive body parts: less blood needed → arterioles generalised vasoconstriction
	Most Useful : more blood needed → arterioles locally vasodilate
2. Hormones:
	1. Norepinephrine + Epinephrine cause ==generalised vasoconstriction==(NE stronger) + ==increase rate and strength of heart contraction==
	2. Epinephrine adds to ==local vasodilation of skeletal muscles + heart==

3. [[Reconditioning Blood|Potent vasoconstrictors agents]]:
	1. Vasopressin →  water balance
	2. Angiotensin II regulates salt balance


### Veins
Influenced by Sympathetic activity:
Undergo vasoconstriction: Increase Venous returns by [[Veins Acting as Blood Reservoir#Factors affecting Control of Cardiac Output venous return|↓venous capacity & ↑venous pressure]] ==→ more blood return to heart → ↑ EDV → ↑SV
