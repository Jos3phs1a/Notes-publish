## Measurement Metrics
**Mean arterial pressure:** avg pressure driving blood forward in mm Hg
					$$P_{Diastolic}  + \frac{1}3 P_{Pulse}$$
													 OR  
					$$\frac{2}{3} P_{Diastolic}  + \frac{1}{3}P_{Systolic} $$ ^4ad161

**Systolic pressure:** ==maximum pressure== when blood ejected to arteries ^90fc31

**Diastolic Pressure:** ==Minimum pressure== when blood drain into vessel during diastole ^ddc4da

**Pulse pressure:** ==difference== between ==systolic and diastolic== pressure

$$PulsePressure=P_{Systolic}  - P_{Diastolic}$$


How to measure Sys P and Dias P:

1. Cuff Pressure prevents blood flow through vessel
	1. no sound heard
2. cuff pressure decreases to normal range
	1. Turbulent blood flow → first sound heard (SYS)
3. Cuff pressure decreases less than 80mm Hg,
	1. Blood flow becomes smoother → Last sound right before nth heard (DIAS)

## Reacting to Change in Blood Pressure
System involved: Barorecptors

Stimuli: Drop in Blood Pressure
Sensing: Baroreceptors detect change in blood pressure
Increase input
	Action: ↑S ↓P
	Effect, ↓[[Control of Cardiac Output#^ba240b|HR]] & SR
	Arteriole vasoconstriction

affected by [[Control of Cardiac Output|cardiac output]] & [[Total peripheral resistance]] ( ↑ resistance = ↑ BP)
