## Short-term regulation
- Amount of food consumed
- Appetite signals - hunger/satiation

### Hormones
**Ghrelin** → appetite stimulator
**PYY3-36** → inhibits appetite stimulating NPY secreting neurons
**Cholecystokinin** → released by [[Small Intestine#Duodenum|Duodenum]] activates satiety center in brainstem

### Physical stimuli
Stomach distension → activates satiety center in brainstem

## Long Term regulation
### Hypothalamus
Contains Arcuate Nucleus → contributes to both long term energy balance and short term food intake
produces 2 substances:
**Neuropeptide Y(NPY)**: appetite stimulator
**Melanocortin**: group hormones contributing to homeostasis ^d71656

### Leptin
Appetite hormone, indicates amount of triglyceride fat stored in adipose tissue
Suppresses appetite, ↓ food consumption, ↑ weight loss
Acts on [[food intake#^d71656|arcuate nucleus]] causing ==↓NPY ↑melanocortin secretion==
