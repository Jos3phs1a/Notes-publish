[[Body Temperature]] regulated by hypothalamus via **negative feedback loops**

Receptors for skin temp and core temp respectively trigger hypothalamus to activate body response to increase/decrease body temp

![[Excalidraw/Metabolism#^group=uQVRZ6rMVlykufqd1CwkI|Temperature Regulation|center|500]]


## Receptors

### Peripheral
Receptors located on the skin
10x more receptors that detect cold than warm temperatures
### Central
Receptors within body
More receptors detecting cold than warmth
found in 
- hypothalamus
- spinal cord
- abdominal viscera
- great veins in upper abdomen and thorax


## Responses
### Heat production
#### Shivering
- Primarily involuntary 
- rhythmic, oscillating skeletal muscle contractions at rapid rate of 10-20 per second

#### Thermogenesis by brown fat
- primary heat pad of body 
- brown adipose tissue stimulated converting chemical energy from food into heat ^09d13c
### Heat Loss
#### Blood flow regulation
- blood vessels in the dermis layer of skin undergo [[Regulating Blood Flow|vasocontraction/vasoconstriction]] to limit heat transfer

### Coordinated Adjustments
Different parts of the hypothalamus react to different stimulus
Posterior → cold → wants to increase body temp
Anterior → Hot → wants to decrease body temp
These responses are affected by [[Hypothalamic temperature set point]]
