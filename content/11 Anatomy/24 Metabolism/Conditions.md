## Fever
- form of defense to destroy foreign particles in body
- can be dangerous as it can damage body functions as enzymes/hormones/cells cannot function optimally due to high temp

### mechanism
- infection detected → macrophages release [[endogenous pyrogen]]
- endogenous pyrogen release prostaglandins → raise [[Hypothalamic temperature set point]]
- initiate cold response → heat production → ↑body temp

### Process
1. Hypothalamic set point ↑ 
2. Cold response - vasoconstriction + shivering
3. body temp ↑ 
4. fever stops
5. hypothalamic set point ↓
6. vasodilation + sweating 
7. body temp ↓

## Hyperthermia
- Elevated body temp above accepted range
- ==set point unchanged== unlike fever
### Exercise induced
- can reach peak of 40°C as long as rate of heat loss = rate of heat gain

### Heatstroke
- Beyond 40.5°C 
- Thermoregulation overwhelmed, body becomes impaired
- lack of compensatory heat loss measures
- damage to organs

#### Acclimatization
body can built tolerance to heat via conditioning
- ↑rate of sweating
- ↑plasma vol
- ↓loss of salts + urine
- ↑aldosterone
## Hypothermia

- lower core temp without reset of set point
- Temp regulation impaired below 35°C
- body cannot build tolerance as easily with conditioning unlike for Hyperthermia
