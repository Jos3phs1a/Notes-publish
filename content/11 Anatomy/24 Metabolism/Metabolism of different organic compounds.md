## Functions
- Structural maintenance & repairs
- support growth
- produce secretions
- store [[nutrient reserves]]

## Carbohydrates
- Main resource used to generate ATP and other high-energy compounds
- 1 glucose → 30-32 ATP through oxidative phosphorylation

### Related of process
#### Gluconeogenesis
synthesis from noncarbohydrate precursors e.g. lactic acid, glycerol, amino acids
stores glucose as glycogen in liver and skeletal muscles
#### Glycogenesis
formation of glycogen from glucose
slow process
#### Glycogenolysis
breakdown of glycogen into glucose
quick process

#### Glycolysis
conversion of glucose to pyruvate acid for ATP production


## Lipids
- Not soluble in water
- circulate through bloodstream as [[lipoproteins]]
	- free fatty acids small % of total circulating lipids, bound to [[Blood Composition#^4f10bd|albumins]] 
- efficient source of energy
	- cells can gain 120 ATP from breakdown 1 one fatty acid molecule
	- fatty acids breakdown yields 1.3x more energy than glucose

### Lipolysis
[[Nutrient Classes#^77798d|triglycerides]] = 1 glycerol + 3 fatty acids
[[catabolism & anabolism#Catabolism|catabolism]] of lipids to convert glycerol to pyruvate
fatty acids converted to acetyl-CoA in beta oxidation

### Lipogenesis
Synthesis of lipids
- fatty acids - cannot be produced, must be consumed
- Glycerol - synthesized from dihydroxyacetone phosphate, intermediate product of glycolysis
- Nonessential fatty acids & steroids - synthesized acetyl-CoA 


## Proteins
- cellular proteins can be recycled in cytosol to form new proteins to repair body
- when glucose & lipid reserves are inadequate, liver:
	- breaks down internal proteins
	- absorbs amino acids from blood for energy\

### last resort in metabolism
- difficult to break apart than complex carbohydrates & lipids
- by product, ammonium ion, is ==toxic to cells==
- are most crucial for structural and functional components of cells

### Acquisition of protein
body can produce 11 non-essential amino acids on demand 
9 essential amino acids cannot be made at all or to sufficient quantity, must take in from diet

## Vitamins
- function as coenzyme for enzymatic reactions
- deficiency cause metabolic deficits
- assigned to either of 2 groups

### Fat-soluble 
- Vitamins A, D, E ,K 
- body contains significant reserves of these vitamins
- normal metabolism can continue several months without dietary sources

| Vitamins | Functions                                                                                  | Sources                                     |
| :------: | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------- |
|  **A**   | structural component of visual pigment retinal                                             | leafy greens, yellow vegetables             |
|  **D**   | converted to calcitriol, increase rate of intestinal calcium & phosphorus absorption       | synthesized when skin exposed to sunlight   |
|  **E**   | stabilizes intracellular membranes                                                         | meat, milk, vegetables                      |
|  **K**   | helps synthesize several proteins, including [[Hemostasis#^f20729\|four clotting factors]] | vegetables; produced by intestinal bacteria |

### Water soluble
- components of coenzymes
- rapidly exchanged between fluids in GIT and blood
- excess excreted in urine
- Relatively less storage compared to fat-soluble vitamins
