- Endogenous pyrogens are ==proteins that are released by the body to trigger an immune response==. 
- part of the innate immune system and are also known as cytokines.


- Produced by immune cells like neutrophils, macrophages, and lymphocytes
- Produced by endothelial cells, astrocytes, and glial cells
- Produced in response to exposure to exogenous pyrogens

Examples of endogenous pyrogens: 

- Interleukin (IL)-1, IL-6, IL-12
- Tumor necrosis factor (TNF)-α
- Interferon gamma (INF-γ)
- Ciliary neurotropic factor (CNTF)
