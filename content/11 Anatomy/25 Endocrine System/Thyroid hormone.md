## Functions:
- involved in glycolysis and ATP production
- calorigenic effect:
	- increase in the body's metabolic rate and heat production
	- strong, immediate, short-lived cellular metabolism
- Synthesis of new proteins related to :
	- other systems
	- growth - stimulate [[Growth Hormone]] secretion 
	- CNS development by increasing [[Processes affected by ANS action|S Activity]] 
	- ↑cardiovascular activity
	- ↑metabolism

## Synthesis, storage, secretion

--- start-multi-column: ID_hfbr
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: Right
```

1. Tyrosine-containing [[Thyroglobulin]] produced within **Thyroid follicular cell**
2. Iodide from dietary intake absorbed via [[Absorption Mechanism#Secondary Active|secondary active transport]] from blood into [[colloid]] by symporters
3. In follicular cell, iodide oxidized by TPO
4. Active iodide enters colloid
5. Iodide reacts with tyrosine to form MIT
6. 2 x MIT → DIT
7. DIT + MIT → T3
8. 2 x DIT → T4
9. Thyroid follicular cells engulf Tg-containing colloid by phagocytosis
10. Iodinated products split from Tg via lysosomes
11. T3 & T4 diffuse into blood via secretion
12. MIT & DIT deiodinated, iodide recycled

## Effects

--- start-multi-column: ID_agjd
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```


- ↑ metabolism in almost all cells in body
- excess quantity of hormone can occasionally increase basal metabolic rate by 60% to 100% above normal
- when no thyroid hormone is produced, [[Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)]] ↓ to half of normal
