## calcitonin
- ↓ plasma concentration of Ca$^{2+}$  
- produced in thyroid gland
- ==lowers blood phosphate levels by inhibiting the release of phosphate from bones==, → more phosphate taken up into bone tissue
## Parathyroid Hormone (PTH)
- ↑ plasma concentration of Ca$^{2+}$  
- produced in parathyroid gland
- can also ==promote absorption of Ca$^{2+}$ and PO$_4^{3-}$==


## Vitamin D (Calcitrol)
- Hormone ==increasing Ca$^{2+}$ and PO$_4^{3-}$ absorption in the intestines==

## Osteoblasts
↓ plasma concentration of Ca$^{2+}$  
form new bones and add growth to existing bone tissue
Found in fibrous periosteum layer of bones
Osteoblasts and its precursors produce RANKL and Osteoprotegerin (OPG) that regulate Osteoclast development
## Osteoclasts
↑ plasma concentration of Ca$^{2+}$  
dissolve old and damaged bone tissue so it can be replaced with new
Found in in pits in the bone surface which are called resorption bays, or Howship's lacunae
