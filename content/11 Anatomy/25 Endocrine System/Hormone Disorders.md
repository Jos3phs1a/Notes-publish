## Adrenal Gland
### Hyperaldosteronism
aka **Conn's syndrome**
↑ Aldosterone secretion
cause:
- **Increased sodium reabsorption** → Raises blood pressure.
- **Increased potassium excretion** in the kidneys → Causes **hypokalemia*****

### Hypoaldosteronism
**deficient aldosterone secretion**, leading to:

1. **Reduced sodium reabsorption** → Lower blood pressure.
2. **Reduced potassium excretion** → **Hyperkalemia**
### Cortisol hypersecretion
aka **Cushing Disease**
↑ Cortisol secretion due to tumor in anterior pituitary gland
### Adrenal androgen hypersecretion
aka Polycystic ovary syndrome(PCOS)

### Adrenocortical insufficiency
aka Addison's disease
**adrenal cortex dysfunction**


## Endocrine Pancreas

### Hyperglycemia
Abnormally high glucose levels in blood
### Diabetes mellitus
High glucose concentrations overwhelm reabsorption of kidneys
Glucose appear in urine
Polyuria → excess urine
Type 1 → destruction of pancreatic β-cells leads to little or no insulin production
Type 2 → Insulin resistance leads to decreased effectiveness of insulin, often combined with reduced insulin secretion over time
### Hypoglycemia
Body lacks sufficient glucose to function properly
Symptoms:
hunger, tremors, sweating, rapid heartbeat, anxiety, dizziness, headache, and irritability
