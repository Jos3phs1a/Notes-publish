## Function
**Maintain homeostasis** and regulate long-term processes e.g. growth metabolism, development, reproduction

## Endocrine vs Exocrine
**Endocrine:** use of ==ductless glands== secreting messengers through ===bloodstream to act on target cells===
**Exocrine:** substances are ==secreted directly== into the organ ==through ducts == 

Trend: When **Duration↑** (how long function is exerted),  **Speed↓** (how fast effect produced)

## Mechanisms of communication

|   Mechanism   | Transmission                | Chemical Mediators                               | Distribution of effects                                                                                                                   |
| :-----------: | --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
|  **Direct**   | Through gap junction        | ions<br>small solutes<br>lipid-soluble materials | limited to adjacent cells of same type interconnected by connexons                                                                        |
| **Autocrine** | Through extracellular fluid | Autocrines                                       | limited to cell that secretes hormone<br>typically affects own cell regulated by [[Control of hormone secretion\|negative feedback]] loop |
| **Paracrine** | ^                           | Paracrines                                       | limited to local area (short distance), <br>target cells must have appropriate receptors                                                  |
| **Endocrine** | Through Bloodstream         | Hormones                                         | target cells mostly in other tissues and organs (long distance)<br>must have appropriate receptors                                        |
| **Synaptic**  | Across Snyapses             | Neurotransmitters                                | Limited to very specific area<br>must have appropriate receptors                                                                          |
