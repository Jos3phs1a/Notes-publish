## Regulation Mechanisms

### Local controls to match air/blood flow

**Bronchioles** & **Arterioles** to force blood and air to needed regions to balance gas exchange

#### Closer to Apex
O₂ rich + blood poor

Arterioles:
dilate → ↓resistance → enhance blood flow → increase efficient gas exchange
Bronchioles:
constrict → ↑resistance → reduce air flow → more air goes to base

#### Closer to Base
O₂ poor + blood rich

Arterioles:
constrict → ↑ resistance → reduce blood flow → send more blood to apex
Bronchioles:
dilate → ↓resistance → enhance air flow → receive more air from apex

### Pulmonary arteries mechanism to match ventilation & perfusion

works opposite to **systemic arteries.**
**systemic** arteries take in CO₂ from muscles and release O₂ into them. VS **pulmonary** arteries take in O₂ from alveoli and release CO₂ to alveoli
when PO₂ ↑(excess) or PCO₂↓(lacking)→ **systemic arteries Constrict** VS **pulmonary arteries** **dilate** vice versa
Normal exchange rate
asthma, bronchiole constricted, mismatch in flow → gas exchange not good
other pulmonary arteries dilate, sending more blood over to clear passage, while obstructed pulmonary arteries constrict → normal blood flow restored
