## Important landmarks:
- **~98% saturated:** Arteriole blood in lungs → O$_2$ restocked in blood → PO$_2$ highest at 104mmHg
- **~75% saturated:** Normal Venous Blood after passing through tissue → PO$_2$ at 40mm Hg = average venous blood levels
Gap between lungs and tissue → % of O$_2$ provided to tissue ~23% aka dissociation of O$_2$

## Shifts in Dissociation Curve:
### Shifting Right:
- For the same partial pressure of O$_2$, more oxygen will be discharged into body, saturation will be lower

### Factors affecting curve shifts:
- **pH**: Lower pH → CO$_2$ acidic, body needs to clear → more O$_2$ required in tissue → Curve Shift right
- **Temperature**: → Higher Temperature → Tissue burning more energy → more O$_2$ required → Curve shift right
- [[2,3-dyphosphoglycerate]]: Additional 2,3-DPG → shift right
