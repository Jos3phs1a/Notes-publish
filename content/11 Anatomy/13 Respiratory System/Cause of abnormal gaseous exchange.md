**partial pressure of gases in alveoli and blood**

Diffusing from HP to LP

Higher altitudes → Less P**O**₂

**Diffusion barrier thickness**

alveolar - capillary thickness 0.5μm

if thickens → slower diffusion e.g. fibrosis,edema

**surface area for diffusion**

larger area in contact with alveolus and capillaries → ↑diffusion

less alveolar volume/no. functional alveoli due to pneumonia → less SA

**Properties of gases involved**

gases more soluble in blood diffuse faster **CO**₂ > **O**₂

**Rate of blood flow**

higher blood flow → more gases carried in and out of body

normal alveoli can equilibrate gas within 0.25s so exercise does not decrease gas exchange efficiency

**abnormal:**

blood clots in pulmonary arteries e.g. embolism

higher blood flow reduces time for gas to equilibrate e.g. exercise

thickened membranes of capillaries
