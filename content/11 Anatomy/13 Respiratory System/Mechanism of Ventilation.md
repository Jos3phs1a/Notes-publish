## Relevant terms & factors
**Terms:**

- **(intra)alveolar aka intrapulmonary pressure aka:** pressure in alveoli or lung roughly equal (can be positive or negative)

- **intrapleural aka intrathoracic pressure**: pressure in pleural cavity always negative about -4mmHg of Patm (always negative)
 
- **Transmural pressure:** Difference between: alveolar pressure - pleural pressure (always positive)

- **Atmospheric pressure:** 760mmHg

### Negative intrapleural pressure

1) Elasticity of lungs: Lungs naturally collapse(recoil)
2) Elasticity of Chest wall: Chest wall naturally expands
3) Surface tension: Created between Parietal and Visceral [[16 pleura|pleura]] to keep pleura together
4) When the chest and lungs move away from each other, negative pressure created

## Mechanism
### 1. Initiation 
[[18 Diaphragm|Diaphragm]] contracts + [[19 Surrounding structures#Muscular|external intercostal muscles]] contract, chest wall expand → [[#Negative intrapleural pressure]] → air travels from HP to LP

### 2. Mid inspiration
Alveolar expand to max → most negative pressure → allow most amount of air to flow in

### 3. End inspiration

Thoracic volume highest → According to Boyle Law, Intrapleural Pressure is also at lowest

Alveolar pressure returns to zero → atmospheric air stays in alveolus no air flowing in and out of lungs → allow for diffusion into bloodstream
