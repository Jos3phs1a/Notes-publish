## Linear Kinetics
___
Guiding question: 
	What is kinetics?
	What laws dictate  kinetics?
	What are the scenarios related?

Definition: study of forces associated with motion.

Newtons 3 laws of motion:
- 1st law: An object in motion, stays in constant motion until external force causes a change
- 2nd Law: Acceleration directly proportional to force applied and acts towards direction applied $F=ma$
- 3rd Law: Every force exerted on body, there is a reaction force equal in magnitude, opposite in direction

Application to Translational motion e.g. momentum, and Rotational motion e.g. torque

### Momentum & Impulse
___
***Momentum(P):*** product of mass and velocity of object

Unit: kgm/s  Equation: P=mv

==Law of conservation of momentum: total momentum of a given system remains constant in absence of external forces==
![[Pasted image 20250125174345.png]]
***Impulse(I)***: change in momentum, caused by force applied to a system over time

Unit: Ns  Equation: $I=F\Delta t$ or $\Delta M$
 
### Work, Energy, Power
___
Law of conservation of energy: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred from one form to another, totally energy in an isolated system is constant.

Unit: Joules(J)

Work: product of Force and displacement in the direction of force

Unit: Joules  Equation: $W=Force(F)\;*\;Displacement(s)$

Power: Work done over time
Unit: Watts(W) Equation: $P=\frac{{Work\;done}(W)}{time(t)}=\frac{Force x Displacement}{time(t)}=Force(F)\;*\;Velocity(v)$


## Angular Kinetics

### Overview
Revolves around rotational equilibrium state where 
- zero torque 
- angular momentum is constant (zero/non-zero)

### Torque

Definition: aka moment of force, product of force and moment arm
Moment arm = perpendicular distance from line of force to pivot
Unit: Nm  Equation: Force(F) x moment arm(d) ^cc8e90

**Mechanical Advantage:**
Ratio of length of force arm to length of resistance arm

$$MA = \frac{Length_{force\;arm}}{Length_{resistance\;arm}}$$
 3 classes of levers: 

1. 1st class: MA = 1
	a. Position to acquire equilibrium e.g. skull
