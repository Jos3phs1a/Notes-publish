#### Cartilaginous
- Bone ends united by cartilage
- Slightly moveable
- e.g. Synchondrosis (hyaline), Symphysis (fibrocartilage)

#### Synovial
- Bone ends covered with articular cartilage, enclosed by capsule with synovial membrane
- Freely mobile but dimension of movement depend on structure

### Functional Classification

#### Synarthrotic (immovable):
Formed by two tightly fit bones do not allow relative motion

#### Amphiarthrotic (slightly moveable):
Allow slight relative motion

#### Diarthrotic (freely moveable):
Varying degrees of motion

Depending on:
- Articular cavities
- Ligamentous capsules
- Synovial membranes and fluids
- Generally all synovial joints are diarthrodial

### Articular Cartilage

**Function**:
	Facilitate movement of articulating bones by reducing stress

By:
	-Reducing friction and wear in between joints
	-Distributing over larger contact area due to compliant nature
	
**During Movements**:
	Articular cartilage constantly experience:
	- Tension: cartilage respond by realigning collagen fibres to carry applied load
	- Shear: frictional forces due to articulating surfaces, normally low and insignificant unless during intense physical activity/ trauma
	- Compression
