### Passive insufficiency

Inability of biarticular muscle to lengthen at one joint if already lengthened at the other.

can generate tension force

e.g. hip flexion and knee extension lengthens hamstring muscles, if both performed together ROM will be significantly reduced

hamstring fully lengthened when only performing one movement(in this case knee extension)



both hip flexion and knee extension occur, hamstring cannot fully lengthen at either joint



similar case for hand, when wrist flexion, finger **extensors** are stretched, prevent fingers from closing grip as fully lengthened.

### Active insufficiency

The inability of biarticular muscle to maximally shorten or contract at one joint if already shortened on the other joint

effect: reduces force generation of the muscle

e.g. flexion of fingers is much harder when wrist is also in flexion
