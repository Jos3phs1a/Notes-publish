
### Analyzing Posture
Qns to ask:

- What are the main labels of postures?
- What are the root causes of the posture?
- What muscles become weak/tighter

Rules:

Muscles are affected by shifts in bone:
Long term stretching -> muscles become weaker
Long term shortening -> muscles become tighter
Generally 1 main cause: other parts are compensating

- Head: neutral
- Scapulae: flat against back, no abduction/adduction
- Cervical spine: Slight lordosis, convex anteriorly
- Thoracic Spine: Slight kyphosis, convex posteriorly
- Lumbar Spine: Slight lordosis, convex anteriorly
- Pelvis: Neutral, ASIS & PSIS level
- Hip joints: neutral, no flexion/extension

Must relate to position of femur

- Knee Joints: Neutral, no flexion/extension
- Ankle joints: Neutraml, no plantar/dorsiflexion

## Kyphotic-Lordotic


--- start-multi-column: ID_78ru
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

Cause:

Excess kyphosis of thoracic spine

Excess lordosis of cervical spine + lumbar spine

- Head: Forward
- Scapulae: Abducted (rounded shoulders)
- Cervical Spine: Excess lordosis -> hyperextension (convex more anterior)
- Thoracic Spine: Excess Kyphosis -> Hyperflexion
- Lumbar Spine: Excess Lordosis -> Hyperextension
- Pelvis: Anteriorly tilt - PSIS higher than ASIS
- Hip joints: In Flexion
- Knee joints: Slight hyperextended
- Ankle joints: Slight plantarflexed because of backward inclination of the leg

Muscles:

Weak:

- Ab muscles: A tilt -> muscles above (abs) stretched
- Hamstrings: A tilt -> muscles underneath stretched
- Glutes
- Mid + lower trapezius: due to abducted scapulae
- Neck flexors
- External obliques
- Thoracic paraspinals

Tight:

- Quads: A tilt -> muscle below shorten
- Low back muscles: hyperextension of Lumbar -> muscles bunched, shorter

## Lordotic


--- start-multi-column: ID_3mzh
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

Main Cause: Excess Lordosis of Lumbar spine

- Head: Neutral position
- Cervical spine: Normal Lordosis
- Thoracic spine: Normal kyphosis
- Lumbar spine: Lordosis (hyperextended)
- Pelvis: Anteriorly tilted
- Hip joints: Flexed
- Knee joints: Slight hyperextended
- Ankle joints: Slight plantarflexed

Muscles (similar to KL posture for lower body)

Weak:

- Abs
- Hamstrings
- Glutes
- Hip extensors

Tight:

- Quads
- Low back muscles
- Hip flexors
- Erector spinae

## Flat Back


--- start-multi-column: ID_ogq3
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

Main cause: Reduced kyphosis of Thoracic Spine (extension)

- Head: Neutral position
- Cervical spine: Normal curve (slight anterior)
- Thoracic spine: Loss kyphosis (slight posterior)
- Lumbar spine: Loss of Lordosis (hyperextended)
- Pelvis: Posterior tilted
- Hip joints: Flexed
- Knee joints: Slight hyperextended
- Ankle joints: Slight plantarflexed

Muscles:

Weak:

- Quads: P tilt -> rectus femoris stretched
- Hip flexors: hip extension -> flexors stretched

Tight:

- Hamstring: P tilt -> hamstring shortened
- Hip Extensors: hip extension-> extensors shortened

## Sway - back


--- start-multi-column: ID_siui
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

Main cause: posterior pelvic tilt affects lumbar + lower thoracic spine

- Head: Forward
- Cervical spine: hyper extension
- Thoracic spine: Increased flexion (long  
    kyphosis) with posterior displacement of the upper trunk
- Lumbar spine: Flexion → loss of lordosis
- Pelvis: Posteriorly tilted
- Hip joints: Hyperextended
- Knee joints: Hyperextended
- Ankle joints: Neutral

Weak:

- Quads:
- Lower abs:
- Hip flexors
- Erector Spinae(upper thoracic)

Tight:

- Hamstrings
- Glutes
- Upper abs
- Erector spinae(lower)
