## Function
- transmit forces from muscles to bones
- allow for efficient generation of tension for movement

## Structure
- type of [[enthesis organ]] 
- Composed mostly of type 1 collagen fibers, arranged in parallel
- [[Extracellular matrix]] comprise of proteoglycans, glycoproteins, water → enhance resilience and elasticity
- weak to ==shear and compression forces==

### Positional Tendons
Transmit forces to bones for movements at joints
for stability and adaptability
e.g. anterior tib tendon, digital extensor tendon

- relatively inextensible under load
- experience lower strains during activity


--- column-break ---

### Energy storage tendons
Store + release elastic strain energy to increase locomotion efficiency
for movement and efficiency in [[stretch shortening cycle]] 
e.g. Achilles tendon, superficial digital flexor tendons

- stretch and recoil under load
- high strains during activity

### blood supply
limited blood supply
exhibit inner hypovascular core (Hole), surrounded by perfused peripheral region (Donut).
