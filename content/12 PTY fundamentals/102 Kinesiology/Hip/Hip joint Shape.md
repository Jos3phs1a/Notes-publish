# Shape of femur

Shape of proximal femur affects how forces are distributed to the hip
affected by
- genetics/abnormal growth = [[02a IA pathologies of hip#Dysplasia|femoral dysplasia]] 
- trauma or systemic factors

## Angle of inclination
Definition: angle along frontal plane between femoral neck and medial side of femoral shaft
at birth: 165-170° → decreases every 2° every 2-8 years
avg adult: 125°
Coxa vara < 125°, Cova valga >125°

Abnormal angles affect articulation of hip joint → affect biomechanics e.g. ==alter leverage, hence force production== of muscles connecting pelvis to greater trochanter(eg glutes & abductors)

## Femoral Torsion
Definition: relative twist between femur shaft and neck
angle between back of shaft to middle of neck ==along transverse plane at superior view==
At birth: 40° anteversion
avg adult: 15° between 8 - 20°
Retroversion < (significant) 15°, [[Excessive anteversion]] >(significant) 15°

## Acetabular alignment
Typically measured using imaging technology
### Center-edge angle
angle to which acetabulum covers femoral head.
avg 25-35°
too little 
	- greater risk of dislocation
	- degeneration
too much 
	- impingement
	- injury during extreme motions

### Acetabular Anteversion angle
angle to which acetabulum faces anteriorly
avg: 20°
Too little/retroverted
	- Abnormal stress
	- degeneration
Too much
	- dislocation risk
