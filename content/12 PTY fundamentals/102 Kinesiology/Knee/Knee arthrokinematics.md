# Screw-home mechanism

- External rotation of tibia during knee extension
- cannot be performed independently unlike [[Knee Osteokinematics#Axial Rotation|Axial rotation]] 
- produces increased [[Joint Congruency]] and stability

### Driving factors
- shape of medial femoral condyle
- passive tension in [[Knee ligaments#^6e01d6|ACL]] 
- lateral pull of quads

**During Flexion**:
- popliteus muscles drive initial movement
	- femoral-on-tibial flexion → externally rotate femur 
	- tibial-on-femoral flexion → internally rotate tibia
