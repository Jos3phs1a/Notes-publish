### Axis of rotation

--- start-multi-column: ID_s4ll
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

Knee movements cause [[Evolute]], 
**Implications:**
- no definitive axis of rotation, average is at **lateral epicondyle of femur**
- affect moment arm and internal torque, explains maximal-effort internal torque varies across range
- has clinical implications as clinics require fixed axis as a metric
	- ROM measurement
	- isokinetic testing
	- hinged knee orthosis rehab

# Axial Rotation

tibia and femur able to rotate about each other
axis of rotation: around perpendicular to shaft of tibia but not exact

less rom during knee extension: 
more rom during knee flexion: 90° knee flexion can allow up to 45° rotation
external rotation range > internal rotation range ~ ==2:1 ratio==
