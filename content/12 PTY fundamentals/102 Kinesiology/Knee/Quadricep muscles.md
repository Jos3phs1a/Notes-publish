# Structure

4 Muscles
- rectus femoris (20% of extension torque)
- Vastus medialis
- Vastus lateralis
- Vastus intermedius 

unite to form quadriceps tendon, attach to [[Patella#Anterior Surface|Patella]] base and sides
Large PCSA and ACSA, 2.8x greater than hamstring, capable of 6000N of force

Muscle fibers run in different direction along the knee 

**Vastus lateralis:**
largest cross sectional area

**Vastus medialis:**
extends farther distally from knee 
2 fibers:
- Medialis longitudinal - 70% of cross sectional area
- Medialis Obliquus - 30% of cross sectional area but more distal angle of pull has significant impact on stability and [[Patellofemoral Joint#local factors|patella position]]
  
**Vastus intermedius:**
- poorly defined articularis genu
- pulls the capsule and synovial membrane proximally during active knee extension

# Torque generation

- Quads produce extensor torque about 2/3 greater than hamstrings
- Traditional ==isokinetic concentric H:Q torque about 0.5==
	- *During rehab* aim for ratio of ==1:1== in concentric strength
	- More commonly studied now: $$functional\;ratio=\frac{Eccentric\;Hamstring}{Concentric\;Quads}$$
- Isometric - dynamic stabilization
	- hamstrings also cocontract to stabilize Knee & [[Hip joint stability|Hip]]
- Eccentric - decelerate descent of body's mass and reduce joint loading (significant contribution to reducing ligament stress)
- Concentric - accelerate tibia or femur movements
- [[Patella]] aids in increasing internal moment arm
