# Osteokinematics

- able to produce low - moderate forces at high shortening/lengthening speeds 
- allow fast acceleration/deceleration of lower leg during activity
	e.g. rapidly contraction to flex knee during swing phase of walking, quick eccentric decelerate knee extension during late swing phase

- Able to compensate for weak or lax ligaments by acting as dynamic stabilizers

### MCL examples
e.g. pes anserinus muscle group work eccentrically to resist external rotation and valgus load on the knee

### ACL example
Hamstrings reduce tension on ACL in knee flexion
as knee flexes, hamstrings are parallel to ACL, can act eccentrically along same line of pull as ACL, reducing stress on the ligament
