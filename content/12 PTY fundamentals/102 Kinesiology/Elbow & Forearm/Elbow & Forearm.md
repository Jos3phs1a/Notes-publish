## Anatomy of Elbow Forearm complex
Three Bones + 4 Joints
	1. Humeroulnar Joint
	2. Humeroradial Joint
	3. Proximal radio-ulnar Joint
	4. Distal radio-ulnar Joint

Movements performed
	1.Flexion/Extension
	2.Supination/Pronation

##### **Natural Cubital Valgus**
Forearm is angled slightly away from the body at 13$\degree$
Purpose: Keep carried objects away from side of thigh during walking

**Effect of excess cubitus valgus**
structures ==placed under stretched==

### Elbow joint
#### Structure 

##### articular capsule
encases 3 joints:
	Humeroulnar
	Humeroradial
	proximal radio-ulnar

##### Ligaments
Capsule is  stronger with these ligaments:
1. Medial Collateral Ligament
	 Anterior + Posterior + Transverse
	 Different fibers that specialise in protecting a certain direction → more effective in stability, vs 1 fiber for all directions
	 
2. Lateral Collateral Ligament Complex
	a. Radial
	b. Ulnar
	
3. Annular Ligament + Quadrate ligaments



### Distal radio-ulnar joint

#### Structure
##### Triangular fibrocartilage complex
1. Triangular Fibrocartilage:
	Characteristic triangular shape, connects **ulnar head, lunate, triquetrum**
	act as stabilizer for both forearm and [[00 Hand & Wrist|wrist]]
2. Ulnar Collateral ligament
	Ulnar styloid to triquetrum
3. Palmer Ulnocarpal ligament
	Ulnar styloid to lunate, triquetrum
4. Palmar radio-ulnar joint ligament
	distal radius to distal ulnar
5. Fascial Sheath
	connective tissue

### Interosseous membrane
piece of connective tissue 
Structure:

3 fiber groups running in different direction:

| proximal oblique cord <br>central band<br>distal oblique fibers | ![[Pasted image 20250127232417.png]] |
| :-------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |

##### Role: 
1. reduce stress on radius/radio-carpal joint by connecting to ulna:
		a. through connective fibers between radius and ulna
		b. site for muscles attachment that generate/receive force 

##### Mechanism:
compression forces:
	80% directed through radiocarpal ↑ likelihood of fracture
	force transferred through fibers → equal distribution across radius and ulna

Distraction/Pull force:
	Force ==slackens membrane== → ↑demand on other tissues e.g. oblique cord & annular ligament
	Brachioradialis(dynamic stabilizer) contract to hold radius tight to capitulum
