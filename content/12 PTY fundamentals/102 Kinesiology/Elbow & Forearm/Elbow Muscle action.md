### Law of Parsimony
Definition: there exists a ==hierarchy of muscle recruitment== depending on the ==demand(e.g. load/speed) on the muscle==

body wants to spend least energy possible, smaller muscles recruited first(least energy) → demand↑, bigger muscle recruited

| Actions                                                      | Hierarchic Recruitment                                                                                                         |
| :----------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Elbow extension from gentle unresisted to highly resisted    | Anconeus → Medial head → Lateral head → Long head of triceps brachii (crosses 2 joints, results in extra movement of shoulder) |
| Elbow Flexion from gentle unresisted to highly resisted      | Brachialis → Biceps brachii + Brachioradialis                                                                                  |
| Forearm Supination from gentle unresisted to highly resisted | Supinator → Biceps brachii                                                                                                     |
### Elbow flexors
Recall principles of torque and lever arms,
Flexors produce highest force/torque at 90$\degree$ 

Forced generated is also affected by position of arm in shoulder flexion/extension
recall [[Muscle Tension Properties#4. Force-velocity relationship|Force-Velocity Relationship]], & assume constant tempo of action i.e. 2 sec eccentric, 2 sec concentric

| In shoulder flexed position                                                                                            | In shoulder extended position                                                                                                                          |
| :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Flexors placed in shorter position, less optimal length<br>Speed of contraction higher<br>Force ==produced lower==<br> | Flexors placed in longer position, [[Active Length Tension Curve\|more optimal length]]<br>Speed of contraction lower<br>Force ==produced Higher==<br> |


### Elbow Extensors

Torque influenced by both muscle length & leverage 

leverage:
At near full elbow extension, [[Kinetics#^cc8e90|moment arm]] is highest, presumed to generate most force

due to [[Active Length Tension Curve|length tension relationship]]:
measured Force produced at 20$\degree$ < Force produced at  90$\degree$ 

summary:
Other muscles: 
	at 90$\degree$ , ↑↑Torque = ↑ML + ↑MA 
Elbow Ext: 
	at 90$\degree$, ↑↑Torque = ↑ML + **↓MA**
	at 20$\degree$, ↓Torque = **↓ML** + ↑MA

suggesting muscle length has higher impact on torque generation 

### Supination/ Pronation
| Primary Supinator Muscles   | Secondary Supinator Muscles                                                              |
| :-------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Supinator<br>Biceps Brachii | Radial wrist extensors<br>Extensor policis longus<br>Extensor indicis<br>Brachioradialis |

| Primary Pronator Muscles             | Secondary pronator Muscles                                  |
| :----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Pronator Teres<br>Pronator Quadratus | Flexor Carpi Radialis<br>Palmaris Longus<br>Brachioradialis |

Brachioradialis sits can act on both due to its position

Pronator quadratus
- effective in producing torque, but does not produce high amount
- also stabilize distal radio-ulnar joint

Supinator muscle similar to Pronator quadratus and anconeus, effective torque generator, but lower quantity

#### Torque at different angles
Supination torque generated highest when elbow is at 90$\degree$ flexion

at 90$\degree$ Biceps Brachii produce more torque than Supinator
at 30$\degree$ Supinator contributes more than at 90$\degree$

Triceps will also co-contract synchronously to maintain 90$\degree$ flexion
- is very taxing
