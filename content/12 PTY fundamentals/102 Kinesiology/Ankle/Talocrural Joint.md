- Articulation of trochlea and side of talus with rectangle cavity created by distal tibia and both malleoli
- [[mortise]] shape creates major stability, allow force to pass between leg & foot 
	- 90-95% compressive forces pass through talus and tibia, 5-10% through lateral talus and fibula
- lined with ~3mm articular cartilage which compress to take loads

# Osteokinematics

- 1 primary degree of freedom
- axis of rotation between lateral and medial malleoli, but not in pure medial-lateral axis as lateral malleolus is slightly inferior and proximal to medial malleolus
- axis deviates ~10° in frontal plane, ~6° horizontal plane
	- dorsiflexion associated with slight abduction + eversion
	- plantarflexion associated with slight adduction + inversion


# Arthrokinematics

## Dorsiflexion
### NWB scenario
talus rolls forward, slides posteriorly according to [[Convex-Concave principle]]

| taut ligaments                                 | slack ligaments               |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| calcaneofibular ligament                       | anterior talofibular ligament |
| posterior talofibular ligament                 | anterior capsule              |
| posterior tibiotalar fiber of deltoid ligament |                               |
| achilles tendon                                |                               |

 In WB scenario, only difference is tibia initiate the movement roll slide anteriorly over talus, but end result of ligaments same
