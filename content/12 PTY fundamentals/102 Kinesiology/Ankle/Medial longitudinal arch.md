# Windlass effect

key component of push off during gait

tension of the plantar fascia translates to pushing the foot into normal arch

1) toe off: calf muscle contract → left heel/calcaneus
2) BW transfer to metatarsal heads → MTP extension
3) plantar fascia stretches → ↑ tension 
4) tension brings two ends of aponeurosis together, strengthens midfoot and forefoot, but the calcaneus–MTP distance **doesn’t significantly decrease**

analogy: **cable** attached between two fixed points. If you **rotate** one of those points (like MTP extension), cable winds and **tightens** — plantar fascia doesn’t get shorter, it gets **more tensioned**.

## when poor arch
- forefoot sinks under load
- reduced MTP extension
- limited stretch of plantar fascia → foot remains flat
- intrinsic muscles work harder
