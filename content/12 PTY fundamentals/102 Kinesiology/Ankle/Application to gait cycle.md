
# Early stance phase


As foot flat occurs, to right before push off, foot is placed into dorsiflexion
During this time, 
[[Talocrural Joint#Arthrokinematics|Talocrural Arthrokinematics]] contributes heavily in foot stability
increased stability caused by:
### Increasing width of talus at the anterior portion
**Wedging Effect** →  tibia ==slides forward relative== to talus during dorsiflexion, mortise ↑ surface in contact with trochlear surface of talus 
→ [[Joint Congruency]] at ==highest==
==Interosseous membrane & tibiofibular== ligaments prevent tibia & fibula from ==spreading apart==

![[Pasted image 20250330160302.png]]
### Passive tension of Ligaments
↑ Dorsiflexion → ↑ stretch of posterior ligaments → ↑ passive tension → ↑ stability 


# Early to midstance phase 

[[Subtalar Joint]] movements are very slight, only vary about max 10°
### First one-third 
[[Subtalar Joint]] pronated, allowing flexibility to of midfoot to mould onto terrain  

### Late Stance
or toe-off phase, foot arch rises sharply, [[Subtalar Joint]] supinated, adds rigidity to midfoot.

↑ Pronation → ↑ Internal rotation of lower limb → create valgus stress 

may be result of pathalogy 
- e.g. flatfoot, 
- weak supinators e.g. tib post

excessive pronation leads to:
- local inflammation & pain on medial foot as plantar pressure applied medially
- chain reaction affecting medial side of knee

## Supination

--- start-multi-column: ID_8wrl
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

occurs roughly with the beginning of swing phase of opposite leg, 
↑ Supination → ↑ External rotation of lower limb → ↑ rigidity

gastrocnemius and soleus use this ==stability to transfer forces== from the Achilles tendon,  metatarsal heads during the push off phase
of walking or running.

if relatively pronated, cause instability 

if excessive, more activity ==required to reinforce medial longitudinal arch==, causing pain and fatigue due to overuse
