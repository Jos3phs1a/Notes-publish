[[Flexibility#Stretching|Stretching]] is mainly muscle tightness/soft tissue, different from [[ROM Exercises]] which is about joint movement

| Contraindications   | - Bony block limits joint motion<br>- Recent fracture, incomplete bony union<br>- Evidence of acute inflammation/infection e.g. heat, swelling<br>- Necessary healing of restricted or adjacent tissues could be disrupted by stretching<br>- Sharp pain w joint movement<br>- Hematoma or tissue trauma<br>- Joint hypermobility → failure<br>- Shortened soft tissue provide necessary joint stability → when stretched joints become less stable<br>- Shortened soft tissue allow functional movement for patients with conditions otherwise not possible                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| Determinants        | - ==Alignment/Form==:<br><br>Position of limb or body so stretch force directed to appropriate soft tissue<br><br>- ==Stabilization==<br><br>Fixation of bony segment attached to muscle or tissue + prevent compensation<br><br>- ==Intensity==<br>Magnitude of stretch<br><br>- ==Duration==<br>Length of stretch<br><br>- ==Speed==<br>Rate of initial application of stretch<br><br>- ==Frequency==<br>Number of sessions<br><br>- ==Mode==<br>Form or manner which stretch force is applied or source of stretch force                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| Modes of stretching | Principles:<br>Low-load + long duration:<br>Safest form<br>affected by total elongation time<br>e.g. 3 x30s = 9x10s<br>Lower intensity = Higher Tolerance<br>Longer total duration = Yield longer lasting decrease in muscle-tendon stiffness<br>Slowly applied stretch less likely to increase tensile stress<br>Higher intensity = less frequency of stretching, allow for healing<br>Frequency minimum 2 times per week for healthy hypomobile patients, ↑frequent for patients w pathology<br><br>Types:<br><br>1. ==Long-Duration static==<br>5s~5mins<br>Low intensity, slowly apply, continuous, end-range stretch<br>Eg. 2x30s<br><br>2. ==Short-duration cyclical==<br>Repeated stretching, re-applied multiple reps per session<br>Low intensity, Low velocity<br>E.g. 4x15s<br><br>3. ==Short-duration ballistic==<br>Rapid, Forceful<br>High velocity, High intensity<br>Fast joint movement + quick elongation of tissue<br>Better for young, healthy, bad for elderly sedentary, contractures population<br><br>4. ==Short-duration dynamic==<br>Low velocity, low intensity, more warm up<br>Lubricate synovial joints etc |
