
## Properties

### Passive [[Flexibility#Range of Motion(Full)|ROM]] Exercises

|                                    |                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Indications/<br><br>Reasons to use | 1. Acute inflammatory conditions<br>Active ROM would negatively impact healing e.g. inflammation after injury/surgery<br><br>Aim to prevent hypomobility<br><br>2. Patient unable/not supposed to move segment<br><br>Dislocation, comatose, paralysis<br><br>3. After surgical repair and active motion compromise repair<br><br>Tendon transfer surgery, as not fully integrated into body ~ 6 weeks |
| Goals                              | - Maintain tissue mobility + mechanical elasticity<br>- Synovial movement and diffusion of materials in joint<br>- Reduce impact of contractures<br>- Improve circulation<br>- Assist healing<br>- Raise patient awareness of movement<br>- Assess limit of motion, joint stability, muscle flexibility<br>- Demonstrate active ROM<br>- Prior to stretching technique 'loosen' tissue                 |
| Limitations                        | Does not:<br><br>- prevent muscle atrophy<br>- Increase strength/endurance<br>- Improve circulation compared to active<br>- Patient may contribute -not purely passive                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |

### Active/Active-assisted

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Indications/Reasons to use|1. Always use as long as no contraindications(symptoms/conditions making treatment unsafe) against active ROM<br><br>Start ASAP if muscles are weak + safe to perform<br><br>1. Active ROM for adjacent joints of immobilized joint to ensure no formation of contracture/muscle atrophy<br><br>e.g. elbow fracture, mobilize shoulder + wrist<br><br>1. Promote physical activity + reverse sustain posture|
|Goals|- Maintain muscle elasticity + contractility<br>- Sensory feedback from muscles<br><br>Teach muscle to contract properly, reduce jerky/uncoordinated movement<br><br>- Provide stimulus for bone & joint integrity<br>- Increase circulation, prevent vein thrombus<br>- Develop coordination & motor skills|
|Limitations|Does not:<br><br>- Increase/maintain strength<br>- Skill or coordination unless movement pattern used|

### Precautions & contraindications

|            |                                                                                                                                                                               |
| ---------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Principles | Motion should be within pain-free range<br><br>Not resistance training → pain = warning<br><br>Monitor symptoms & signs<br>e.g. BP, heart rate, pain, discomfort, nausea etc. |
