# Stance Phase 0-60%
## Initial contact
aka heel strike, point at which heel of reference leg touches ground
- 0-2% of gait cycle
- Initial point of double support - contralateral limb at end [[#Terminal Stance]] (heel off, toe about to push off ground)

## Loading Response
heel portion largely in contact, entire foot about to land 
- 2-10% of gait cycle
- body weight transferred to leg and to floor
- Contralateral limb in [[#Preswing]]

## Midstance
Foot is completely in contact with the ground, other foot is off the ground
- 10-30% of gait cycle
- Unilateral stance, Head, Arms, Trunk aligned over single limb
- important to be stable at this point, [[Hip muscle action#Abductors stability and compression| Abductors active in mitigating pelvic drop]] 
- Contralateral limb in [[#Midswing]] 
## Terminal Stance
aka Heel off
- 30-50% gait cycle
- limb + rest of body moves forward past forefoot - hip is in extension
- Heel is not in contact with ground
- contralateral limb - [[#Terminal swing]] 


## Preswing
Aka Toe off
- 50-60% gait cycle
- Only metatarsal heads in contact with ground, [[Biomechanics of tiptoe|leg is tiptoeing]] 
- onset of double support period
- Contralateral limb - [[#Initial contact]] 

# Swing Phase 60-100%

## Initial swing
Foot is off the ground
- 60-73% gait cycle
- Knee flexion to shorten limb, accelerate limb
- Contralateral limb - [[#Midstance|Early midstance]] 
## Midswing
Leg is directly under/ anterior to HAT
- 73-87% gait cycle
- maximum knee flexion
- directly opposite to contralateral stance limb
- Contralateral limb - [[#Midswing|Late Midstance]] 
## Terminal swing
Leg is extends forwards and prepares for [[#Initial contact]] 
- 87-100% gait cycle
- Muscles work eccentrically to decelerate 
- Contralateral limb - [[#Terminal Stance]]
