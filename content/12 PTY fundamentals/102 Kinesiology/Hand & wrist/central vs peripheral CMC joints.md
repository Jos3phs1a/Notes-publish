CMC joints vary in mobility based on function
## Central joints
- 2nd and 3rd digits joints rigid, stable and fixed central pillar for hand
- strong ligaments + interlocking articular surfaces → very little movement → difficult to classify, ranging from planar to complex saddle joints

| Joint   | Proximal Articulating surface | Distal Articulating surface |
| ------- | ----------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| 2nd CMC | Distal surface Trapezoid      | Base 2nd metacarpal         |
| 3rd CMC | Distal surface Capitate       | Base 3rd metacarpal         |
## Peripheral joints:
- 4th, 5th ,[[1st CMC joint]] mobile, able to "fold" around central pillar
- ↑ dexterity by allowing concavity of palm to fit around many objects

| Joint   | Proximal Articulating surface              | Distal Articulating surface |
| ------- | ------------------------------------------ | --------------------------- |
| 4th CMC | distal concave surface Hamate and capitate | Convex base 4th metacarpal  |
| 5th CMC | distal concave surface Hamate              | Convex base 5th metacarpal  |
