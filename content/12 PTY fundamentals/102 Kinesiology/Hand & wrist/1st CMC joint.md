Most complex CMC joint with extensive and essential movements
- classic saddle joint
	- metacarpal: 
		- sagittal plane → convex  frontal plane→ concave
	- trapezium: 
		- sagittal plane → concave frontal plane → convex

# Arthrokinematics 

### Abduction/Adduction
movement towards/away from the plane of the hand/sagittal plane
- ==Convex articular surface of 1st metacarpal== rolls in ==palmar direction, slides dorsally== on ==fixed concave surface trapezium==
- Adductor pollicis + CMC joint ligaments elongate
vice versa for adduction

### Flexion/Extension
Movement along the palm/frontal plane
involves axial rotation of metacarpal
- Flexion: ==concave metacarpal surface== rolls and slides in ulnar ==(medial) direction== across transverse diameter of joint
vice versa for Extension

![[Hand & wrist images#^group=p7aGklBQVrt7x29IjNJc9|Flexion Extension|center|750]]
### Opposition/Reposition
==rotating the 1st MC on trapezium== to place pad of thumb against the other digits
Is a composite of two phases of movements
- Abduction
- Flexion + medial rotation
