## Scapular movement
upward rotation of scapula during full shoulder abduction serves at least 3 important functions

1) upward rotation projects glenoid fossa upward+ anterior-laterally, providing structural base to maximize upward and lateral reach of upper limb.
2) preserves optimal [[Active Length Tension Curve|Length-tension relationship]] of abductor muscles of [[Glenohumeral Joint]]
3) preserve volume of [[subacromial space]] 
	poor Scapular Humeral Rhythm leads to reduced subacromial space → painful, damaging compression aka [[01 Subacromial pain syndrome|SAPS due to impingement]]
