
## Structure:
- Scapula & Humerus
- [[types of joints|Ball & Socket]]
- Humeral Head does not fit perfectly into glenoid fossa (==25% engulfed==) allowing for more movement but also less stability
- Anatomical position:
	Glenoid fossa: upward rotated, articular surface project antero-laterally, aligning to scapular plane
	Humeral head: project medially, superiorly, posteriorly due to retroversion (rotating back)

- Diameters
	Longitudinal side: humeral head 1.9x glenoid fossa
	Transverse side: humeral head 2.3x glenoid fossa
	Result: 1/3 humeral head covered

#### Mobility:

**Articulating Surface**
	Not pure ball & socket
	Scapular movements provide extra mobility
	Lined with articular cartilage for lubrication of joint surface

**Joint capsule**
	Vol is 2x humeral head- increasing movement
	Synovial membrane lines inner wall of capsule - lubrication

#### Stability
1. Ligaments:
	Capsular ligaments:
	i. Superior GH ligament - resist inferior translation + ER & EXTN in neutral
	ii. Middle GH ligament -  resist ant translation 
	iii. Inferior GH ligament complex - resist ant/post translation + ER/IR at 90° abduction

2. Coracohumeral ligament

Heal by fibrosis, causing stiffness in body
Joint Capsule:
	Fibrous capsule strengthened by GH capsular ligaments
