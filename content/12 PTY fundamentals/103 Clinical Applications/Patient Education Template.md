# Principles

- Procedural - what I'm going to do
- Sensory - what patient is going to feel
- Behavioural - what should patient do
- Risk/Benefit - what does the treatment do
- Seek consent - understand? any questions? get started? 

ALWAYS ASK CONSENT FOR PHYSICAL CONTACT + REMOVAL OF CLOTHING
# Sensory risk id tests

## Observation of target area
check for open wounds and bruises on treatment area
## Sharp Blunt
- Purpose
	to see if you can tell between ==different types of pain sensations== so that treatment will be safe
- Procedure
	demo stimulus - *press blunt, press sharp* 
	*test with patient facing away*


## Hot Cold
- Purpose
	to see if you can ==distinguish between different temperatures==
	extra explanation: some patients might not be able to tell if something is too hot or too cold → risk skin burns or frostbite/frostnip if we don't monitor
- Procedure
	Demo stimulus - *Press warm, Press cold*
	*Test with patient facing away*
## Nail Blanch
- Purpose
	Check for normal blood flow in your hands and legs
- Procedure
	Press nail until change colour
	observe colour change back to red ==within 3 seconds==

## Cold reaction
- Purpose
	Test your skin's reaction to ice to make sure you have no allergies or bad reactions
	some people develop rashes due to cold treatment
- Procedure
	Use ice cube/ice pop to gently rub skin for ~30 secs
	observe local response after 5 mins
	any swelling or rashes indicate [[cold urticaria]] which looks like hives 

# [[02 Cryotherapy|Cryotherapy]] 

- R/B:
	How this helps? ==slow down blood flow== that ==causes swelling and redness== around your injury.
	substances in body trying to start healing process  → more blood flow but this also causes swelling and pain 
	
	
	It also reduces your pain by blocking the pain sensors on your body
	**Analogy:** imagine a ==hose as your nerve== and the ==water passing through = pain signals== to brain, I freeze the water, very hard to get flow through the hose, so the body cannot feel the pain.

	During the treatment, just relax and try not to move the injured area too much
	
- S:

However, if **you feel any extreme pain or discomfort, you must immediately tell me**, otherwise you may be a **risk of a cold burn**. If I'm not around, ring the bell
	
- C:
	- Do you understand what we are going to do?
	- Do you any questions?
	- Shall we proceed?

- Conclusion
	- cold will make your muscles and nerves a bit ==numb & less responsive==  ==avoid any strenuous activity for a few hours==
# [[03 Thermotherapy|Thermotherapy]]

- R/B:
	1) The heat ==increases blood flow== to ==speed up the healing process== of the body.
	
	2) Helps relax your muscles, reduce tightness & stiffness
	**Analogy:** Traffic example, heat reduces the traffic, ==motor nerves== that make your ==muscle contract fire less and muscles relax==
	
	3) Reduces pain
	**Analogy:** nerves = road, pain signals = cars and traffic
	When there's ==fire or blockage== on road, cars from ==sensor neuron== cannot use the road anymore, so its ==harder to reach their destination(brain)==. So pain lesser because the nerves are too busy sensing the warmth.
	
- P: 
	Hot packs: "I'm going to place this Heat pack wrapped in a towel over your injured area and leave it on there for about 20 minutes"
	"For the few minutes, I will check to make sure everything is going smoothly" ==(check skin temp + any adverse reactions)==
	
- B:
	During treatment just relax and try not to move the heat pack around too much
	
- S:
	feel ==mild comfortable warmth== over the area
	If it gets ==too hot for you==, or if the ==heat concentrates at a particular area==
	If **you feel any extreme pain or discomfort, you must immediately tell me**, otherwise you may be a **risk of a skin burn**.

	
- C: 
	- Do you understand what we are going to do?
	- Do you any questions?
	- Shall we proceed?
	
- Conclusion
	- muscles and nerves more ==relaxed and less responsive== due to the longer effects, ==avoid any strenuous activity for a few hours==

# [[05 Electrotherapy|Electrotherapy]]

## TENS/IFC   

- R/B
	Using this device, I will send small and safe electric currents through your injured area, 
	==Triggers nerves that block pain signals==, so your body feels less pain
	Release of ==chemicals in your brain== to ==reduce pain levels==
	
- P
	I'm going to place these pads around your injury
	The treatment will last about 30 minutes, I will monitor to you closely for the first few minutes, once things look ok I'll give you privacy for the remaining duration and check in occasionally.
	
- B

	==Do not move or touch the equipment during treatment==, if in doubt or uncomfortable just call me. 
	if need expose: so that I can visualize the muscles around better, and place the pads such that treatment is more effective
- S
	you will feel ==tingling sensation==, sometimes a ==bit numb around the area== between the pads
	if you feel **anything other than tingling or any pain or discomfort**, must **notify me immediately**, other you **may risk skin and other tissue damage**


- C

	- Do you any questions?
	- Shall we proceed?
