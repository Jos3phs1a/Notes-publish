# Pathologic Models

- Evidence to suggest tendinopathy cannot fully reverse
- implications on management and prevention
	- preventive load management strategies
	- eccentric loading to induce beneficial cell remodeling
	- supervised gradual loading programs
	- complete rest not recommended, detrimental to tendon
## Inflammation Model
- inflammation often present (symptom rather than cause)
- [[White blood cells#Macrophages/Monocytes|Macrophages]] and [[White blood cells#Mast cells|mast cells]] involved
- Elevated inflammatory mediators such as prostaglandins & metalloproteinases

## Cell Mediated Pathology Model
- Tenocytes (tendon cells) with collagen matrix respond to strain by altering gene expression
- Too much/little strain causes cells to drive pathology
## Collagen Disruption/Tearing Model
- excess stretch/strain leading to damage
- small microtears develop and grow into larger disruptions



# Donut & Hole Theory

Shift in mindset towards understanding anatomical vascularity and structural difference in tendons
- Hole = hypovascular core     Donut = Well perfused region
- ==core more vulnerable to initiating pathology== → spreads outwards to surrounding region
- affected tendons thicken, with **↑ pathological tissue** at centre + **↑ healthy tissue** in peripheral region ^6b5b99

### Implications
- [[#^6b5b99|Presence of extra healthy tissue]] allows ↑ load bearing capacity 
- Core pathology can stimulate adaptive growth in peripheral region → targeted loading + remodeling without risk of overload and failure

## Load tolerance Paradigm
- Tendons have limited capacity, load tolerance envelope similar to [[elasticity model]]
- Exceeding load tolerance envelope results in tendinopathy as tendons unable to adapt 
- Building tendon capacity through progressive loading + introducing load variability can improve load tolerance

## Biomechanical paradigm
- aim to understand mechanical forces acting on structures during movement
- Excessive tensile, compressive, shear forces
- abnormal joint mechanics e.g. impingement, malalignment
- muscle imbalance/weakness

# Risk Factors

### Intensity
- High intensity and rapid load activities place excess strain on tendons 

### Vol/Frequency
- sudden spikes in frequency without adequate adaptation
- constant high vol

### Sudden change in activity
- abrupt change in activity, alter in loads, footwear, surface

### Individual predispositions
- [[#Biomechanical paradigm]] e.g. low muscle capacity, altered biomechanics, faulty movement patterns
- Systemic factors: Age, menopause, cholesterol etc
