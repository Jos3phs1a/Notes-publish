Tendinopathy is classified into 3 stages of continuum + 1 substage, can move up or down this continuum by adding or removing load, progressing in the continuum makes it harder to revert back to normal.


## Reactive 
 1st stage on the tendon continuum and is a ==non-inflammatory proliferative response== in the cell matrix due to ==rapid onset related to load==
 
- fusiform swelling/thickening of tendon
- easily aggravated by exercise, slow to settle
- changes ==may not be visible== on imaging


## Dysrepair
if tendon not offloaded and allowed to regress to normal ↑ protein production result in ==separation of collagen and disorganisation== within cell matrix

- changes ==visible on imaging==, but still difficult to diagnose → importance on [[History taking]]

## Degenerative
final stage of continuum, suggested there is a poor prognosis for tendon and changes are **irreversible**

- long history of symptoms
- variable swelling and bumps

## Reactive on degenerative
combination of  [[#Reactive]] & [[#Degenerative]] 

- Result of flare up of normal part of tendon
