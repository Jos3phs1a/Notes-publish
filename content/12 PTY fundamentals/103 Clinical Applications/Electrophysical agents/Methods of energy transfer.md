# Conduction

Transfer heat directly between molecules

## Examples

#### Cryotherapy 
Ice Packs
Ice Massage
#### Thermotherapy
heat packs
# Convection

direct contact between skin and **Fluid particles** 
Note especially during [[03 Thermotherapy]], blood circulation reduces the effect of therapy by shifting warmer blood away from site of treatment

## Examples
#### Cryotherapy
Ice bath
Cold whirlpools

#### Thermotherapy
Fluidotherapy
Warm whirlpools

# Evaporation

cooling effect felt when vapor applied to region transfers heat away as it dissipates

for myofascial pain + trigger point release

## Examples
#### Vapocoolant sprays e.g. ethyl chloride
apply 12-18 inches away from skin
sweep to provide even distribution along muscle
repetitions: 30-60 sec x 3-5 times 



# Conversion

Involves transformation of nonthermal energy eg mechanical, electrical, chemical into thermal energy

Relies on power of energy source

No need direct contact, rely on medium for effective energy transfer as air is poor conductor

e.g.
[[04 Ultrasound]] sound → heat
[[05 Electrotherapy]] 
# Radiation

Transfer of energy from one material to another without intervening medium or direct contact

Influenced by:
- intensity of radiation
- size of source
- distance from treatment area
- angle between radiation and tissue
