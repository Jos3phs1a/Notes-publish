# Effect on hemodynamics

## Blood flow 
Cold generally decreases blood flow through 2 means
### Indirectly
affect blood flow via [[Regulating Blood Flow#Mechanism|vasoconstriction]] 
- ↓ [[histamine]] & [[bradykinin]] 
- ↑ Muscle tone
- ↑ Dorsal root ganglion stimulation via [[Processes affected by ANS action|Sympathetic activity]] 

### Directly
- ↓ [[Total peripheral resistance#Blood viscosity|Blood viscosity]] 


## cold induced vasodilation(CIVD) aka hunting response
- seen <10°C to prevent frostbite, occurring in distal extremities e.g. toes or fingers <1°C for longer than 15mins
- response to high dosage of tissue cooling to body areas
- mediated by axon reflex responding to pain of prolonged cold

### Guidelines
#### CIVD is intended goal
do not use cryotherapy due to inconsistency

#### CIVD is not intended
apply cryotherapy < 15 mins + monitor

## 1 Nerve conduction velocity(NCV)
aka speed of signal transfer between neurons
cold decreases NCV in both **sensory & motor neurons**

### Variables
#### Duration
superficial cooling duration affects time taken for NCV to revert to normal
- ~ 5 mins → 15 mins to revert
- if > 20 mins → 30 mins to revert

#### Type of fiber treated
[[Nociceptors#A-delta fibers|A-delta fibers]] experience larger effect 
[[Nociceptors#C fibers|C fibers]] experience smallest effect

### Application
take precaution when applying cryo to peroneal nerve and ulnar nerve

--- column-break ---

## 2 Pain relief
when cold is sufficiently intense, pain threshold ↑ and sensation of pain ↓ 

### proposed causes
many inferred mechanisms but unable to definitively prove due to lack of evidence
- direct factors e.g. spinal gating
- indirect factors e.g. reducing [[Physiological effects of cryotherapy#Nerve conduction velocity(NCV)|NCV]], muscle spasms, [[edema]]/swelling, acute [[Inflammation]] via ↓ flow of fluid into interstitia

## 3 Muscle Performance
Maximum tetanic tension is relatively stable between temperatures of ==25 and 35°C==
if muscles temps below this range → ↓ muscle performance, unable to generate sufficient tension`

### Application
perform cryo before or hours after strenuous activity
avoid physical tasks requiring accuracy, balance, proprioception for a few hours after cryo 

--- column-break ---

## 4 Spasticity
cold can reduce muscle tone, *can be(not totally understood)* attributed to change in ==muscle spindle and neurons activity/sensitivity ==
thus reducing muscle spasticity temporarily

### Application
10-30 minutes of cooling: reduces discharge from afferent spindles & golgi tendon organs 
persisting for 1 - 1.5 hours after cooling

# Metabolic Effects

## Lower metabolic rates
- reducing reactions involved with [[Inflammation]]
- cartilage-degrading enzymes have reduced activity e.g. collagenase, elastase, hyaluronidase, protease, histamine
