## Burns
- Too high temperatures exceeding [[therapeutic window]] cause protein denaturation and cell death
- occur when:
	- duration too long
	- Agent too hot
	- patient inadequate vasodilation response

#### Measures to avoid tissue damage
- superficial heating agents 
- limit initial temp of agent
- use enough insulation between agent and skin
- provide patient means to call PT
## Fainting
- heating specific area causes vasodilation to local and distant areas(to smaller extent) 
- distant reaction = **consensual response**, ↓ cerebral blood flow may induce faints
	- if faint: lower head and elevate feet to ↑ blood flow to brain and facilitate recovery
- standing up may induce faints due to ==hypotensive effects of heat and postural hypotension==
	- keep patient head elevated using pillow during heat application
