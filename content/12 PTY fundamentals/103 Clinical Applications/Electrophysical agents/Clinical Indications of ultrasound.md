# Pain control

### Direct Influence
[[physiological effects of ultrasound#Non-thermal effects|Non thermal Modality]] can modulate pain receptors
altering nerve activity and elevating pain threshold

### Indirect Influence
↑ blood flow & capillary permeability → ↑ O$_2$ delivery to hypoxic areas
↓ activity of chemosensitive pain receptors


# Usage in various stages of healing

## Inflammatory
- Stimulating effect on platelets & [[White blood cells|WBC, macrophages, mast cells]]
- reduces time in this phase to move on to proliferative phase

## Proliferative 
- stimulates fibroblasts, endothelial cells, myofibroblast which contribute to scar tissue production
- optimise scar tissue production

## Remodelling
Improves tensile strength by:
- Aid in lining new collagen fibers
- change collagen profile from type III to type I


# Phonopheresis (sonophoresis)

technique by which therapeutic ultrasound is used to introduce pharmacologic agents, usually anti-inflammatory or analgesic drugs, through intact skin into the subcutaneous tissues

- ultrasound alters permeability of [[Layers of skin|stratum corneum]]
- when permeability ↑, medication will diffuse across better
- medication is typically mixed with non reactive base like ultrasound gel
