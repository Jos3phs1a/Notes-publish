Ratio of highest intensity within the beam to average intensity reported on output meter

$$BNR=\frac{Spatial\:Peak\:Intensity}{Spatial\:Average\:Intensity}$$
Ideal = 1:1 but ==not possible== to due flaws in mass production
- Higher BNR = less uniform beam and higher peak intensity generated
- Most sound heads have **BNR of 5:1 to 6:1**
- Higher peak intensities will generate hotspots on beams, [[Adverse effects of ultrasound#Burns|risk of burns]] + uneven treatment ↓ efficacy
E.g. BNR = 4:1 + output 3W, peak intensity = 12W, **DON'T KEEP TRANSDUCER STILL** during [[Application of ultrasound]]
