

# Dosage consideration

Before application, must decide the [[Parameters of Ultrasound]] to use
Will vary based on patients tolerance and type of tissue applying & tissue bulk 

Guidelines are as follows


| Phase         | Pulse ratio                                                                | intensity (W/cm$^2$) | Frequency                                                  | Treatment area                                                                          |
| ------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Acute**     | lowest e.g. 1:4/3 ~25% [[Parameters of Ultrasound#Duty cycle\|Duty cycle]] | 0.1 -0.3             | **Superficial** <2cm - 3MHz<br>**Deep**  > 2cm - 1 MHz<br> | X times of [[Parts of an Ultrasound#Transducer\|Transducer Head]] <br>e.g. 1x 2x 3x etc |
| **Sub Acute** | mid range e.g. 1: 2/1 ~ 33%-50%                                            | 0.2-0.5              | ^                                                          | ^                                                                                       |
| **Chronic**   | High e.g. 1:1/Continuous                                                   | 0.3-1.0              | ^                                                          | ^                                                                                       |

### Treatment Time
Normally 1 min worth of ultrasound head is given per treatment head area, 
ie if pulsed, require longer time, if area larger need more time
$$Treatment\:time=1\:min\:*\:treatment\:heads\:fitted\:*pulse\:factor$$
**pulse factor** = sum of pulse ratios
e.g. for 25% [[Parameters of Ultrasound#Duty cycle|Duty cycle]] 1:3, Pulse factor = 4

#### Note
Good guideline but will vary based on case, sometimes should overestimate treatment area
sometimes should lower intensity/pulse ratio based on patient tolerance/symptoms
# Methods

## Direct contact
Applying ultrasound directly over the skin (with gel)
![[Pasted image 20250525182306.png|center]]

### Technique
important to get good contact surface by moving with curvature of the skin 

![[Pasted image 20250525182241.png|center|600]]

To avoid [[Adverse effects of ultrasound#Burns|Burns]], always move transducer head in either circular or longitudinal manner, lines **should overlap one another**
Movement should be done relatively slowly, don't need rush
