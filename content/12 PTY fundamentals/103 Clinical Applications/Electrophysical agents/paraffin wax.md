- used for arthritis and ROM restrictions of wrist, hands, fingers
- paraffin has low specific heat capacity, does not feel as hot as water at same temp
- mixture of 6-7 parts wax to 1 part oil
- maintain temp at 45 - 50°C using paraffin oil or mineral oil

| Advantages                                                                           | Disadvantages                                                     |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Low specific heat allows higher temp but without risk of burn                        | effective only for distal extremities                             |
| low thermal conductivity, heating of tissues to occur more slowly, low risk of burns | bath is most effective, limits accessibility for other body parts |
| even distribution of heat to treatment area                                          | no method of temp control                                         |
| oils in wax add moisture to skin                                                     | heating lasts about 20 mins only                                  |
| wax remains malleable after removal, use as exercise tool                            | passive intervention, cannot perform exercises                    |
| relative inexpensive                                                                 |                                                                   |
