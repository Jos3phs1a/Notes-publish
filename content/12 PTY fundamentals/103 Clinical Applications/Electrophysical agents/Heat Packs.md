## Usage
Typically wrapped in tower before applying directly over area of treatment

## Rate of heat transfer
Must always factor 
affects the size and required temperature of heat pack used, 
ie 
2 heat packs same temp, the larger one with more mass delivers more heat to tissue

$$ Rate\:of\:Heat\:transfer = Area_{\:contact} * Thermal\:Conductivity * \frac{Temp\:diff}{Tissue\:thickness}$$

| Advantages                | Disadvantages                                                        |
| ------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| easy prep and application | no method of temp control                                            |
| various shapes and sizes  | does not conform to all body parts                                   |
| moist, comfortable heat   | sometimes awkward to secure in place                                 |
| relative inexpensive      | passive intervention, patient cannot perform exercise simaltaneously |
|                           | may leak and must be discarded                                       |
