# How it works

- using high frequency mechanical waves to transmit mechanical energy which is converted to heat energy upon contact of projected area
- Waves are produced by applying AC current on crystal on [[Parts of an Ultrasound#Transducer|transducer]], constant expansion(compressions) and contraction(rarefactions) produces longitudinal waves
- This rapid expansion and compression can only happen between [[Parameters of Ultrasound|1MHz and 3MHz]]



# Ultrasound beam

2 components
1) Fresnel zone - Near field, closer to [[Parts of an Ultrasound#Transducer|Transducer]]
	- Denoted by X$_0$ max
	- Length determined via $$Radius_{transducer}^{2}\:*\:Frequency$$
	
2) Fraunhofer zone - Far field

Ultrasound waveform projection is not uniform due to deformities in mass produced crystals
causing energy projection to be uneven
Results in [[Beam nonuniformity ratio]] which affects [[Application of ultrasound#Technique|Application]]
