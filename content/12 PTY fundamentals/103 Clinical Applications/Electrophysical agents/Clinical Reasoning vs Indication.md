**Clinical reasoning** is the **cognitive process** that healthcare professionals use to gather information, interpret data, and make decisions about a patient's care.

**Clinical indications** are the **specific medical reasons** that justify using a certain **treatment, test, or procedure**.

|Aspect|**Clinical Reasoning**|**Clinical Indications**|
|---|---|---|
|**What it is**|Thinking and decision-making process|Justification for a specific medical action|
|**Focus**|How decisions are made|When and why to use a specific tool/intervention|
|**User**|Clinician's internal process|Often part of guidelines or protocols|
|**Example**|Diagnosing shoulder pain as rotator cuff tear|Ordering ultrasound imaging for suspected tear|
