## on Innervated Muscles
Unlike voluntary contractions, NMES recruit [[motor units]] in a different order

| Contraction             | Voluntary                                              | NMES                                   |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------- |
| Motor Command           | Upper motor neurons                                    | Electrical stmulator                   |
| Spatial Recruitment     | Smaller to large [[motor units]]<br>Type 1 → Type 2b   | Large to small ie <br>Type 2b → Type 1 |
| Temporal recruitment    | Asynchronous ie not all motor units contract same time | Synchronous                            |
| Onset of muscle fatigue | Slow                                                   | Rapid                                  |
| Quality of contraction  | Gradual & graded increase in force                     | Rapid & Jerky onset of contractions    |

#### Muscle Strengthening
Based on amplitude, current can aid patient in improving **Maximum Voluntary Isometric Contraction(MVIC) Strength**

**Without injury:** amp > ==50% MVIC==
**Recovery from injury/surgery**: amp > ==10% MVIC==

## on Denervated Muscles
nerve injury or disease prevents muscles from contracting
#### Muscle Reeducation
**Electrical Muscle stimulation(EMS)**  used to 
1) stimulate and induce contraction 
2) help regain voluntary control
3) prevent atrophy

[[NMES application parameters]], contractions produced when [[Parameters of Electrical Stimulation#Direct|DC]] Or [[Parameters of Electrical Stimulation#Pulsed|Pulsed]] has ==width > 10ms== 
*Note may trigger [[Physiological Effects of Electrotherapy#^72ad92|A-delta and C nerves]] if present, cause discomfort*  

Application of this is [[Functional Electrical Stimulation]] 
## Reduce Spasm/[[edema]]
- visible contractions produced
- only use when caused by lack of muscle activity, [[Physiological Effects of Electrotherapy#^596099|not systemic]]
