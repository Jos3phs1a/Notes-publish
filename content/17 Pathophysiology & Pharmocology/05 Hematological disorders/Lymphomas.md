# Hodgkin Lymphoma

## overview
- 40% of adult lymphoma, less common than [[#Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma]]
- more prevalent in males
- Bimodal age distribution, more common amongst 15-35 yr olds
- malignant, progresses from one group of lymph nodes to another

**risk factors:**
- [[Epsteinbarr virus]] (present in 70% cases)
## Pathophysiology
Characterized by presence of **Reed–Sternberg (RS) cells** - malignant transformed lymphocytes
- large, binucleate with occasional mononuclear variants. 
- triggering of transformation remains unknown
- ==contagious spread in local area==

## Presentation
symptoms are variable and depend on site of disease
- **Constitutional symptoms:** Fevers, itchy skin, night sweats
**Variable symptoms:**
- [[lymphadenopathy]] commonly at the neck localized, painless
- [[splenomegaly]] but less severe than [[Leukemias#^14b20e|CML]] 
- Nephrotic syndrome → esp in children
- Jaundice → if liver nodes obstructed

# Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma

## overview
- involve either B-cell(70%) or T & NK-cells(30%)
- nodal or extranodal(outside lymph nodes)
- more common amongst older adults
- spread is ==less predictable== & ==more distant== compared to [[#Hodgkin Lymphoma]]

**risk factors:**
- AIDS patients
- organ transplant
- immunosuppressed patients/immunodeficiency
- [[Epsteinbarr virus]] esp for [[#Burkitt lymphoma]]
## Pathophysiology
- progressive clonal expansion of B cells, T cells, or NK cells
- activated by chromosomal translocations or tumor-suppressor loci may be inactivated by deletion or mutation of chromosomes.

## Subtypes
### Diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL)
- **mature B-cells acquire genetic mutations** 
- _“diffuse”_ because the normal lymph node architecture is **completely effaced** by sheets of large malignant B-cells
**prognostic factors**
- age
- perf status
- LDH
- extranodal site
- stage

### Burkitt lymphoma
- B-cell NHL
- highly aggressive & fast growing
- 3 main types: endemic, sporadic, immunodeficiency-related

**treatment**
- aggressive multidrug regimens, such as combination chemotherapy
- no role for radiation therapy
### Lymphoblastic lymphoma
rare variant of NHL overall (2% to 4%)
but accounts for almost one-third of cases of NHL in children and adolescents, male
predominance.
vast majority(90%) is of T-cell origin; remainder arises from B cells.

**treatment**
- combined chemotherapy (intensive therapy). 
- In early stages, response rate is high, with increased survival, but high relapse rate for adults

### other substypes
- Follicular lymphoma
- Mantle cell lymphoma
- Peripheral T-cell lymphoma
etc.
