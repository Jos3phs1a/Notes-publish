- **Definition:**  
    This refers to **any procedure** in which hematopoietic (blood-forming) stem cells are collected and infused into a patient to restore bone marrow function.
- It includes **both** types of transplant

# Classification
### Autologous HSCT
Patient’s _own_ stem cells are collected, stored, then reinfused after high-dose chemotherapy or radiation.
for:
- [[Myelomas]]
- [[Lymphomas]]
### Allogeneic HSCT
Stem cells come from a _donor_ (either related or unrelated, or umbilical cord blood).
for: 
- [[Acute leukemias]]
- myelodysplastic syndrome

# Procedure

| **Stage**                | **Purpose**                                                                                                        | **Main Components**                  |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------ |
| **Preparation**          | Assess fitness and match donor<br>siblings preferred, if not unrelated donor with matching genetic makeup is useds | HLA typing, infection screening      |
| **Conditioning**         | Eradicate malignancy + suppress immunity (prevent new stem cell rejection)                                         | High-dose chemo ± TBI                |
| **Transplant (Day 0)**   | Infuse stem cells                                                                                                  | IV infusion of harvested stem cells  |
| **Engraftment**          | Establish new hematopoiesis                                                                                        | Monitor counts, infection prevention |
| **Post-Transplant Care** | Immune recovery, GVHD prevention                                                                                   | Immunosuppressants, follow-up        |
### conditioning phase
targeted therapy used to clear lymphocytes

|**Type**|**Intensity**|**Examples**|**Used for**|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Myeloablative conditioning (MAC)**|High-dose chemo ± total body irradiation (TBI)|Busulfan + Cyclophosphamide, or TBI + Cyclophosphamide|Younger, fit patients|
|**Reduced-intensity conditioning (RIC)**|Lower-dose chemo; less toxic|Fludarabine + Melphalan / Busulfan|Older or comorbid patients|

# Complications

## Graft versus host disease
### mechanism
when **donor T-lymphocytes** attack **recipient’s body** 

pro-inflammatory cytokines produced by remaining recipient cells present in blood
new cells enter patient, T-cells in graft proliferate and grow in response to cytokines → damaging tissues

### treatment
- steroids
- immunotherapy (Immunosuppressant)
	Tacrolimus / Cyclosporine
	Mycophenolate mofetil
	Extracorporeal photopheresis (ECP)
- target therapy
	Infliximab - targets cytokine TNF-α to reduce inflammation
