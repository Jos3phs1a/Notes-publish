# Pathophysiology

similar to other cancers, poor differentiation followed by uncontrolled cell division aka clonal expansion & proliferation.
## Chromosomal translocations 
==major mechanism== behind leukemias
[[Acute leukemias#Acute lymphoblastic leukemia|ALL]]:
- t(12;21) → **ETV6-RUNX1**
- t(9;22) → BCR-ABL (in some cases)

[[Acute leukemias#Acute myelogenous leukemia|AML]]: 
- t(15;17) → **PML-RARA** (seen in acute promyelocytic leukemia)
- t(8;21), inv(16) → fusion genes that alter transcription factors

[[#Chronic myeloid leukemia|CML]]: 
- t(9;22) → **BCR-ABL1** protein (Philadelphia chromosome) presents 95% of patients 

## other mechanisms
- Point mutations (e.g., FLT3, NPM1, IDH1/2 in [[Acute leukemias#Acute myelogenous leukemia|AML]])
- Gene deletions or amplifications
- Epigenetic alterations (e.g., DNA methylation changes)
- Abnormal cell signaling or microenvironmental factors

# Complications
## Leukostasis
**medical emergency** that can happen in [[#Acute leukemias]]
- The **white blood cell count becomes extremely high** (usually >100,000/μL),
- The **blasts (large, sticky, immature cells)** make blood **viscous (“thick”)**,
- And the cells **clog small blood vessels**, impairing oxygen and nutrient flow
    
This causes **microvascular obstruction** and **ischemia** in vital organs. e.g. eye brain, etc.

## Disseminated intravascular coagulation
a condition in which blood clots form throughout the body, blocking small blood vessels.
symptoms: chest pain, shortness of breath, leg pain, speech problems , problems moving limbs

## Tumor lysis syndrome
cancer cells are rapidly destroyed by treatment, releasing their contents into the bloodstream which are toxic to body
may cause kidney failure, heart arrhythmias, and seizures

### [[Hematopoietic Stem Cell Transplant]]
[[#Acute myelogenous leukemia|AML]]:
MRD = minimal residual disease
- **Negative MRD:** No detectable leukemia (better prognosis)
- **Positive MRD:** Persistent leukemic cells (higher relapse risk)

**NRM (Non-Relapse Mortality)** = death from transplant complications (not from leukemia relapse).

**HCT-CI (Hematopoietic Cell Transplantation–Comorbidity Index)** = a score that predicts the risk of transplant-related mortality based on comorbidities.
