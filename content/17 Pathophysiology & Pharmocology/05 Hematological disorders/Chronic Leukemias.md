# Chronic lymphocytic leukemia
 - proliferation of monoclonal lymphoid cells
 - most cases occur in elderly
 - considered indolent (slow progressing)

## Diagnosis
using [[Flow cytometry]] and Mutates' scoring system

|              Marker              | Typical CLL expression | Scoring principle   | Meaning                                                                           |
| :------------------------------: | ---------------------- | ------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
|             **CD5**              | Positive               | +1 if positive      | usually a _T-cell marker_, unusual expression on B-cells is characteristic of CLL |
|             **CD23**             | Positive               | +1 if positive      | strongly expressed in CLL but absent in mantle cell lymphoma                      |
| **Surface immunoglobulin (sIg)** | Weak or negative       | +1 if weak/negative | CLL cells express very low levels of surface Ig                                   |
|             **FMC7**             | Negative               | +1 if negative      | _absent_ in CLL, but positive in mantle cell and other lymphomas                  |
|          **CD22/CD79b**          | Weak or negative       | +1 if weak/negative | These markers are part of the B-cell receptor complex and are _weak_ in CLL       |

## Staging
_extent_ and _severity_ of the disease using Rai(US) or Binet(UK) system

# Chronic myeloid leukemia
- pluripotent hematopoietic steam cell neoplasm
- arises from reciprocal translocation and fusion of ==BCR on chromosome 22== and ==ABL1 on chromosome 9==, resulting in ==dysregulated tyrosine kinase== on chromosome 22 called the **Philadelphia (Ph) chromosome**
- monoclonal population of dysfunctional granulocytes

three phases of the disease: 
1. chronic phase
	2 to 5 years,
 2. accelerated phase 
	Blasts >15%, Basophils >20%
	6 to 18 months, primary symptoms develop
 3. terminal blastic phase
	Blasts >30%
	extramedullary disease with localized immature balsts
	(“blast crisis”) with survival of only 3 to 6 months.
