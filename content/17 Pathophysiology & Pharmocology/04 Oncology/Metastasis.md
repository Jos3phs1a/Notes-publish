### Orderly spread
Same type of cancer always spread in a pattern
- colorectal e.g. pelvis → lymph nodes → liver → lungs
- cervical

--- column-break ---

### Random spread
hard to discern where next cancer cells will spread to 
- breast 
- lung 

--- end-multi-column

## Mechanism
1. Cells detach from primary tumour and go pass tissue lining of primary site
2. spread to other parts of the body through blood or lymphatic vessels
3. Establish & growth at new site

Frequently develop in:
- [[first pass organs]]
- metabolize substances that can development into carcinogens
- are frequent sites of cancers due to high exposure to substances
	e.g. [[Liver]], [[15 Lungs|lungs]] , [[Small Intestine]]

- Crucial organs
- Vascular organs (rarer) e.g. [[00 kidney Anatomy|kidney]]
