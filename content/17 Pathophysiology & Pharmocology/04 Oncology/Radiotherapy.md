## Procedure
Encompass following principles:
- irradiate all tumor
- usually including regional lymph nodes
- with margin
- within one "field" - **entire target region** (tumor + margin) is treated by a **single beam configuration**.

### Fractionated treatment
- normal tissues have better tolerance than cancer cells
- short cycles of radiation to kill tumour while allowing normal cells to recover
- minimize [[#Side effects]]

## Mechanism
1. ionisation of water generates ==Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS)== or free radicals 
2. highly reactive and damage molecules in the cell by oxidation 
3. ==Damage DNA bonds== in cells and prevent mitosis 
4. Cells with DNA damage do not die immediately, but are ==unable to reproduce== 

- most marked acute effects will be in those cells/tissue with highest mitotic activity
	- [[#Side effects]] typically manifest when this occurs (around 2 week mark)
- Radiosensitivity of different cancers vary 
	- ↓ sensitivity due to ==↓ division and effect by radiation== → ↑ dosage required
	- In descending order: Lymphoma, small cell, BCC, SCC AdenoCa, Sarcoma, RCC, Melanoma
## Side effects
since radiotherapy is targeted at tumour site, [side effects tend to stem from local tissue and vary](https://www.cancer.org/cancer/managing-cancer/treatment-types/radiation/effects-on-different-parts-of-body.html)
e.g. 

| mouth                                                | arm                  |
| ---------------------------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| abundance of nerves, ↑sensitivity                    | less sensitive       |
| can easily develop ulcers and pain during swallowing | radiation dermatitis |

### Early 
- Common, occurs from 2 wks after treatment
- Symptoms are typically reversible and treatable
e.g. 
- Fatigue
- Skin change
- vary based on treatment site e.g. hair loss, mouth problems

--- column-break ---

### Late
- Variable,  depends on the radiation dose and the area treated
- occurs from > 3 months after end of therapy
- often irreversible 
e.g. 
- decreased function in gonads and fertility in men and women
