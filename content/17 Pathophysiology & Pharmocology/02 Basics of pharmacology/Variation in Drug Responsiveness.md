# Mechanism

## Difference in drug concentration that reach receptor site
- attributed to difference in [[02c pharmacokinetics Metabolism]]
- results in different clinical response
- some people may have multidrug resistance genes(MDR) → active pumping of drugs out of cells e.g. tumor cells resistance to cancer drugs

## Variation in concentration of endogenous receptor ligand
Drugs often compete or interact with natural (endogenous) ligands like neurotransmitters, hormones, or autacoids.

**Effects:**
- If **endogenous ligand levels are high**, the drug may have reduced effect (antagonists may need higher doses).
    
    - Example: In **hyperthyroidism**, excess thyroid hormone reduces sensitivity to β-blockers.
        
- If **endogenous ligand levels are low**, the drug effect may be exaggerated.
    
    - Example: In **Addison’s disease** (low cortisol), sensitivity to exogenous corticosteroids is greater.
## Alterations in number or function of receptors
Drug response depends directly on receptor density and sensitivity.

Upregulation (increase in receptor number):
	Leads to **supersensitivity** when agonist is given.
Downregulation (decrease in receptor number):
	Leads to **tolerance**.
## Changes in components of response distal to receptor
After receptor binding, the signal is transmitted through second messengers, ion channels, enzymes, and cellular machinery. Variations here also affect response. 
Can diminish or exaggerate response independent of receptor binding.
