Distribution of drugs to various tissues in the body is measured via

$$Vd=\frac{Amount\;of\;drug\;in\;body}{Concentration\;in\;plasma/blood}$$

- Vd can vastly exceed physical vol of body

**Small Vd:** drug stays within bloodstream e.g. aminoglycosides (~5L, close to blood vol)
**Large Vd:** drug goes to organs e.g. digoxin (exceed body weight)

| Drug factors                                                     | Patient factors                                          |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| Lipid solubility - fat soluble distribute into fatty tissue, Vd↑ | Fat & water content (age,gender,obesity)                 |
| Molecular size                                                   | Protein levels e.g. albumin                              |
| degree of ionization                                             | disease states                                           |
| Partition coefficient                                            | Concomitant drugs - displaces drugs from binding protein |
| protein binding - ↑ binding, stays within bloodstream, Vd↓       |                                                          |

## clinical significance

- affects [[02d pharmacokinetics Excretion#half life|half life]] of drug 
	**high** **Vd** will have a _**longer**_ **elimination** **half-life** than a drug with _**lower**_ **Vd.**

- _single_ most important utility of Vd is **calculating the loading dose of a drug**
