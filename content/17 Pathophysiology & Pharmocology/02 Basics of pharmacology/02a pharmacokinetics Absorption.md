Effect of drugs depends on how much enters our bloodstream, different methods of administration have varying efficacy and considerations

## Bioavailability
**Definition:** ==fraction of unchanged drug== that reaches ==system circulation== after administration

affected by [[#routes of administration]] 

##  routes of administration
1. **Oral**
	after absorption by [[Small Intestine#Ileum|Ileum]], pass through the [[liver]] via the hepatic portal vein → some broken down before entering the bloodstream
	other factors:

| Drug factors                                                          | Patient factors                 |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Lipid solubility                                                      | Area of absorptive surfaces     |
| Molecular size                                                        | Gut pH                          |
| Degree of ionization/pKa - strong acid remain unionized, ↑ absorption | Gut motility                    |
| Stability in gastric acid - some require coating to prevent breakdown | Presence of food or other drugs |

2. **Transdermal**
3. **Intravenous**
	[[#Bioavailability]] 100% as drugs are directly injected into the bloodstream
