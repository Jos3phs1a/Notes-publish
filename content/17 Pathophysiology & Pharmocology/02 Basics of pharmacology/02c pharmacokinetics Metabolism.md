refers to body's process of chemically transforming drugs, mainly in the [[liver]], to make them easier to excrete in the [[00 kidney Anatomy|kidney]] 

Typically undergo these enzymatic phases either as standalone or in succession i.e. phase 1/2 only or both phases

1. [[#Phase 1 reactions|reactions of functionalization]]
2. [[#Phase 2 reactions|reactions of conjugation]]

- drugs are converted from more fat soluble to water soluble(hydrophilic) forms
- metabolites (product) can be active/inactive(only a few drugs)

## Phase 1 reactions
involves cytochrome P450(CYP 450) enzymes which are:
- Heme-containing enzymes
- CYP 3A4 enzyme most prevalent
- non-drug compounds also broken down by CYP enzymes (e.g. CYP2E1 for alcohol)

## Phase 2 reactions
adds a molecule to the drug to increase hydrophilicity
e.g.
- glucuronidation
- sulphation
- acetylation
- methylation
