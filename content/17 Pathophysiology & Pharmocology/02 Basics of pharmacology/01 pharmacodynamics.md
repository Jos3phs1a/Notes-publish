# Principles

## Drug - Receptor interaction
Drugs are similar to enzymes, substances that induce effects on the body which are activated through drug-receptor complex via [[lock & key analogy]]

**Agonists:** main protein of drug that binds to receptor to induce effect

**Antagonists**
1. Competitive: reduce the effect of the drug by competing with agonists to 

2. Non-competitive: reduce effect of drug by ==altering receptor or the reaction==, preventing effect of drug even when agonist binds to receptor

**Allosteric activator**: increase effect of drug by altering receptor or the reaction

## Downstream signaling
![[Pasted image 20250918234724.png|center|800]]

Drug-receptor complexes trigger **intracellular signaling cascades**. These cascades modify the activity of downstream molecules through signals and eventually produce the drug’s physiological effect.

- Signals can either by ==amplifying or inhibiting ==
