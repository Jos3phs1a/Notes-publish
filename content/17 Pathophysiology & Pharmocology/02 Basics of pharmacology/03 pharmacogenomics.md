 Study of genetic factors that underlie variations in drug response differ from [[01 pharmacodynamics]] in the sense that it is a study of specific populations rather than individuals.
 
Understanding variations in genetics amongst populations → explains differences in drugs response and side effects 

Helps predict outcomes of treatment on certain populations 

## Genetic polymorphisms
the existence of two or more forms (alleles) of a particular gene within a population, where each form occurs at a frequency of at least 1%

### Example of Phase 1 
CYP 3A4/5

- ↑ rate of CYP 3A5 expression amongst asians
- cytochrome P450 enzyme CYP 3A4/5 metabolizes wide variety of drugs including tacrolimus
- some patients express CYP 3A5 genes → ↑ metabolism
- In kidney transplant, requires higher tacrolimus dose to prevent ↑ risk of organ rejection

### Example Phase 2
TMPT (Thiopurine S methayltransferase)

- Enzyme responsible for methylation of thiopurine group of drugs
- e.g. Azathioprine, useds in transplant and treatment of immunological conditions
- metabolized by TMPT into TGNs (Active metabolites)
- 3 clinical phentypes, high, intermediate, low enzymatic activity
- low level of TMPT activity → ↑ risk for toxicities including myelosuppression

### Hypersensitivity to drugs
- Presence of HLA-B 1502 for carbamazepine(anti-epileptic); HLA-B5701 for abacavir (HIV)
- Risk of steven-johnson's syndrome or Toxic Epidermal necrolysis (TEN)
