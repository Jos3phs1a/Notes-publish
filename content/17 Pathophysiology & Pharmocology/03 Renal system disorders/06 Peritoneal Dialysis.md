# Procedure


--- start-multi-column: ID_1c0o
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

- PD catheter inserted permanently into abdomen
- 2L of PD solution filled into and drained out of peritoneum or abdominal cavity, surrounded by periotoneal membrane
- membrane filters waste and fluids
- PD solution replaced with fresh one, usage depends on individual

## Benefits
- can be home based
- self directed but requires supervision
- more flexibility and patient ownership 
- gentler treatment, less food restrictions
- painless, no needles

## Modalities
--- start-multi-column: ID_6bao
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

### Continuous Ambulatory PD
- Each session: 30 mins
	- inflow: 10 mins
	- outflow: 20 mins
- 3-4x/day
- PD solution can remain in abdomen for 4-6 hrs before draining
- "cap up"(dry) at night or dwell

--- column-break ---

### Automated PD
- performed by cycler during sleep
- 8-10 hrs
- CCPD: continuous cycling PD with 1 day dwell
- NIPD: nocturnal intermittent PD with day dry

# Complications

## infective
- PD peritonis - [[Inflammation]] of [[Serosa#Peritoneum|Peritoneum]]
- Exit site infection
- Tunnel infection

## Non infective

### Metabolic
- Hyperglycemia - due to the high glucose content in PD solution
- Insulin resistance - [[Diabetes]] risk factor
- weight gain
- dyslipidemia

### Mechanical
--- start-multi-column: ID_w1f6
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

- [[Hernias]] 
	- inguinal/femoral
	- Ventral
	- umbilical
	- Paraumbilical
- Leaks
	- Peri-catheter leak with subcutaneous [[edema]]
	- Pleuro-peritoneal leak
	- Vulva/scrotal swelling
- Catheter obstruction
	- Constipation
	- fibrin
	- kink
	- omentum wrapping
	- tip migration
- Abdominal pain
	- inflow
	- outflow

- Encapsulating Peritoneal sclerosis

	- Suboptimal clearance of toxins
