## Pathophysiology

1. [[Liver#Cirrhosis|Cirrhosis]] and portal [[Hypertension]] trigger neurohormonal cascade which leads to the development of HRS. 
2. Production and release of vasodilators and cytokines e.g. nitric oxide and prostaglandins which cause splanchnic and systemic vasodilation. 
3. Systemic drop in circulating pressure triggers carotid and aortic arch baroreceptors to activate three separate compensatory mechanisms. 
	- [[Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System (RAAS)]]
	- vasopressin release
	- [[01a Anatomical divisions#1. Central Nervous System(CNS)|sympathetic nervous system]]. 
4. The progression of the cirrhosis causes the fall in [[Control of Cardiac Output|cardiac output]] and ↓ [[Total peripheral resistance|vascular resistance]] 
5. induces further renal vasoconstriction.
6. This leads to further renal hypoperfusion, worsened by renal vasoconstriction with the end point of renal failure.
