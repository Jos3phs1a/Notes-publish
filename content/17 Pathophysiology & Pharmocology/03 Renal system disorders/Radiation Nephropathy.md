# Risk factors

- Even within normal ranged of photon irradiation can cause radiation nephropathy,e.g. expose to total dose fo 23Gray, fractionated in 20 doses over 4k wks
- CKD may not occur from irradiation alone, require renal vol <30% of both kidneys
- Lower radiation doses may still cause kidney injury years after treatment
- Renal failure may not occur if only one kidney irradiated and that kidney is damaged 
	- un-irradiated kidney likely become damaged from renin-mediated hypertension due to severe unilateral renal scarring
- Total body irradiation for chemotherapy conditioning before hematopoietic stem cell transplant → can potentiate effects of ionizing radiation
- targeted radionuclide therapy used for treatment of neuroendocrine malignancies
# Clinical presentation

typically manifest late into treatment, months to years

| Type                          | latent period |

| Chronic radiation nephropathy | >18 months    |
