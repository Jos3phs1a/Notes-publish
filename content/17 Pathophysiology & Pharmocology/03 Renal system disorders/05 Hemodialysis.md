# Access

## Arteriovenous Fistula
surgical access procedure to connect an artery and a vein directly.
- may need to wait months to allow AV fistula to heal before ready for hemodialysis treatments, linking back to [[03 end stage kidney disease#Unplanned initiation of RRT|timing of treatment]] 
- Safest for long term usage 
- doesn’t require artificial material
- provide greater blood flow → faster treatment

## Arteriovenous Graft
synthetic tube to connect an artery to a vein
- for patients who need to start dialysis more urgently or for those with small or thin veins
- less blood flow → slower treatment
- need synthetic tubing, may need medicine e.g. clopidogrel to avoid body [[00 immune system|immune system]] response
- only lasts 2-3 years before requiring replacement

## Vascular Catheters
flexible tube placed through the skin to channel blood to and away from dialysis machine

**Types**
- Tunneled: goes under the skin , separate exit site
- Non-tunneled: does not go under the skin, insert directly into vein

## Complications


--- start-multi-column: ID_ymk5
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

### Infectious
especially [[#Vascular Catheters]] run high risk of infections esp NTDC including;
- blood stream infection
- exit site/tunnel track infection
- Central vein stenosis
- Mural thrombus
- Occlusion due to
	- blood clot
	- fibrin sheath
	- kink

--- column-break ---

### Non infectious
- Primary failure
- Stenosis
- Thrombosis
- Steal syndrome
- high outflow cardiac failure
