# Classification

- Complete failure of [[00 kidney Anatomy|kidneys]] 
- [[glomerular filtration rate|eGFR]] <15 ml/min

# Management

2 main routes

- Conservative → Palliative 
- Renal replacement → [[07 kidney transplant]]/[[05 Hemodialysis]]/[[06 Peritoneal Dialysis]]

## Relevance of timing 
### Unplanned initiation of RRT
- **Higher risk of infections**: esp catheters during emergencies → bloodstream infections, [[sepsis]], and catheter-related complications.
- **Increased morbidity**: Patients tend to have longer hospital stays, worse outcomes, and higher mortality when RRT is initiated emergently.

### Allows timely dialysis access placement
accesses are safer, more durable, and associated with better patient outcomes compared to temporary central catheters
- AVF: surgically inserted has lowest complication rates
- AVG/PD catheter: for when AVF not available

### Allows transplantation referral 
- Undergo **preemptive renal transplant** (receiving a kidney before dialysis is ever needed).
- This approach is associated with **better graft survival, longer patient survival, and improved quality of life** compared to starting dialysis first.
