### ASA
e.g. Asperin, Acetylsalicylic acid

- Irreversibly inhibits COX-1 & COX-2
- Weakly selective to COX-1
- Decrease [[Inflammatory mediator#^6d6f59|Prostaglandin]] production

**Adverse effects:**
- never use for children with viral infections as ASA + viral infection contribute to development of Reye syndrome, ==fata

### Acetaminophen
e.g. Panadol, Paracetamol
Exact mechanism unknown

- selective COX-2 Inhibitor property
### NSAIDs
e.g. Ibuprofen (Advil or Motrin), piroxicam (Feldene), or diclofenac sodium (Arthrotec), celecoxib (Celebrex)

- Competitive inhibitors of COX-1 & COX-2
- ibuprofen, naproxen weakly selective to COX-1
- Decrease [[Inflammatory mediator#^6d6f59|Prostaglandin]] production

**Adverse effects:**
- similar to [[#ASA]] but less severe
### Glucocorticoids
| Drug                              | Features/potency                                                                                     |
| --------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Cortisone/Hydrocortisone(active)  | short acting, 1/2 life: 6-12hrs<br>cold drug, liver process to activate                              |
| Predinisone/ Prednisolone(active) | Intermediate acting, 1/2 life: 12-36 hrs<br>processed in liver<br>5x more potent than hydrocortisone |
| Dexamethesone                     | Long acting, 1/2 life: 36-72hrs<br>25x more potent than hydrocortisone                               |
| Betamethasone                     | Long acting, 1/2 life: 36-72hrs<br>used to manage eczema and autoimmune disorders                    |
**Mechanism:**
- reduce [[Inflammation#Vascular event|Vascular event]] via ↓ capillary permeability 
- reduce [[White blood cells]] and mast cells at injury site, decrease [[Inflammatory mediator#^6d6f59|Prostaglandin]] production
- block immune response

**Adverse effects:**
- increased risk of infection due to decrease immune response
- catabolic effect (↑ tissue breakdown), osteoporosis, [[Types of cellular adaptation#muscular conditions Atrophy Atrophy|Atrophy]] 
- delayed healing
- delayed growth in children
- sodium, water retention [[edema]], [[Hypertension]]
- ↑ gluconeogenesis, ↑ blood sugar
