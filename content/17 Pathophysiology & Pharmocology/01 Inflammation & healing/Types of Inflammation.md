## Acute
- sudden onset
- short duration
**features**
- Edema
- Migration of leukocytes

#### Causes
Trauma events e.g. lacerations, falls, bruises etc causing some form of tissue damage either internal or external or both

--- column-break ---

## Chronic
- acute episode of inflammation with continued tissue destruction
**features**
- less swelling, exudate
- presence of more lymphocytes ,macrophages, fibroblasts
- more severe/on going tissue destruction
- more collagen & fibrous scar tissue

#### Causes
- Persistent infection e.g. myobacterium TB
- Persistent indigestible material
	- endogenous e.g. necrotic bone, calcium, uric acid
	- Exogenous e.g. asbestos, silica
- Immune mediated reactions
	- [[Immune Diseases#Autoimmune|Autoimmune]] reactions
	- Organ transplant rejection
	- unregulated immune response e.g. inflammatory bowel, ulcerative colitis
	- hypersensitivity reactions e.g. bronchial asthma
- Following [[#acute|Acute inflammation]] where cause persist/recurring episodes e.g. acute pancreatitis, acute cholecystitis
