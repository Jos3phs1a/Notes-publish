## History
- previous RAI/thyroidectomy
- family history

## Examination
- weight
- thyroidectomy signs
- Bradychardia
- Goiter
- Dry skin

## Investigation
- low FT4, TSH levels
- Thyroid peroxidase antibody (TPO Ab)
	presence could be sign of lymphocytic infiltration in [[Thyroid gland]] → autoimmune activity

--- end-multi-column


## Effects 
- atherosclerosis
- pericardial effusion
- cognitive impairment
- muscle cramps
- menstrual irregularities
etc.
## Treatment
- hormone replacement therapy
- Levothyroxine(synthetic T4) (Synthroid, Levo-T) medication that replaces thyroid hormones
	- Primarily T4 as is slow acting, longer duration + less drastic change compared to T3
	- ↓ CVD risk 
	- T3 therapy considered rarely


# Myxedema Coma

severe manifestation of [[#Hypothyroidism]]

- multi system dysfunction, poor thermoregulatory, cardiac, neurologic activity

## Diagnosis
symptoms generally non specific but can follow diagnostic criteria to assess possible presence of condition
![[Pasted image 20251019220943.png|center|600]]

## Treatment
**Acute stabilization**
- stabilize ABCs

**Thyroid specific therapy**
- intravenous glucocorticoids empirically cover adrenal insufficiency: prevent adrenal crisis
- intravenous levothyroxine: due to ==impaired GIT absorption== (increase [[02a pharmacokinetics Absorption#Bioavailability|Bioavailability]])
	- side effects: risk of ==CVD in elderly==
- switch to oral once patient is alert
- Supportive treatment + monitoring e.g. passive rewarming, fluid management
