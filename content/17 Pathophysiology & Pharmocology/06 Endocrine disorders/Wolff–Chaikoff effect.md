## Definition

a **temporary reduction in thyroid hormone synthesis** that occurs when there is a **sudden large increase in iodine levels** in the body.

In simple terms — when the thyroid gland is exposed to **too much iodine**, it **“shuts down” temporarily** to prevent overproduction of thyroid hormones (**T₃ and T₄**).

---

## **Mechanism**

1. **Excess iodine intake** → from diet, contrast media, or medications (like amiodarone).
2. **High intrathyroidal iodine concentration** inhibits the enzyme **thyroid peroxidase (TPO)**.
3. This stops:
    - **Iodide organification** (iodine binding to thyroglobulin), and    
    - **Coupling reactions** (formation of T₃ and T₄).
4. As a result → **thyroid hormone synthesis decreases** for several days.
    
This is a **protective mechanism** — it prevents the thyroid from producing too much hormone when exposed to an iodine overload.
