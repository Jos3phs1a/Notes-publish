# Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)

body breaks down fat at rate too fast, [[Liver]] processes fat into fuel called ketones, causes blood to become acidic.
- usually [[Diabetes#Type 1|T1DM]] more prone 
- develops quick: ==hours - days==
- mortality low <1%
## Symptoms
- excessive thirst 100ml/kg
- frequent urination
- nausea vomiting
- abdominal pain
- weakness/fatigue
- shortness of breath
- fruity scented breath
- confusion

## Diagnosis

- glucose > 14mmol/L
- Urine ketones 3/4+, blood ketones >1
- HCO$_3$ < 18 mmol/L pH <7.30

# Hyperglycemic Hyperosmolar Syndrome (HHS)

when extremely high glucose blood levels cause dehydration and ↑ plasma osmolarity
- usually seen in [[Diabetes#Type 2|T2DM]]
- develops slower: ==Days - weeks==
- High mortality rate: 20%
## Symptoms
- excessive thirst
- frequent urination more severe 100-220ml/kg
- capillary glucose  "Hi" > 33.3mmol/L
- drowsiness, confusion
- dry mouth
- fever 
- hallucination

## Diagnosis
- Blood glucose > 33.3mmol/L
- Effective serum osmolality > 320mOsm/kg
- Altered sensorium - confusion, ↓ awareness

# Pathogenesis


absolute insulin deficiency → [[#Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)|DKA]] → lack of insulin secretion

relative insulin deficiency → [[#Hyperglycemic Hyperosmolar Syndrome (HHS)|HSS]] → insulin resistance too high but serum insulin is ok

## Investigations
**to confirm diagnosis**
- serum glucose
- arterial blood gas
- urine + blood ketones
**investigate precipitating factors**
- blood + urine cultures
- [[Electrocardiogram|ECG]] 
- chest Xray
- Lipids, amylase analysis
**Monitoring**
- electrolytes
- venous pH

## Management
- Secure Airway, Breathing, Circulation (ABCs)
- Aggressive hydration with isotonic fluid, improve circulating vol
- clear blood and urine of ketones
- correct electrolyte imbalance
- identify and treat precipitating event
