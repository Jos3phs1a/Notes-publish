# Thyroiditis

inflammation of thyroid gland that damage thyroid producing cells

## causes:
- autoimmune disease (Hashimoto thyroiditis, Graves disease, postpartum thyroiditis or painless sporadic thyroiditis), 
- infection (painful subacute thyroiditis or suppurative thyroiditis), 
- drugs (amiodarone, lithium, interferons and interleukin-2; and checkpoint inhibitors) 
- fibrosis (Riedel thyroiditis)

## Effects
Depending on stage and causes:
- [[#Hyperthyroidism]]— damaged thyroid cells leak hormones.
- Followed by [[#Hypothyroidism]] — gland becomes depleted.
- And sometimes return to **normal thyroid function (euthyroid)** after recovery

Treatment changes depending on [[#Effects|stage/phase of condition]]:
- Thioamides ineffective as intervention as problem is with secretion, not production of hormones

During **Thyrotoxic phase**, management includes:
- symptom relief esp pain
- anti-inflammatory agents
- beta-blockers
- monitor development
