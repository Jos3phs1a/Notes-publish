# Definition

- Symptomatic - symptoms + CBG <4.0mmol/L
- Asymptomatic - no symptoms but low CBG < 4.0mmol/L
- Severe - ==acute event== requiring assistance of another person to actively administer treatment
- Relative - symptoms but CBG > 3.9mmol/L

# Effects

- on the brain
	Decline in IQ
	MRI shows cortical atrophy in 50% of severe hypoglycemia

- Induced unawareness
	series of body counter regulatory mechanisms
	1. lower uptake of glucose in tissues → ↑ hepatic glucose production 
	2. ↓ insulin production, ↑ glucagon production
	3. Increase adrenaline secretion → causing [[#Autonomic]] symptoms
	4. Brain adapts until recurrent episodes require even lower amount of glucose for symptoms to present → life threatening **neuroglycopenia**

- sudden death
	Possible alterations in cardiac ventricular repolarisation
	prothrombotic state leading to myocardial infarction
	QT interval abnormalities

# Treatment

- stabilize ABCs
- check consciousness intervention varies based on alertness
	- alert → fast acting glucose drink
	- drowsy/vomiting → IV D50% bolus 40mls
	- Monitoring
	- capillary glucose 30 min after correction, 2-4 hrs after
	- Find cause
