 autoimmune disease that causes your thyroid to make too much thyroid hormone. It happens because your body makes antibodies to your thyroid gland.

## Etiology

Like all autoimmune diseases, occurs more commonly in patients with positive family history. 
more common in monozygotic twins than in dizygotic twins.
precipitated by environmental factors like stress, smoking, infection, iodine exposure, and postpartum, highly active antiretroviral therapy (HAART) due to immune reconstitution


## Pathophysiology

caused by thyroid stimulating immunoglobulin

Thyroid stimulating immunoglobulin binds with thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) receptor on thyroid cell membrane and stimulates action of thyroid-stimulating hormone. 
It stimulates both, thyroid hormone synthesis and thyroid gland growth, causing hyperthyroidism and thyromegaly

### Graves' orbitopathy (ophthalmopathy) 
caused by inflammation, cellular proliferation and increased growth of extraocular muscles and retro-orbital connective and adipose tissues due to the actions of thyroid stimulating antibodies and cytokines released by cytotoxic T lymphocytes
