# Hyperthyroidism

## Effects
due to widespread [[Thyroid hormone#Functions|function of thyroid hormone]], can have large effect on organ systems
including but not limited to:
- atrial fib due to ↑ tissue thermal genesis, ↑ BMR, ↓ serum cholesterol lvls + vascular restrictions
- heart failure due to ↑ CO + HR
- Osteoporosis due to ↑ bone resorption
- Neuropsychiatric effects
- Diarrhea & abdominal cramps
- menstrual irregularities, impaired fertility

## Causes
- [[Graves disease]] 
- Toxic adenoma → benign tumor
- Toxic multinodular goiter → gland producing thyroid hormone
- subacute thyroiditis → inflammatory disorder 
- Medication induced thyrotoxicosis

## Treatment
--- start-multi-column: ID_1wcs
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```


**Oral Thioamide** medication
- inhibit peroxidase enzyme preventing formation of thyroid hormones
- start at high dose, then decrease overtime
- remission rate: 40%, meaning less than half of patients  ==experience a significant reduction or complete disappearance of their signs and symptoms after treatment==. → relapse can **consider surgery**

#### Side effects of thioamides
- rashes
- agranulocytosis - having severely low levels of [[White blood cells]] called neutrophils, believed to be **immune-mediated destruction of bone marrow precursors** or **direct toxic suppression** of granulocyte production.
- hepatotoxicity - propylthiouracil(PTU) causes acute liver injury, mechanism unknown, likely due to immunological reaction to a metabolic product of its metabolism
- vasculitis

# # Thyroid storm

Life threatening endocrine emergency characterized by exaggerated symptoms of [[#Hyperthyroidism]] and multiorgan decompensation
mortality rate: 1.2%-25%, requires aggressive intervention

## diagnosis
using burch and wartofsky scoring system for thyrotoxicity 

![[Pasted image 20251019205314.png|center]]


## Treatment

**Acute stabilization**
- secure ABCs
**Thyroid specific therapy**
- decrease thyroid hormone synthesis → [[#Side effects of thioamides|thioamides]]
- prevent thyroid hormone release → Lugol's iodine via [[Wolff–Chaikoff effect]] 
- decrease peripheral action of thyroid hormones → corticosteroids/beta blockers, block conversion of T4 to T3 which is more potent in effects on body 
**Treat precipitants** e.g : 
- Abrupt discontinuation of antithyroid medicine
- Thyroid surgery
- Non-thyroid surgery
- Trauma
- Acute illnesses like infections, including COVID-19, diabetic ketoacidosis, acute myocardial infarction, cardiovascular accident, cardiac failure, and drug reactions
etc
