# Classifications
## Type 1
caused by autoimmune [[Pancreas#^381c60|B-cell]] destruction, usually leading to absolute insulin deficiency

## Type 2
Interplay of mechanisms, typically involving:
1. **progressive** loss if [[Pancreas#^381c60|B-cell]] insulin secretion frequently 
2. background of ==insulin resistance==, typically associated with [[Obesity]]
3. ↑ blood sugar due to: bloodstream unable to recognize insulin produced in [[Pancreas]], glucose not taken in by muscles → perceived "low" glucose → triggers ==glycogen conversion== 
also shown correlation to [[metabolic inflexibility & mitochondrial dysfunction]]
effects can be tackled via [[Exercise impact on Diabetes|exercise]]

## Gestational diabetes mellitus
diabetes during 2nd or 3rd tri of pregnancy, not clearly overt diabetes prior to gestation


# Diagnosis

1. **Fasting plasma glucose:** blood test measures blood sugar level after fasting for ==at least 8 hours==, typically overnight
	impaired fasting glucose(IFG): 6.1-7.0mmol/L
	DM: > 7.0mmol/L
2. **[[#Oral Glucose Tolerance Test]]:** ingest 75g glucose, test body's processing ability
	Impaired glucose tolerance(IGT) & **[[#Gestational diabetes mellitus]]**: 7.8-11.1 mmol/L
	DM: >11.1 mmol/L
3. **Random plasma glucose test**:  measures blood sugar level at any time of day, without requiring fasting beforehand
	DM: > 11.1 mmol/L
4. **[[Assessment of diabetic patients#HbA1c|HbA1c]](glycated hemoglobin)**:  measures amount of glucose (sugar) attached to hemoglobin
	DM: > 6.5%
