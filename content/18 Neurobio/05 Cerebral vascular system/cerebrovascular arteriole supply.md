# Anatomy

located beneath the [[Meninges#Pia mater|pia mater]] along with [[cerebrovascular venous supply]]

Normal blood flow through the brain of the adult person averages 50 to 65 ml/100 g of brain tissue/min.

2 main supply routes
- **anterior** → two internal carotid arteries
- **posterior** → two vertebral arteries merging into basilar arteries

### Circle of willis
located in ventral(under) side of the brain, surrounding [[04a Visual Field#Optic chiasm|optic chiasm]] and **pituitary stalk**.
 formed by 
 - 2 [[#Anterior cerebral arteries]]
 - 1 anterior communicating artery
 - 2 internal carotid arteries (connects to post comm but not large coverage of brain itself)
 - 2 posterior communicating artery
 - 2 posterior cerebral artery
## Supplies

**Most important concept**: smaller branches have **very limited collateral (alternative) blood flow**, ==compensation v limited== so most likely result in ==permanent damage== during stroke.
### Anterior cerebral arteries
Lower limb motor & sensory
- Medial frontal lobe
- Medial parietal lobe
- Superior motor and sensory areas (lower limb representation)

#### branches
1. **Recurrent artery of Heubner**
    - **caudate nucleus** and **internal capsule** (deep structures).
2. **Orbitofrontal artery**
    -  **orbital surface of the frontal lobe**.
3. **Frontopolar artery**
    - **frontal pole** (front tip of the frontal lobe).
4. **Callosomarginal artery**
    - Runs along **cingulate gyrus**, supplies **superior frontal gyrus** & **paracentral lobule**.
5. **Pericallosal artery**
    - Continuation of the ACA; runs over the **corpus callosum** and supplies **medial frontal and parietal lobes**.
### Middle cerebral artery
largest supply of the brain
**face and upper limb representation** on the motor and sensory homunculi.
- **Frontal lobe (lateral part)** → motor areas for face and upper limb.
- **Parietal lobe (lateral part)** → sensory areas for face and upper limb.
- **Temporal lobe (superior part)** → primary auditory cortex.
- **Broca’s area (speech production)** — in the dominant hemisphere.
- **Wernicke’s area (speech comprehension)** — in the dominant hemisphere.
- **Internal capsule (via deep branches)** — important for motor fibers descending to the body.

#### branches
- cortical (superficial)
	- Superior: - lateral frontal lobe, including **Broca’s area**. 
	- Inferior: lateral temporal and parietal lobes, including **Wernicke’s area**.
- central perforating (deep)
	- Lenticulostriate arteries: internal capsule, nucleus, putamen
### Posterior cerebral artery
mostly vision and visual association areas
- **Occipital lobe:** → **Primary visual cortex (area 17)** — damage causes **contralateral homonymous hemianopia**.
- **Inferior temporal lobe:** → **Visual association areas** involved in object and face recognition
- **Thalamus and midbrain:** via deep branches.

#### branches
 - cortical (superficial)
	 - calcarine: primary visual cortex along calcarine fissure
	 - parieto-occipital
	 - posterior temporal branches: inferior temporal gyrus and visual association areas
 - central perforating (deep)
	 - Thalamoperforating: thalamus & midbrain
	 - Thalamogeniculate: lateral geniculate body
