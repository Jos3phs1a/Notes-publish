## Neurogenic

[[02b Autonomic nervous systems|ANS]] and ==neurochemical controls== have ==little impact== on perfusion
to maintain constant perfusion without drastic changes
- strong influence, systemic changes (like stress, exercise, or blood pressure spikes) could cause large swings in cerebral blood flow — which would be dangerous.

# impairments

people who have chronic [[Hypertension]] → hypertrophic remodeling of [[cerebrovascular arteriole supply|cerebral blood vessels]] →  autoregulatory curve shifted higher 
- partially protects brain from damaging effects of high blood pressure. 
- but makes brain vulnerable to severe ischemia if BP is drops fast below the range of autoregulation. 

**Impairment of autoregulation** makes CBF much ==more dependent on arterial pressure.== 
For example, 
- in [[preeclampsia]]
	pressure-dependent increases in cerebral blood flow, disruption of the vascular endothelium, edema, and seizures in some cases. 
- In old age, atherosclerosis and various brain disorders
	increasing the risk for blood pressure–dependent injury of the brain.
