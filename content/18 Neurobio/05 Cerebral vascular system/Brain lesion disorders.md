post [[stroke]] complications

location of lesion affects types of disorder experienced

## Dysarthria
motor disorders where muscles used for speech become weak/ difficult to control
**structures involved:** either hemisphere/brainstem → tongue movement muscles (cranial nerve XII)
**symptoms:** slowed/slurred speech 
## Aphasia
Left hemisphere region lesion → Broca or Wernicke area affected where they are located

### Broca
- **Speech:** Slow, effortful, and non-fluent
- **Comprehension:** Relatively preserved
- **Repetition:** Impaired
- **Example:** “Want… water…” (meaningful but missing grammar)

--- column-break ---

### Wernicke
- **Speech:** Fluent but nonsensical or irrelevant
- **Comprehension:** Poor
- **Repetition:** Impaired
- **Example:** “Yes, the table, it’s blue and I’m running.”

--- end-multi-column

## Neglect syndrome
==attention & spatial awareness== disorder
person fails to attend to one side of space - typically opposite to brain lesion

commonly occurs in right inferior parietal lobe lesion than left → left neglect more common

- **right parietal lobe** attends to **both left and right visual fields**.
- **left parietal lobe**, mainly attends to the **right visual field**. → right can compensate during lesion
## Gaze preference
to keep eyes looking forward, brain sends equal input 

if one side has stronger input than other, gaze pushes to opposite side
ie right sided lesion → left overpower → push eyes right → right gaze preference
