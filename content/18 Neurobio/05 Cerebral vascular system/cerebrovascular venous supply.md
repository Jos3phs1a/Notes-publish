# Anatomy

located beneath the [[Meninges#Pia mater|pia mater]] along with [[cerebrovascular arteriole supply]]

small branches → main veins → sinuses → internal jugular veins
### Superficial System
The superficial system of veins is largely responsible for draining the cerebral cortex:
- **Superior cerebral veins**: Drain ==superior surface==, carries blood to ==superior sagittal sinus.==
- **Superficial middle cerebral vein**: Drains ==lateral surface== of each hemisphere, carries blood to ==cavernous or sphenopalatine sinuses.==
- **Inferior cerebral veins**: Drain ==inferior aspect== of each cerebral hemisphere, carries blood to ==cavernous and transverse sinuses.==
- **Superior anastamotic vein** (Trolard): Connects ==superficial middle cerebral vein== to ==superior sagittal sinus.==
- **Inferior anastamotic vein** (Labbé): Connects ==superficial middle cerebral vein== to ==transverse sinus.==

### Deep System

- **Subependymal veins** – numerous subependymal veins, receive blood from ==medullary veins== and carry to the ==dural venous sinuses== 
- **Great cerebral vein** (vein of Galen) is formed by union of two deep veins, and drains into the ==straight sinus==.
- **Medullary veins**: Originate 1-2cm below the cortical grey matter, and drain into subependymal veins. Drain the ==deep areas== of brain

### Sinus
Major sinuses:

- **Superior sagittal sinus**
- **Inferior sagittal sinus**
- **Straight sinus**
- **Transverse sinuses**
- **Sigmoid sinuses** (→ become **internal jugular veins**)
- **Cavernous sinuses** (around the pituitary gland)
