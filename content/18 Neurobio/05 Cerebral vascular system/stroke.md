**Definition:**
condition in which blood clot or blood vessel rupture interrupts blood flow to brain.
[[01ba Anatomic Divisions of Brain|Brain]] starved of metabolites resulting in cell and tissue damage

### Non-modifiable
- Age
	can occur in children and adults, but risk of stroke doubles for each decade between the ages of 55 and 85
- Gender 
	Men have higher risk for stroke, but more women die from stroke
- Race 
	African American>white>Asian 
	Large-artery atherosclerotic infarctions more prevalent in Indians (25.0%), whereas lacunar infarctions occur more frequently in Chinese (51.8%)
- Family history

--- column-break ---

### Modifiable
- hypertension: most potent risk factor for stroke.  
-  Cigarette smoking.  
	causes approx 2x increase for ischemic stroke and  4x in risk of hemorrhagic stroke.  
- [[Heart Conditions & Disorders|Heart disease]]
	Common heart disorders can result in blood clots.  
-  Warning signs or history of TIA or stroke  
	TIA increases risk of stroke
- Diabetes  
	In terms of stroke and cardiovascular disease, having diabetes is the equivalent of aging 15 years.  
- [[Cholesterol imbalance.  
	Excess [[lipoproteins#Low-density lipoproteins (LDLs)|LDLs]] can cause cholesterol build up in blood vessels, leading to atherosclerosis  
- Physical inactivity and obesity  
	[[Obesity]] & inactivity associated with [[Hypertension]], [[Diabetes]], and [[Heart Conditions & Disorders|heart disease]]

# Classification

## Ischemic
### Symptoms
- aphasia: trouble with speech and understanding
- Paralysis/numbness: in face or limbs, especially towards one side
- Poor vision: sudden blurry of blackened vision in one/both eyes([[06 Eye disorders#Diplopia|diplopia]])
- Headache: sudden, severer headache accompanied by vomiting, fizziness or altered consciousness
- Sudden poor gait: stumbling, fizziness, loss of balance/coordination

### Thrombotic 
clot form in artery that is already narrow
fat, cholesterol, plaque can increase risk factor

#### large-vessel 
blockage is in one of brain's larger supplying arteries eg [[cerebrovascular arteriole supply#Middle cerebral artery|Mid cerebral artery]] or carotid
#### Small-vessel
involves one (or more) of the brain's smaller yet deeper, penetrating arteries
aka **lacunar stroke**(<1.5cm) → can be viewed under CT/MRI


--- column-break ---

### Embolic
clot break off and travel to other part of body of brain


--- end-multi-column

### Treatment
golden zone: 4.5 hours
Pharmacological: tissue plasminogen activator drug(tPA) drug to dissolve clot 
Surgical: thrombectomy/embolectomy → only for larger vessels & longer time window

## Hemorrhagic
when blood vessel in brain becomes weak and bursts → blood leak into brain (**hematoma**)
- seizures
- dilatation of [[01a eye anatomy#Pupil]]
### Symptoms
similar to that of [[#symptoms|symptoms of ischemic stroke]] due to ↑ ICP compressing brain tissue preventing function

### Risk factors
- [[Hypertension]] → ↑ BP cause tiny arteries to burst inside brain
- Blood tinner therapy
- AVM: tangle of blood vessels with no capillaries between
- Aneurysm: bulge or weakening of arterial wall
- Head trauma: fractures to skull and penetrating wounds causing bleed
- Bleeding disorders
- Tumors
- Amyloid angiopathy: degenerative disease
- Drug usage: can cause ICH
- idiopathic

### Treatment
- surgical to relieve ICP and swelling 
## Transient Ischemic attack (TIA)
- brief interruption of blood flow to part of the brain that causes temporary stroke like symptoms
- 'mini stroke' that occurs when a blood clot blocks an artery for a short time
- commonly caused by tiny clots that temporarily occlude portion of brain
