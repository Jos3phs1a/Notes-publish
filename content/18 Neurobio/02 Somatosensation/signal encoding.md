The body is able to tell apart different ==modalities== (types) of sensations through encoding, allowing for specificity to communicate type of sensation felt

# Labeled line principle
- labeled lines = primary afferents and receptors
- 1 nerve tract can only terminate at specific point in [[01a Anatomical divisions#1. Central Nervous System(CNS)|CNS]]
- sensation felt determined by where signal leads

# Properties

These properties allow signal encoding, allowing the body to discern different sensations.

## Receptive field
Definition: Region that when stimulated produce change in activity of neuron 
aka ==territory covered== by a single neuron

![[Pasted image 20250911020208.png]]

Receptive fields ==tend to overlap==, causing multiple signals to be sent, [[lateral inhibition]] ==prevents loss of acuity== of signal.

![[Pasted image 20250908023530.png|center]]
## Duration
intrinsic property of neurons affect ability to monitor active time of stimulus

**slow adapting:**
- active for duration of stimulus
- good in monitoring stimulation across whole durations

**Fast adapting:**
- active only during change in stimulus
- good in detecting sudden changes
