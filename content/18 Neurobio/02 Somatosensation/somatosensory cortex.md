#### Brodmann’s area 3b
aka ==primary somatosensory cortex (S1)==, lies on the postcentral gyrus (right behind the central sulcus)
is the main area as:
1. it receives dense inputs from Ventral Posterior nucleus of the [[01ba Anatomic Divisions of Brain#Thalamus|thalamus]]
2. its neurons are very responsive to somatosensory stimuli (but not to other sensory stimuli)
3. lesions here impair somatic sensation 
4. when electrically stimulated, it evokes somatic sensory experiences

## other divisions
#### Area 3a 
receives dense input from thalamus; however, this region is concerned with the sense of body position rather than touch. 

#### Area 1 & 2
Areas 1 and 2 receive dense inputs from area 3b. 
- The projection from 3b to area 1 sends ==mainly texture information==, area 2 emphasizes ==size and shape==. 
- Small lesions in area 1 or 2 produce predictable deficiencies in discrimination of texture, size, and shape.
