# nociceptive processing 

Laminae **I,II,V** are key processing zones 
as pain signals first pass through these laminae before entering [[02a Anterolateral pathways]] 


--- start-multi-column: ID_60rv
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

[[Nociceptors]] distribution across laminae I, II, and V:

1. **Lamina I**: Major termination zone for ==nociceptive Aδ and C fibers==, including peptidergic C fibers and some Aδ nociceptors.
    
2. **Lamina II**: ==Nociceptive & thermoreceptive Aδ and C fibers== also innervate much of lamina II, except for most ventral part. Non-peptidergic C fibers terminate in the central part of lamina II, peptidergic fibers primarily target lamina IIo.
    
3. **Lamina V**: Some ==Aδ nociceptors== extend their branches, contribute to processing of nociceptive information in deeper laminae.
    

This distribution reflects the functional organization of the dorsal horn, where nociceptive inputs are processed and relayed to projection neurons for transmission to the brain.
