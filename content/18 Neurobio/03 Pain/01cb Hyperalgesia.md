# Mechanisms
## Primary 
- occurs at the site of injury, reflection of pain sensitization 
- result of reduced activation threshold and increased responsiveness of [[Nociceptors]]
## Secondary
- occurs in an area adjacent to or remote from the site of injury
### Central factors
central sensitization, [[01a Anatomical divisions#1. Central Nervous System(CNS)|CNS]] becomes over sensitize to stimuli
- **Spinal Cord Changes (Dorsal Horn Neurons)**
	[[02a Dorsal column medial lemniscal pathway#2nd order neuron|2nd order neurons]] become **sensitised** — they respond more strongly and to a wider range of inputs than before.
	
- **Altered Descending Modulation**
	brain usually has systems to **dampen pain** (descending inhibition).
	In central sensitisation, inhibitory systems are weakened, and excitatory influences ↑
	This loss of “brakes” lets pain signals amplify instead of being controlled.
	
- **Brainstem and Thalamus Activation**
	become more excitable.
	enhances the perception of pain in areas not directly injured.
### Peripheral factors
[[Inflammation]] and release of [[Inflammatory mediator]] esp prostaglandins, bradykinins and substance P → ↑ sensitivity of [[Nociceptors]]
