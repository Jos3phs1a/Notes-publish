# Classification

1. **Dynamic** **mechanical**: object moving across the skin (stroking or brushing); mediated by A-beta fibres
2. **Thermal**: mild temperature changes; mediated by A-delta and C-fibres
3. **Tactile** (or static): gentle touch or pressure; mediated by A-delta nociceptive fibre

# Mechanism

- **Sensitisation of dorsal horn cells:** Increased excitability of second order neurons in the spinal cord
- **Sensitisation of mechanoreceptors:** Low threshold A-beta fibres become sensitised. These fibres are usually not [nociceptive](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Nociception "Nociception") (rather mechanoreceptors). A-beta fibres communicate with and activate nociceptors (A-delta fibres) through various mechanisms (phenotypic switching, sprouting and opening of postsynaptic excitatory pathways), resulting in pain when non-painful mechanical stimuli is applied. As a result there is a loss of segregation of touch and pain. 
- **[Central inhibition](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Pain_Descending_Pathways "Pain Descending Pathways") errors:** A change in the balance of [descending inhibitory] and facilitating pathways from the brain to the spinal cord can result in central sensitisation Mental state can influence the perception of allodynia by altering the inhibition of nociceptive input.
