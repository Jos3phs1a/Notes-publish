# Charge distribution

ECF and ICF contain ion, containing different amt of charge

==Outside cell==: High Na$^+$ , low K$^+$
Inside cell: Low Na$^+$, high K$^+$

Creates gradient → movement of ions across membrane

Movement of K$^+$ and Na$^+$ ions cause change in voltage, ==membrane more permeable for K$^+$==, so resting potential is closer to K$^+$ at ==**-70mv**==

# Mechanism

Stimulus at [[01d Neurons|Axon Hillock]]  causes depolarisation to threshold

![[Pasted image 20250901134802.png|center]]

1. **At threshold -50mv:**
	Na channels open(activated)
	==Na$^+$ rush into ICF==, increase voltage across membrane
	 ^70762c
2. **At peak:**
	Na Channel: Inactivation gate close, ==Na$^+$ ion stop flowing==
	K Channel Activate gate open, ==K$^+$ ion rush out of ICF==, [[04a Neural Communication#^76aff6|repolarisation]]

3. **At resting:**
	==Inactivate gate open==, activate gate closed
	Further K$^+$ ion leaving cause [[04a Neural Communication#^76aff6|hyperpolarisation]] below resting
	K gate close, voltage ==return to resting potential==
