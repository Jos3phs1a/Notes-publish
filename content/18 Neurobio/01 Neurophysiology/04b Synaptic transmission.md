# Structure

#### Definition
Junction between [[01d Neurons]]
#### Types
1. **Electrical:**
	Neurons connected directly via gap junctions eg cardiac muscle

2. **Chemical**(most common)
	Messages sent via chemicals across space between 2 neurons

#### Structure:
- Presynaptic Neuron/terminal:
	area where neurotransmitters(NTs) are stored and APs are received
- Synaptic Vesicles: 
	bundles of NTs chemicals are stored and transported
- Synaptic cleft: 
	aka synaptic gap, space between axon and dendrite of neurons
- Post synaptic neuron/ terminal: 
	where receptors of dendrites receive NTs and signal transmission begin

# Mechanism

1. Axon terminal receives AP, **depolarizes**
2. Voltage-gated Ca$^{2+}$ channel opens, Ca$^{2+}$ inflow
3. Vesicles flow out of terminal, release NTs into synaptic cleft
4. NTs diffuse to receptors, receptors generate cause voltage gated Na$^{+}$and K$^{+}$ to react, generate AP
5. NTs broken down sent back to synaptic cleft, reabsorbed by axon, recycled in mitochondria

# EPSP/IPSP

| EPSP                             | IPSP                             |
| -------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Depolarization                   | Hyperpolarization                |
| Increase chance of AP generation | Decrease chance of AP generation |
Result of 2 factors are based on:
- [[05 Type of Neurotransmitters]]
- Type of Receptors receiving the NT

Some NTs can have either EPSP or IPSP based on which receptor they are detected by
