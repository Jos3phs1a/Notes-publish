## Regions

### [[Gray vs White matter#^be7c9d|Gray matter]] 

is divided into **horns**:
Each horn contains layers called [[laminae]] that perform functions
- **Dorsal horn (posterior)** — receives **sensory (afferent)** input 
- **Ventral horn (anterior)** — contains **motor (efferent)** neurons that send signals to muscles
- **Lateral horn** — present in thoracic and upper lumbar regions, contains **autonomic** neurons

--- column-break ---

### [[Gray vs White matter#^be7c9d|White matter]]
divided into **columns:**

- Dorsal (posterior) column: **ascending sensory tract** e.g. [[02a Dorsal column medial lemniscal pathway]] for ==fine touch, vibration, proprioception==
- Lateral column: both ascending and descending tracts, for ==Pain, temperature, motor control==
- Ventral (anterior) column: **descending tracts** for ==voluntary motor pathways==


--- end-multi-column

### Variations at segment levels

--- start-multi-column: ID_3y84
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: Right
```

Each segment of the spinal cord is different
- [[Gray vs White matter#^be7c9d|White matter]] thickest at cervical levels,
ascending fibers already entered the cord and descending fibers not yet terminated on targets
- sacral cord mostly [[Gray vs White matter#^be7c9d|Gray matter]] 
- 2 enlargements:
	- cervical  → cervical plexus
	- lumbosacral → sacral plexus
- Thoracic level has less [[Gray vs White matter#^be7c9d|Gray matter]] than cervical and lumbrosacral 
	as lesser muscles around the abdomen compared to extremities
