Location map of body to receive sensory and send motor outputs

### Somatotopic map
shows:
- relative proportion of [[somatosensory cortex]] used for sensory input from each area of the body
- distribution of motor output from primary motor cortex to different parts of body
- precise distribution is unique for each individual
- use-dependent modification
- ==upper limb== takes up most proportion but distribution defers among individuals and ==can be altered via neuroplasticity==

### Relationship with Dermatomes & Myotomes
- [[03b Dermatomes]] & [[03c Myotomes]] describe **where sensory/motor information enters the nervous system** 
-  [[03a Sensory & motor homunculus]] shows **where that information is processed in the brain**.
- Both use same signal pathways but have different symptoms [[03d clinical relevance of sensory & motor maps]] 

- Example: Touch on the **thumb** (C6 dermatome) travels through the C6 nerve root, ascends the spinal pathways, and activates the **thumb region of the sensory homunculus**.
