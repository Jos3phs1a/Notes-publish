# Optic nerve signal pathway

#### Image projection onto retinas and correlation to visual fields
images are formed diagonally and visual field is marked on the contralateral side.
ie assuming vision all ok, image on far left projected in nasal field of left eye → far left visual field is ok 
refer to [[#Nerve lesion]] section

**right visual field** (everything on right) projects onto the **left half of each retina**. vice versa
ie nasal of right eye, temporal of left eye

**Temporal half** of retina stays on ipsilateral side, **nasal portion** will cross each other

# Nerve lesions

--- start-multi-column: ID_0jiw
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

#### 1: Complete left Optic nerve lesion
- complete loss of left visual field
- Left ipsilateral + right contralateral optic nerve dmg

#### 2: Partial left optic nerve lesion
e.g. Left ipsilateral dmg
- loss of left temporal retina
- loss of inner visual field

#### 3: Optic Chiasm lesion
Both contralateral nerves dmg
- loss of both nasal retina 
- outer visual field loss

#### 4: Optic tract or LGN fiber tract lesion
- loss of ipsilateral temporal + contralateral nasal 
- loss of ipsilateral inner field + contralateral outer field
