divided into 3 layers
# Outer
## Cornea
- protective clear covering over the [[#Pupil]] and iris. 
- continuous with [[#Sclera]] 
- [[03a Refraction of light in eye|refracts light]] to form image

## conjunctiva
- membrane that folds back from the eyelids and attaches to the [[#Sclera]] 
## Sclera
- tough outer wall of the eyeball; the white of the eye.

## [[01b aqueous humor]]
watery fluid that lies between [[#Cornea]] & [[#Lens]] (anterior chamber)

# Middle, Uvea

[[#Iris]], [[#Ciliary body]], [[#choroid]]
## Iris
- layer of sphincter muscles, pigmentation provides eye’s color.
- controls diameter of [[#Pupil]] and amount of light entering eye

## Ciliary body & muscles
- internal, ring-shaped [[Type of muscles#Smooth Muscle|smooth muscle]]  
- contains ciliary muscles which help changes the shape of [[#Lens]]
## Choroid
- contains layers upon layers of blood vessels for [[#Retina]] and muscles etc
- contains [[Special cells#Melanin|melanin]] → light absorption stops light from scattering prevent overload and confuse [[02a photoreceptor cells]] 
## Pupil
- dark circle in center of iris
- ==regulates amount of light== entering inner eye via dilation and constriction
- response slows with age
## Lens
- located behind the iris.
- suspended by ligaments (called zonule fibers) attached to the ==ciliary muscle== , forms a ring inside the eye.
- Flexible to ==focus upside-down image onto [[#Retina]]== from different distances 

## Vitreous humor
viscous jelly-like fluid that keeps eyeball spherical
between [[#lens]] & [[#retina]] (posterior chamber)

# Inner, Nervous tunic
## [[02d Retina]] 
- thin layer of light-sensitive cells at back of eye. 
- Made up of 2 types of [[02a photoreceptor cells]]

![[01c Ophthalmoscopic Appearance of the Eye#Macula]]
#### Fovea
a small depression within the neurosensory retina where visual acuity is the highest
## Optic nerve 
- transmits signals from [[#Retina]] to [[04b lateral geniculate nucleus]] of [[01ba Anatomic Divisions of Brain#Thalamus|thalamus]], and further to primary optic visual cortex, V1 of striate cortex
- in retina image is upside down, ==brain flips image back upright== for interpretation
