## Rods
long, cylindrical outer segment, containing many disks.
- more disks and higher photopigment concentration → 1000x sensitivity to light
- all contain the same photopigment
## Cones
shorter, tapering outer segment with fewer membranous disks
responsible for seeing colour
- 3 different types, each containing different pigment → sensitive to different wavelengths of light.

# Distribution across [[01a eye anatomy#Retina|retina]]

### At [[01a eye anatomy#Fovea|Fovea]]
- contains majority of [[#Cones]] within 10° of the fovea
- no [[#rods]]
- better for ==high-resolution vision==
(c) magnified cross section of human central retina shows dense packing of cone inner segments

### At peripheral 
- Higher proportion of [[#rods]]
- High ratio of [[02a photoreceptor cells]] to ganglion
- More sensitive to weak light → able to see dim light sources ==with peripheral vision but not central vision==
(d) more peripheral location on retina, cone inner segments larger and appear as islands in sea of smaller rod inner segments

## Cross section image
In cross section, [[01a eye anatomy#Fovea|Fovea]] appears as a pit in [[01a eye anatomy#Retina|retina]]. 
- appearance due to lateral displacement of cells above [[02a photoreceptor cells]] → allowing light to strike photoreceptors without passing through other retinal cell layers → maximizes visual acuity
