## Microscopic
signal pathway: [[02a photoreceptor cells]] → [[02b Bipolar cells]] → [[02c ganglion cells]] → brain ^1cb54a

3 principles:
1. ==only light-sensitive cells== in retina are the rod and cone photoreceptors, most other cells are ==influenced by light via direct and indirect synaptic interactions== with photoreceptors. 
2. ganglion cells are the ==only source of output== from retina. No other retinal cell type projects an axon through the optic nerve. 
3. Ganglion cells are only retinal neurons that [[04c Generation of Action potential|generate APs]]. All other retinal cells depolarize or hyperpolarize, and release neurotransmitter at rate proportional to membrane potential, but ==do not fire action potentials.==

### Horizontal cells
- **Integrate and regulate input** from multiple photoreceptors.
- Provide **lateral inhibition**, which creates the **center–surround receptive field** pattern.
- This **enhances contrast** and sharpens visual edges.
receive input from [[02a photoreceptor cells]] and project to other [[02a photoreceptor cells]] and [[02b Bipolar cells]] 

### Amacrine cells
- **Modulate and refine** visual signals before they reach ganglion cells.
- Involved in **motion detection**, **light adaptation**, and **temporal (time-based) contrast**
- Use various neurotransmitters (like dopamine, GABA, glycine) to **control timing and integration** of signals.
receives input from [[02b Bipolar cells]]  then project to [[02c ganglion cells]], [[02b Bipolar cells]] and other [[#Amacrine cells]] 

## Laminar organization

--- start-multi-column: ID_c2pd
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: right
```

- ==10 distinct layers== with **inside-out arrangement**. ie photoreceptors below, ganglion(output to [[01ba Anatomic Divisions of Brain|brain]]) above
- advantageous as pigmented epithelium below [[02a photoreceptor cells]] maintains the photoreceptors and photopigments. ==absorbs light== that 'skips' retina, minimize light scattering ==that blurs image== → **↑ visual acuity**
-  nocturnal animals have reflective layer beneath, called **tapetum lucidum**
	- bounces light back at photoreceptors if it passes through the retina, ↑ sensitivity to low light + ↓ acuity
