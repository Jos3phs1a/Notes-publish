# Presbyopia

老花眼

- increasing age 
- loss of elasticity of [[01a eye anatomy#Lens|lens]], becoming rigid, unable to [[03a Refraction of light in eye#Lens accommodation|accommodate]]

# Glaucoma

青光眼 (Qīngguāngyǎn)

--- start-multi-column: ID_9quj
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

- leading cause of blindness
- progressive loss of vision due to ==elevated intraocular pressure== 
- Pressure in [[01b aqueous humor]] crucial role in maintaining shape eye → pressure ↑ the entire eye is stressed, damaging the relatively weak point where optic nerve leaves eye. 
- The optic nerve axons compressed, and vision is gradually lost from the periphery inward. 
- no cure, damage to nerve is permanent, but treatment can control condition
**risk factors:** age, BP, smoking, obesity etc

--- column-break ---

- cannot definitively diagnose glaucoma just by observing a person's eye

# Strabismus

Binocular diplopia 斜视 (Xiéshì)
--- start-multi-column: ID_bu8u
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

misalignment of the eyes, causing one eye to deviate inward (esotropia) toward the nose, or outward (exotropia), while other eye remains focused

**causes:** 
- Eye muscles aren’t able to keep the eyes aligned
- Focusing issues, especially in those who are very farsighted, that cause one eye to turn inward or outward
