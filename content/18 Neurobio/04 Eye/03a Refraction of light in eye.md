## Refraction by cornea
- [[01a eye anatomy#Cornea|cornea]] refractive power of about 42 diopters → parallel light rays striking the corneal surface will be focused (2.4 cm) behind it

## Lens accommodation 
- [[01a eye anatomy#Lens|lens]] can change shape → forming crisp images of objects located closer than about 9 m from the eye

Mnemonic: Fast Race Cars Charge
### when object close
==ciliary muscle contracts== and swells in size → ==area inside the ring smaller== decreasing the tension in the suspensory ligaments → the lens becomes ==rounder and thicker== because of its natural elasticity → rounding increases the curvature of the lens surfaces, ==increasing their refractive power==
### when object far
==relaxation of the ciliary muscle== increases the tension in the suspensory ligaments → the lens is stretched into a ==flatter shape ==
