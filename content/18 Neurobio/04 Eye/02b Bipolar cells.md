- act as middle men in [[02d Retina#^1cb54a|signal pathway]] from [[02a photoreceptor cells]] to ganglion cells,
aid in transmitting visual information
- help in **contrast detection** and **light adaptation**
- receive ==graded electrical signals== not all or none
- a single bipolar cell can ==receive inputs from multiple== receptors

## ON Bipolar cells
- detect ↑ in light intensity
- G protein coupled/metabotropic glutamate receptor
- hyperpolarize to glutamate stimulation

--- column-break ---

## OFF Bipolar cells
- detect ↓ in light intensity
- Ionotropic glutamate receptor
- depolarize to glutamate stimulation

# Mechanism

NOTE concept: ==no light → brain cannot perceive anything → deactivate [[01a eye anatomy#Optic nerve|optic nerves]] vice versa==
#### Light condition
1. [[03b Phototransduction#Rods|Photoreceptors]] ↓ glutamate release 
2. [[#ON Bipolar cells]] depolarize →  GLT released
3. [[#OFF Bipolar cells]] hyperpolarize → no GLT released
4. voltage-gated Ca$^{2+}$ open, Neurotransmitter release
5. EPSP in ganglion cells
6. [[04c Generation of Action potential|AP generated]] → no signals to [[02a Functional divisions of brain|brain]]
