
# Radial head fracture


--- start-multi-column: ID_ymuv
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

Most common fracture
**MOI:** 
- direct trauma,
- valgus load
- shear injury

**Types:**
- Type I-undisplaced (most common)
- Type II-displaced wedge fragment
- Type III-comminuted fracture
- Type IV-fracture associated with elbow dislocation.

# Coronoid Fracture

--- start-multi-column: ID_q9eq
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

**MOI:** 
- complication after posterior subluxation
- severe varus stress
- shear injury
can be isolated, but most commonly occurs in association with [[#Radial head fracture]] and [[Elbow Dislocations]] forming **terrible triad**

**Classification:**
**Regan & Morrey**
1) tip/shear/avulsion
2) < 50% height of coronoid
3) ≥50% height of coronoid

**O'Driscoll**
1) tip or coronoid process
2) anteromedial facet
3) ≥ 50% of height of coronoid

# Monteigga fracture

1) Anterior radial head dislocation and fracture of ulna diaphysis with anterior angulation
2) Posterior or posterolateral radial head dislocation and fracture of ulna diaphysis with posterior angulation.
3) lateral or anterolateral radial head dislocation and fracture of ulna metaphysis.
4) Anterior radial head dislocation and fracture of proximal third of ulna and radius.

# Paediatric Fracture

aka little league elbow syndrome
range of lesions in the medial aspect of the elbow

**MOI:**
- valgus overuse injury during early and late cocking phases of throwing

**Variants**
- Delayed or accelerated growth of the medial epicondyle
- Medial epicondylar fragmentation
- Medial epicondylitis
# Management

### ORIF
Open reduction Internal fixation surgical procedure
metal implants on bones to realign them

### Immobilization casts
used frequently in younger patients

### Conservative
- elbow sling, loss pack position for healing
- close reduction
