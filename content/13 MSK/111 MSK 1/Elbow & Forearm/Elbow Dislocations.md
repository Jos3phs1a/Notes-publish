
# Posterior Dislocation



--- start-multi-column: ID_cn49
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```
most common and serious form

**MOI:** radius and ulna forcefully driven posteriorly to humerus

**Types:**
1) [[subluxations & dislocations|Subluxation]] in posterolateral direction
2) incomplete dislocation, coronoid perched on trochlea
3) complete dislocation, coronoid rests behind trochlea

**Complications**
- damage to nerves and vascular structures
- [[04 PTY/111 MSK 1/Elbow & Forearm/Ligamentous injuries]] resulting in instability of collateral ligaments

# Pediatric dislocation

Occurs easily for children as bones are still developing, elbow joint and ligaments not fully formed
NEVER PULL EXTENDED ARM of a child, hold by armpit

**Ossification**
- capitellum (1yo)
- radial head (3yo) - most common
- internal epicondyle (5yo)
- trochlea (7yo)
- olecranon (9yo)
- external epicondyle (11yo)

**MOI:** 
- traction force
- congenital dislocation
- direct trauma
# Management


--- start-multi-column: ID_rgnd
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

## [[subluxations & dislocations#Stable Dislocation|Stable dislocations]]
recovers well, conservative best
- Reduction and splinting at 90°
- immobilization for 2 wks
- ROM exercise when joint stable
- Light duty 2 wks

--- column-break ---

## [[subluxations & dislocations#Unstable Dislocation|unstable dislocation]] 
longer recovery, high chance of other tissue or vascular damage, contractures more severe
- Reduction splinting in hinged brace at 90°
- immobilization 2-3 wks
- ROM exercises with forearm in pronation - less stress on LCL
- active exercise for supination - biceps work as [[Elbow Muscle action#Supination/ Pronation|dynamic stablizers]]
