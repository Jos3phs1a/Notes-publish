# External impingements

mechanical encroachment(intrusion) of soft tissue into [[subacromial space]]
e.g. bursa, RC tendons
typically occurs midrange of motion active abduction, causing ‘painful arc’ 

| Subtype   | Mechanism                                                                                                                                            | Common cause                                                                           | population                           |
| --------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Primary   | **Structural** narrowing of subacromial space causing mechanical compression                                                                         | [[Acromial shape]] (type II/III), spurs, swelling of tissue e.g bursitis, rotator cuff | Older adults, degenerative shoulders |
| Secondary | **Functional** narrowing due to altered biomechanics or instability<br>may occur in [[subacromial space]] or inside [[Glenohumeral Joint\|GH joint]] | Rotator cuff/bicep weakness, [[scapular dyskinesis]], glenohumeral instability         | Young athletes, overhead workers     |

# Internal Impingements

encroachment of the rotator cuff tendons between the humeral head and the glenoid rim directly on [[Glenohumeral Joint]] 

| Subtype            | Mechanism                                                                                                                              | Common cause                                                       | population                               |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------- |
| Posterior-Superior | ==supraspinatus and infraspinatus== caught between ==GT== of humerus and ==posterosuperior rim of glenoid== due to [[hyperangulation]] | Repetitive overhead motion, excessive external rotation            | overhead athletes throwing, swimming     |
| Anterior-Superior  | Contact between subscapularis/biceps and anterosuperior glenoid/labrum                                                                 | Internal rotation with flexion; often seen in anterior instability | Less common; throwers, instability cases |
