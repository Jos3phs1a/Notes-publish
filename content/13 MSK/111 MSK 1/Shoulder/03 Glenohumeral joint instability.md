# Overview
aka unstable shoulder

Can be result of other conditions such as [[01b Rotator cuff tendinopathy|RC tendinopathy]], [[01 Subacromial pain syndrome|SAPS]] etc:
- dislocations
- excess uncontrolled translation due to dynamic/static stabilizers (trauma or non trauma)

Can be unidirectional (anterior, posterior, inferior) or multidirectional, affecting [[#physical exam]]

divided into 3 groups

### Traumatic shoulder instability (TUBs) 
**MOI:** [[subluxations & dislocations]] towards certain direction

| Type of dislocation   | Specific Mechanism                                  | Associated pathology                                                                                                     | Assessment                     |
| --------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------ |
| Anterior              | excess abduction + Ext Rotation                     | [[Glenoid Labrum or Bicep pathology#Bankart Lesions\|Bankart lesion]]<br>[[Hill sachs lesion]]<br>damaged axillary nerve | Apprehension Relocation        |
| Posterior dislocation | excess adduction + Int Rotation on outstretched arm |                                                                                                                          | limited ER<br>Posterior drawer |

### Acquired sport specific instability(AIOS)
laxity of anterior capsule due to repetitive overhead stress induced by [[hyperangulation]] correlation with [[01a Shoulder Impingements#Internal Impingements|Posterosuperior impingements]] 
#### Examination
- Apprehension/Relocation tests: pain + instability
### Atraumatic multidirectional instability
associated with generalised ligamentous laxity througout body
[[Beighton scale]] can be used

#### Examination
- hypermobility during passive ROM
- Increased GH glides + pain
- Weak low traps + SA

# Treatment
each condition requires different treatment
but principles involve:
- ↑ local GH stability
- correct aberrant muscle patterns
