aka painful arc syndrome, subacromial impingement, RC syndrome, RC disease
umbrella term for ==collection of symptoms resulting in pain, weakness and dysfunction of shoulder==,  has multiple mechanisms of injury which can be categorized



| Intrinsic factors | Extrinsic factors                                       |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| vascular          | scapular muscle imbalance                               |
| degenerative      | scapular postural changes and altered kinematics        |
| anatomic          | rotator cuff muscles imbalance (insufficiency/ruptures) |
|                   | anterior/posterior glenoid impingement                  |
|                   | precipitating factors                                   |

- [[01a Shoulder Impingements]]
- [[01b Rotator cuff tendinopathy]] 
- [[01c rotator cuff tears]]
- Impingement-related syndromes (e.g. [[#Neer's Classification]])
- Calcific tendinopathy (sometimes included)
## Neer's Classification 
| Stages | Description                                                                            | Clinical Presentation                                                                                                                   | Treatment Principles                                                                      |
| :----: | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
|   **1**    | <25 yo, <br>Swelling, Tendonitis, No Tears                                             | subacromial pain, tenderness typically overuse injury<br>painful arc<br>positive impingement and Neer's test<br>no loss in strength<br> | reduction and elimination of inflammation<br>restoration of proximal control<br>education |
|   **2**    | 25-40 yo,<br>Permanent Scarring, Tendonitis, No Tears, Subacromial Decompression       | + [[patterns of restriction]], loss of mobility<br>pain on activity<br>                                                                        | reestablish GH capsular mobility                                                          |
|   **3**    | > 40 yo,<br>Tendon disruption(tear), Subacromial Decompression with Debridement/Repair | + weakness in abduction and ext rot, squaring of acromion                                                                               | based on size of tear                                                                     |
