## Overview
aka frozen shoulder

stiffness and pain in [[Glenohumeral Joint]]

**Etiology:** complex, multifactorial. both genetic and environmental factors
generally involves: 
	inflammatory component oh joint capsule
	followed by stiffness and adhesions, resulting in fibrosis of the synovial lining

**Risk factors:** 
- comorbidities e.g. diabetes
- age: 40-60 yr
- gender: men > women

### Primary FS
- idiopathic
- resolves in 1.5 yrs on avg
- no evidence that intensive physiotherapy, injections, drugs can change the outcome, but can help in managing symptoms and preventing further loss in function

--- column-break ---

### Secondary FS
follows trauma or precipitating event  e.g. surgery, hormonal etc

#### Freezing stage (stage 1)
- last for 2–6 months
- characterized by moderate-severe pain and partial restriction of ROM.
*early stage FS may be confused with [[01b Rotator cuff tendinopathy|RC tendinopathy]], difference ROM ==progressively worsens== in FS*

#### Frozen stage (stage 2) 
- last for 4–12 months
- characterized by both ‘pain and ==stiffness==’

#### Thawing stage (stage 3)
- last for 6–26 months
- characterized by minimal pain and gradual resolution of stiffness.
- gradual resolution of inflammation and fibrosis,

### Treatment
conservative treatment most successful in up to 90% patients

[[#Freezing stage (stage 1)]]:
- minimize pain via PT, injections, pain meds etc
	Laser, short wave diathermy, [[04 Ultrasound|ultrasound]] and [[Heat Packs]]
	note: *==cannot relieve pain entirely==, purpose is enabling patient to ==perform ADL== with more ease*
- Mobilisation PT: ‘retaining’, and ‘slowly regaining’ ROM. 
	- during intense pain, vigorous mobilization PT **must be avoided** as it can exacerbate inflammation resulting ↑ pain

[[#Frozen stage (stage 2)]]:
profound loss in ROM due to fibrosis, but less pain
- ==ROM progression==
- consider [[01 Electrophysical agents|EPA]] for pain control?

[[#Thawing stage (stage 3)]]:
- ROM progression
- aim: regain the ‘functional’ followed by total recovery of shoulder ROM.
