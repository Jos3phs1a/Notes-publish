Complete or partial rupture of tendons

## Presentation
- pain
- limited ROM
- shoulder dysfunction
- large number may remain asymptomatic
- inability to sleep on the affected shoulder
- positive impingement signs and
- sometimes weakness on supraspinatus testing.

### imaging
[MRI](https://insightsimaging.springeropen.com/articles/10.1186/s13244-024-01607-w#Sec3) and ultrasound most sensitive & specific

### possible signs


- **Strength**: **Marked weakness**, especially in the muscle specific to the torn tendon:
    - **Supraspinatus tear** → weakness in shoulder abduction (empty can)
    - **Infraspinatus/teres minor** → weakness in external rotation (ext rot lag)
    - **Subscapularis** → weakness in internal rotation (bear hug)
        
- **ROM**:
    - **Active ROM often limited**, especially with large/full-thickness tears
    - **Passive ROM preserved**

### Surgical
Generally **Full thickness tears(FTT)** require surgical treatment, 
for **older patients**, choice for surgery or conservative treatment depends on:
- degree of discomfort and pain during daily and sports activities 
- expectations of the patient.

### principles
- progressive ROM, resistance 
- early mobilization unless contravene by protocol
- targeted exercise for delts and traps

--- column-break ---

### Conservative
Shift towards conservative not proven to be efficient. But option as post [[#Surgical]] function is ==also not as good as pre pathology levels== 

### Principles
- avoid post treatment tissue inflammation and associated pain
- exercise for restoring maximal function, reduce load on RC
