Guidelines

## Intro
Introduce self, get to know the patient
- introduce your main agenda and sequence of events, will ask about the pain and move on to assessments
- "what seems to be bothering you? How can I help you today?"
## Inquire

baseline information must always have

##### Location 
ALWAYS FIRST
where is the pain
	"point to pain?"
	vague vs specific
	deep vs superficial
##### Onset 
**mechanism of injury** → how did this pain come about?
Trauma vs gradual?
	interpretation of trauma may differ, ==always probe more details==
	pain from 1-10
	dull, sharp, etc
	
##### Duration 
==24 hour pattern==, throughout day, during activities? random occurrence etc
Does it **affect sleep**?
when does pain come, how long does it normally take to go away

##### Characteristics 
usually give away nature of condition
- Neurogenic: numbness **P/N**
- Atherogenic: grinding (crepitus), pain on PROM
- Myogenic: weakness, pain on AROM
- Mechanical: instability, locking/restricted, pain in end range/active motion

##### Aggravating 
how does the pain come about
rate PFPS

##### Relief 
what makes the pain go away
how does pain feel after resting
##### Treatment 
medical interventions that you have done to deal with the pain
- previous conditions
- medication
- radiological
- seeing other physician

### Yellow and Red flags
Preempt patient or address indirectly, avoid face front

=="I'm just going to ask you a few questions to rule out any unlikely causes of your current pain. This will help me ensure that the tests and treatment will be safe moving forward."==

"Have you been feeling frustrated or stressed out more often" VS "Do you have depression"

"Have you had any troubles when using the toilet recently" vs "Do you have bowel dysfunction"


#### Psychosocial consideration
beliefs about condition/treatment/physio

Check for life status:
- family
- living condition

Mental aspect: 
- does this pain cause you any distress 

#### unable to perform assessment/treatment
If need to avoid treatment plus push for further assessment:
- Address main concern: 
	Given the current information and condition, I feel that it is ==not safe== for me to conduct any assessments or treatments for now it is ==hard to actually tell what the main cause== of the problem is, especially ==when any movements will only worsen your current pain==
- Provide next move:
	I will refer you to a doctor to get a ==scans and tests done== so that we can make ==eliminate any unlikely causes of your pain==, and to get information that I ==cannot obtain with my eyes or through physical assessments.==
