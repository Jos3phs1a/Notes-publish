# Gluteal

## Possible Conditions
- [[01 Pathology and aetiology|tendinopathy]] aka greater trochanter pain syndrome, gluteal enthesopathy
- Tear
## Tell tale signs

```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

### Subjective 
- Age (4$^{th}$ - 6 $^{th}$ ) , gender (F 2.5x more likely)
- sedentary
- pain over G trochanter
- History of Lumbar Issues - evidence of strong relationship
- History of knee OA and increased BMI
- snapping sensation
- **Functional**: sitting with legs crossed, laying on affected side, prolonged unilateral stance, climbing stairs

--- column-break ---

### Objective
#### Physical Exam
- Pain EOR active/Resisted hip abduction
- Pain EOR passive hip adduction
- Pain on palpating greater trochanter
- Likely poor functional ability in bridges lunges, squats etc
- [[Hip muscle action#Trendelenberg sign|Trendelenberg Sign]]
- FABER may be positive
- External Derotation Test
#### Loading Tests
- Single leg stance
- Resisted hip abduction
- Step up
- Hip Hitch
