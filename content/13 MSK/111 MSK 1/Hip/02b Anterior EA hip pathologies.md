Typically myogenic in nature, related to soft tissues like muscles, tendons and ligaments in the hip & groin region
Have to be careful about exact location of pain due to overlapping and close proximity of structures

## Assessments
- Tenderness and pain can be assessed through palpation
- Pain is more specific compared to [[02a IA pathologies of hip]] 
- Resisted/active movement will bring about symptoms (caution if irritability high)
# Adductor

- Most common cause of groin pain 
- usually due to adductor longus injury → role as [[Hip muscle action#Adductors & Abductors|hip flexor]] 

## Possible conditions
- [[01 Pathology and aetiology|tendinopathy]] 
- Acute strain
- Partial/complete tear

## Tell tale signs

--- start-multi-column: ID_4us7
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

### Subjective
- pain as a result of ↑ training vol, or repetitive motion
- pain upon ==sprinting, cutting, kicking==
- finger sign → identify area of adductor longus tendon attachment to pubic bone

--- column-break ---

### Objective
#### Radiological investigation
- ultrasound, MRI & CT v useful during acute injuries

#### Physical Exam
- palpable tenderness
- pain on resisted adduction and passive abduction - end range
- adductor squeeze w pressure cuff

# Iliopsoas


- 2nd most common cause of groin pain. 
- Pain at anterior part of the proximal thigh, more laterally than [[#Adductor]] related groin pain.

## Possible conditions
- [[01 Pathology and aetiology|tendinopathy]]
- [[bursitis]]
- Partial/Complete tear
## Tell tale signs

--- start-multi-column: ID_ir7p
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

### Subjective
- pain pointed more laterally

--- column-break ---

### Objective
#### Radiological investigation
- ultrasound, MRI v useful in acute injuries as hard to localize due to the different structures present + edema
#### Physical Exam
- Iliopsoas tenderness
- pain upon resisted hip flexion
- pain on stretching hip flexors

--- end-multi-column




# Pubic

No epidemiological data are available in the literature regarding how common pubic-related groin pain is.
## Possible conditions
- osteitis pubis
	inflammatory → loading + sheet/compress forces causes flare ups
- Overuse/stress injury
	
### Subjective
### Objective
#### Radiological investigation
#### Physical Exam
- tenderness immediately adjacent to pubic bone
- no particular resistance tests to test specifically for pubic related groin pain could be identified
	- resisted abdominal work
	- resisted adductor work
# Inguinal

many different terminology in the past, now referred as **inguinal disruption**
exact pathophysiology unknown, however bulging of posterior wall can cause [[01 Pathology and aetiology]], tears of external oblique aponeuroses or tearing of the conjoined tendon
### Subjective
### Objective
#### Radiological investigation
#### Physical Exam
- pain and tenderness in inguinal canal region
- no palpable hernia present
- abdominal pain and weakness
- pain upon cough/sneeze


# Ischiofemoral impingement/Quadratus femoris tear

## Signs
- pain over quadratus femoris/ischiofemoral region rather than laterally over G tronch, with or without groin pain
- snapping sensation in ischifemoral interval 
- pain in variety of postures and activities
