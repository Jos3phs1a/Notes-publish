
# Hip [[osteoarthritis]]

OA is inherently complex and vague with symptoms that are common with other conditions e.g. [[#Femoroacetabular impingement syndrome (FAI)|FAI]]
- because of this, information from [[History taking]] is crucial in gathering conclusive diagnosis
- find signs related to [[osteoarthritis#Criteria for diagnosis|Criteria for diagnosis]] → imaging not required but good to have

## Tell tale signs

### Subjective 
- Risk factors: Older, Gender(Female more prevalent)
- onset: gradual without trauma
- Features
	- vague/radial/deep pain
	- brief morning stiffness
	- crepitus
- other [[osteoarthritis#Risk factors|Risk factors]] and [[#Femoroacetabular impingement syndrome (FAI)|FAI]] 
### Objective
#### Radiological investigations
- [[morphology of hip joint#Cam|Cam deformity]] has decent association - 4x higher risk of developing OA within 5 years and up to10 times higher risk with a greater cam deformity
	typically reduced ROM, IR <20°
- [[morphology of hip joint#Pincer|Pincer deformity]] has conflicting association

#### Physical exams
- Reduced ROM
- Pain in active motion esp flexion and rotation
- Pain in weight bearing
- palpable crepitus
- Accessory movement analysis
- Special tests - FABER, FADIR, Scour, Thomas

	
### Note
- previous depictions of joint space narrowing now show poor correlation to OA
- transitioning to be considered as a systemic issue 
**FABER & FADIR**
- **Low specificity** → if ==Positive== hard to conclude if pain is form [[#Femoroacetabular impingement syndrome (FAI)|FAI]], [[#Labral Tears]], [[#Hip osteoarthritis|OA]]  *may result in false positives* as tests 
- **High sensitivity** → if ==negative,== high probability conditions are not contributing 




# Femoroacetabular impingement syndrome (FAI)

- Motion or position related pain in the hip or groin. 
- may also be felt in back, buttocks, or thigh

## Tell tale signs

### Subjective 
- ==Younger adults (esp females)== more susceptible to developing pain due to high intensity activity
- Onset: either ==acute or chronic==
- Mechanical
	- Pain in flexion, squatting sitting
	- Pain in sharp turns or pivots
	- Clicking, catching, or giving way **Not considered as pathology, only clinical signs**

### Objective
similar to [[osteoarthritis]] (resulting in hard to diagnose), **Importance of** [[#Subjective]] 
#### radiological investigations
- Varying and conflicting results
- [[morphology of hip joint]] has shown some association to hip pain, but majority with deformities are asymptomatic anyway
	- only 26% of people with a cam deformity complain of hip pain


#### Physical Exam
- Weaker lower body strength 
	- Knee extensors, flexors
	- hip extensors, flexors adductors
	- slower gait + possible [[Hip muscle action#Trendelenberg sign|Trendelenberg sign]] 


# Labral Tears

Tear of [[Hip joint stability#Acetabular labrum|Acetabular labrum]]
- can be acute or chronic, developing or coexisting with [[#Femoroacetabular impingement syndrome (FAI)|FAI]]
- resulting in diagnosis and Clinical examination remaining difficult due to reported similar symptoms  
## Tell tale signs
### Subjective

- More prevalent in athletic population similar to [[#Femoroacetabular impingement syndrome (FAI)|FAI]] 
	- 22% with groin pain have labral tears, 55% with mechanical symptoms and hip pain have labral tears.
### Objective
#### Radiological
- **EASIEST** - MRI scan 
#### Physical examination
- similar to [[#Femoroacetabular impingement syndrome (FAI)|FAI]] 


# Dysplasia

increase in abnormal cell growth or development
resulting in shallowing of acetabular socket and or deformation of head of femur
cause ==instability and subluxation==

## Tell tale signs
### Subjective
- More prevalent in younger, active females
- giving way with/without pain
- chronic

### Objective
#### Radiological investigation
- X-ray easiest
#### Physical Exam
- hypermobility - use [[Beighton scale]]
- Apprehension and/or Pain on intra-articular tests

- usually result of overuse e.g long distance running or low baseline fitness
