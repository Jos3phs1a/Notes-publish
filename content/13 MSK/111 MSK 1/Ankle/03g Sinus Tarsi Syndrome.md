## Overview
pain localized at lateral opening of tarsal sinus + symptoms of hindfoot instability
lacks precise diagnostic  criteria → hard to diagnose and treat

**Structures involved:** 
- [[Subtalar Joint]] & talocalcaneonavicular joint, divided by tarsal sinus
- Ligamentous structures e.g. cervical ligament, interosseous talocalcaneal ligament, inferior extensor retinaculum
- calcaneofibular ligament, anterior talocalcaneal ligament that stabilize [[Subtalar Joint]]

**MOI:**
- [[03c Ankle Impingements#Soft tissue|Soft tissue impingements]]
- ligament dmg
- bleeding
- arthritis
- chronic inflammation
- lateral impingement
- hindfoot alignment e.g., valgus or anterolateral facet

## Clinical Presentation
- pain tenderness over sinus tarsi, worse during WB
- hindfoot instability especially on uneven ground

## Assessment
- thorough history taking, emphasis on onset, duration, agg factors, trauma, surguries
- physical exam: palpation, hindfoot alignment, gait, neurovascular test
- Diagnostic tests: dynamic impingement test


## Risk factors
- sports e.g. dancers, basketball
- obesity, flatfoot deformity, alter foot biomechanics → ↑ risk to STS
