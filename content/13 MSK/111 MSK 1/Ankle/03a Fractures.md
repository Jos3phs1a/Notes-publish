### Percival Pott (PM)
categorize based on ==no. of malleoli involved==
1) unimalleolar: involving a single malleolus (med or lat)
2) Bimalleolar: involving med and lat malleoli
3) Trimalleolar: involving all 3 malleoli (med,lat,post)

### Danis-Weber AO (DF)
categorize based on level of fibula fracture and syndesmosis disruption
1) Type A: transverse fracture at/below ankle joint only
2) Type B: spiral fibula fracture starting at level of joint + partial syndesmotic injury
3) Type C: fracture proximal ankle joint + disruption of syndesmosis to same level


--- column-break ---

### Lauge-hansen (LD)
categorize based on position of foot and deforming force during injury
1) pronation-abduction
2) pronation-lateral rotation/eversion
3) supination-adduction
4) supination-lateral rotation/inversion

# Stress fractures

microscopic injuries due to repeated stress 

## Clinical presentation
- exertional lower limb pain, midfoot pain aggravated by physical stress
- tenderness at fracture site

## Imaging
X-rays often used, but may not detect early stress reactions, result in false-negatives
MRI useful for soft tissue injuries and early bone changes. Preferred choice for diagnosis.

### Metatarsal 
- common in athletes, 
- 2nd & 3rd metatarsal
- Treatment: rest WB cast immobilization
### 5th Metatarsal base 
- lateral foot pain
- Treatment: conservative management to operative fixation
### Navicular
- midfoot pain, challenging to diagnose
- Treatment: NWB cast immobilization, surgery
### Talus
- Rare
- Treatment: conservative

--- column-break ---

### Sesamoid
- Forefoot pain during activity
- management ranges from conservative to sesamoidectomy
### Cuneiform
- rare but associated with gait changes and excess exercise
- Treatment: WB immobilization + conservative management

### Cuboid
- rare often undiagnosed
- treatment: conservative
