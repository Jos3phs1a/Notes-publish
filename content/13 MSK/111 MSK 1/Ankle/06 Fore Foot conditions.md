# Morton's neuroma

```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
```

## Overview

**Structures involved**
- plantar digital nerve
- deep transverse metatarsal ligament
- intermetatarsal bursae

**Pathomechanism**
- **Chronic Trauma theory:** ==repetitive mechanical== effects during walking cause trauma to common plantar digital nerve
- **Entrapment theory:** deep transverse metatarsal ligament ==compressing== structure contributing to nerve irritation
- **Intermetatarsal bursitis theory:** ==compression + inflammation== of common plantar digital nerve due to ==intermetatarsal bursitis== in **2nd 3rd intermetatarsal spaces**
- **Ischemic theory:** proposed ==degeneration== in common ==plantar digital artery== preceding ==nerve fibrosis==

--- column-break ---

## Presentation + risk factors
- footwear - high heels, narrow toe box
- pain on ==plantar surface of metatarsal heads==, radiating to ==corresponding toes==
- Subjective sensation of walking on foreign object
- ==intermetatarsal space tenderness== on ==compression==/percussion

## Assessment:
- diagnosis primarily based on history and physical exam
- ==compression and percussion of intermetatarsal spaces==
- [Mulder's click](https://www.google.com/search?sca_esv=e2da0ea2adfc5b70&sxsrf=AE3TifMBxLbdHYWTtyDmhw8nJYFD8xUzag:1752312510699&q=Mulder%27s+click&source=lnms&fbs=AIIjpHyXkckd7Il21lobbVJc1XW8UWqx1w2L_HS4glko4E1W2IeeIZvHkM4DWoJRZaDIWj-19PEa5EIwXyC_8gqy8sSg0YuGujPNov0Y-glAntJwAaUTSCsp6ryI_IVbPAgoByVKwXIZKeaCyoJYKwuW6FpLqoEWYlA9tCSUT3Vy3c5chzomaQ7uR2M-4sx5nof53OCU9lnlQDMSKFNvnz6tTmmYKwrlq8-VNKPoPeVSI9ebEV8f8aw&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwjL-vDlgLeOAxUIT2cHHf0AO3YQ0pQJKAB6BAgTEAE&biw=2880&bih=1366&dpr=0.67#fpstate=ive&vld=cid:ea2aa48f,vid:PHNWpZvezUE,st:0) sign

## Biomechanical factors
- instability of metatarsophalangeal joints
- anatomical vulnerability of 3rd common plantar digital nerve
- compression between metatarsal heads and metatarsophalangeal joints

# Turf Toe


--- start-multi-column: ID_vhyk
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

## Overview
Sprain of plantar capsule-ligament of great toe [[Distal tarsal Joints#Metatarsophalangeal Joints|MTP joint]]

**Structures involved:** 
First MTP joint:
- plantar plate
- joint capsule
- associated ligaments
Supporting structures:
- transverse head of adductor hallucis
- flexor tendon sheath
- deep transverse intermetatarsal ligament

**MOI:**
- Hyperextension of 1st [[Distal tarsal Joints#Metatarsophalangeal Joints|MTP joint]]

--- column-break ---

## Presentation + Risk factors
common among sports requiring acceleration, cutting movements more on Astroturf field

- pain, swelling,[[ecchymosis]] on 1st MTP
- antalgic gait, poor toe-off
- tenderness on plantar aspect 1st MTP, possible on medial, lateral, dorsal aspects
- ↓ passive extension, active flexion ROM
- Muscle strength: pain during flexion or extension against resistance
- Special tests: valgus and varus stress tests

# Hallux valgus


--- start-multi-column: ID_rxob
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

## Overview
aka bunion, deformity of 1st ray. 
lateral deviation of proximal phalanx and medial deviation of 1st metatarsal head

**Structures involved:**
- 1st [[Distal tarsal Joints#Metatarsophalangeal Joints|MTP joint]]: deviation/damage
- Proximal phalanx: ==lateral== deviation
- First metatarsal: ==medial== deviation and potential ==dorsiflexion==
- Collateral ligaments: [[03d Ligamentous Injuries#Medial|strain and potential rupture]] of [[Ankle ligaments#Deltoid ligament|MCLs]]
- Muscles: imbalance between extrinsic(peroneus longus) and intrinsic(abductor hallucis) muscles


--- column-break ---

## MOI + Risk factors
unknown aetiology, precise cause not fully known
**Contributing factors:**
- **genetics**
- **biomechanical abnormalities:** short of dorsiflexed 1st metatarsal, forefoot varus, pes planovalgus, gastrocnemius equinus, joint hypermobility
- **Arthritic conditions:** RA, gout, psoriatic 
- **Connective tissue disorders:** Marfan/Ehlers-danlos, Down syndromes
- **Muscle imbalance:** stroke, cerebral palsy, myelomeningocele 

## Presentation
- Pain: chronic, progressive ==sharp/deep pain== at MTP joint, worse on WB
- Redness, swelling
- Toe deformity
- Tingling or burning sensation: ==compression → medial dorsal cutaneous nerve== neuritis  
- Blisters and skin irritations: ==pressure and friction== against shoes
