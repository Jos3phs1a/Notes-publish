# Lateral

## Overview
Damage to the ligaments on the outer aspect of ankle

**MOI:** sudden inversion of foot aka twisting/rolling

**Structures involved:**  [[Ankle ligaments#Anterior talofibular ligament|ATFL]] & [[Ankle ligaments#Calcaneofibular ligament|CFL]] maybe [[Ankle ligaments#Posterior talofibular ligament|PTFL]] 

**Risk factors:** previous sprains (recurrent), reduced proprioception, muscle weakness, imbalance ↑ susceptibility to lateral ankle sprains

**Additional biomechanical issues:** lateral ankle sprains can lead to altered walking patterns, ↓ ROM dorsiflexion, compromised balance, causing recurrent injuries 

--- column-break ---

## clinical presentation
- pain, swelling, tenderness on lateral aspect
- limited ROM and stability
- cold foot or paresthesia implies neurovascular compromise of peroneal nerve
- weight bearing challenging
- positive anterior drawer and talar tilt

## High Recurrence Rate
Highest recurrence rate of all lower limb injuries, 34% suffer [[#residual problems]] including recurring injury < 3 yrs of first sprain

### Causes
**Mechanical instability:**==excess ROM in inversion==, laxity in ==rear foot==, ==anterior laxity== in [[Talocrural Joint]]

**Functional instability:** frequent instability → stem from sensorimotor deficits

**Sensorimotor deficits:** impairments in proprioception, posture stability, neuromuscular control

**Muscle weakness:** ==↓ concentric inversion== strength, may be due to ==inhibitory reflex==

**Postural sway:** deficits of postural control


--- column-break ---

## residual problems
**Incomplete healing:** microscopic tear in ligaments may not fully heal → weakness and risk factor for injury

**Persistent instability:** untreated laxity/inadequate rehab → ↑ risk factor

**altered biomechanics:** compensation mechanisms altered [[Gait Cycle Phases|gait patterns]]

**proprioceptive deficits:** impaired proprioception may persist → unable to detect and respond to joint position + movements

**Psychological factors:** fear reinjury or apprehension during physical activity

# High ankle sprains


--- start-multi-column: ID_wccv
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

## Overview
aka **syndesmotic ankle sprain** 
injury to ligaments that bind distal tibia and fibula at **distal tibiofibular syndesmosis**
less common injury, ==~11%== of all ankle injuries, more prevalent in sports ==requiring external rotation or dorsiflexion== e.g. american football, skiing

**MOI:** 
- extreme external rotation or dorsiflexion of talus
- direct impact on outer leg
- rapid direction change
- falls

**Structures involved:** 
- Anterior inferior tibiofibular ligament (AITFL)
- Posterior inferior tibiofibular ligament (PITFL)
- Interosseous ligament (IOL)
- Transverse ligament


--- column-break ---

## Clinical Presentation
- less swelling 
- difficulty plantarflexion + WB
- [[ecchymosis]] post injury
- pain and tenderness over distal tibiofibular syndesmosis
- difficulty toe-walking
- chronic pain and prolonged recovery 

--- end-multi-column
