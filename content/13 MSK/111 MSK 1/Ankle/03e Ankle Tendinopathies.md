## Overview

**Structures:** achilles tendon, other structures may be affected in [[#insertional tendinopathy]]

**MOI:** Following default [[01b Continuum model of Tendinopathy|Continuum model]], excess load without time to adapt 

**Contributing factors:** 
1) excess load
2) anatomical abnormalities: Haglund deformity → ↑ exert compressive force on tendon and calcaneus 
3) Footwear & activities: compressive forces from footwear ↑ strain, esp during activities like uphill running
4) Mechanoreceptive Response: excess load 

--- column-break ---

## Clinical Presentation
- pain swelling at tendon
- stiffness, pain on palpation, activity
- pain in morning after prolonged sitting may subside during activity but worsen afterward

## Risk factors
**Intrinsic:**
- ↓ plantarflexor strength
- abnormal dorsiflexion
- hip neuromuscular control
- ↑ pronation

**Extrinsic:**
- training-load errors
- footwear
- type of sport
- environmental conditions
- systemic disease
- genetics

--- end-multi-column


Assessment is mainly clinical
palpation + special tests(arc sign + royal London hospital test)

## spectrum of tendinopathies including:
### insertional tendinopathy
20-25% of AT injuries
- **location:** ==insertion point== on calcaneus
- **presentation:** pain on load e.g. running, jumping, palpating insertion 
- **structural change**:  ==retrocalcaneal bursa==, ==intratendinous calcifications== and ==bone defects== 
### midportion tendinopathy
55-65% of AT injuries
- **location:** pain and tenderness found in ==midportion 2-6cm above insertion==
- **presentation:** ==morning stiffness==, pain prolonged sitting + activity
- **Diagnostic tests**: arc sign, royal london test
### proximal musculotendinous junction
9-25% of AT injuries
- **location:** pain and tenderness at junction ==between achilles and calf==, proximal to midportion
- **presentation:**  pain with activities involving calf muscles, such as pushing off during walking/running
- **Diagnostic tests**: palpation of proximal achilles tendon and assessment of pain during resisted plantar flexion

**Insertional vs midportion:** palpation tenderness anterior to tendon → [[#insertional tendinopathy]], tenderness directly on tendon → [[#midportion tendinopathy]], could also be posterior ankle pain, posterior ankle impingement, os trigonum syndrome

**Midportion vs proximal:** based on location of pain. 


# Peroneal

--- start-multi-column: ID_4h74
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

## Overview

**Structures:** Peroneus brevis, longus, tertius

**MOI:** 
- prolonged, repetitive activities
- ankle inversion injury
- hindfoot varus alignment
- forceful dorsiflexion with hindfoot inversion

**Contributing factors:** 
- variable retromalleolar groove morphology → tendon stability
- presence of [[os peroneum]] within peroneus longus tendon → ↑ injury risk

--- column-break ---

## Clinical Presentation
- ==Posterolateral== ankle pain, worsen with activity
- ==tenderness== over peroneal tendons
- ==palpable mass== suggestive of tendinosis
- pain with resisted eversion/passive inversion stretch/resisted plantar flexion of 1st ray
- active circumduction of ankle, creating tendon subluxation
## Risk factors
- repetitive activities
- hindfoot varus alignment
- forceful dorsiflexion with hindfoot inversion
