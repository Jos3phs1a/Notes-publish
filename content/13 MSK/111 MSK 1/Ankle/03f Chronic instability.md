
## Overview

Repetitive episodes or perceptions of giving way, pain weakness, ==diminished self function > 1 year post injury==

**Structures involved:** 
- [[Ankle ligaments#Anterior talofibular ligament|ATFL]]
- [[Ankle ligaments#Calcaneofibular ligament|CFL]]
- secondary tissue dmg to: Fibularis tendons, osteochondral surfaces, synovial membrane, adjacent ligaments

**MOI:** 
- Excessive (==inversion and internal rotation==) aka **supination** of rearfoot on externally rotated leg
- often occurs in ankle neutral/dorsiflexed

## Clinical Presentation
- Instability/giving way
- pain
- swelling
- reduced function in ADLs and sports

#### Empirical measurements
- CAIT < 25
- IdFAI > 10
- FAAM-ADL < 90%
- FAAM sport < 80%

## Risk factors
- previous [[03d Ligamentous Injuries#Lateral|Lateral Ankle sprains]]

## Biomechanical factors
- Increased inversion/internal rot & plantarflexion on movement
- compensation by hip and knee
- altered proprioception, neuromuscular control, activation patterns


# Development theory

Multifactorial, involving different impairments

### Pathomechanical
mechanical constraints due to:
- laxity
- arthrokinematic/osteokinematic restrictions
- degenerative changes
### Sensory-perceptual
perception and behaviour changes due to:
- somatosensation
- pain
- perceived instability
- kinesiophobia

### Motor-behavioural
motor output influenced by:
- altered reflexes
- neuromuscular inhibition
- muscle weakness
- balance deficits
- altered movement patterns
