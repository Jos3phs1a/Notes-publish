# Plantar fasciitis


--- start-multi-column: ID_7l48
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

pain and discomfort in inferior heel region associated with inflammation or degeneration of plantar fascia
can be palpated at medial calcaneal tubercle

**MOI:** repetitive micro-trauma to plantar fascia, leading to tissue degeneration or fasciosis

**Prevalence/Incidence:**  
- common during ages 40-60 
- 15% of foot injuries
- no significant gender difference

## Presentation
- pain in ==heel region==, may radiate ==along longitudinal arch==
- worse in morning, improve throughout day
- pain on WB on hard surfaces
- sudden increase in activity
- limited dorsiflexion, stiffness in gastroc-achilles complex
- gait abnormalities: antalgic gait

--- column-break ---

## Assessment:
primary clinical, focus on [[History taking]] and physical exam
special tests e.g. windlass test, passive 1st ray extension, view for medial longitudinal arch

### imaging
MRI and ultrasound in atypical cases or refractory symptoms
ultrasound can measure thickness of proximal plantar fascia for [[hypoechogenicity]]

## Risk factors
 - prolonged standing
- long distance running
- female
- flat feet
- high arch
- poor footwear
- ↓ ankle flexion
- sedentary lifestyle 

--- column-break ---

## biomechanical factors
- poor ankle flexion
- trauma to soft tissue


--- end-multi-column
### Possible Differentials
- calcaneal fat pat atrophy
- plantar fibroma
- baxter neuropathy
- stress fractures/injury of calcaneus

# Tarsal Tunnel Syndrome



--- start-multi-column: ID_69dm
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```
## Overview
aka Posterior tibial neuralgia
compression of tibial nerve/its branch as it travels through tarsal tunnel and under flexor retinaculum

**Structures involved**
- Tibial nerve
- tarsal tunnel
- Flexor retinaculum: contains TDVNH

**MOI:** ^fe7f3d
- anatomical anomalies, cysts/varicose veins/spurs
- swelling/inflammation due to trauma
- Repetitive stress
- systemic conditions e.g. diabetes, arthritis


--- column-break ---

## Clinical Presentation
- burning pain in posteromedial ankle and/or plantar aspect 
- paresthesia or dysesthesia(numbness) in plantar aspect 
- Pian after WB
- weakness in flexion
- Positive Hoffman-Tinel sign: tingling when tapping over tibial nerve
- Sensory loss

## Risk factors
- similar to [[#^fe7f3d|MOI]]
