## Pes Planus(Flat foot)


--- start-multi-column: ID_xprc
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```
### Presentation
Absence of [[Medial longitudinal arch]] due to overpronated foot
- forefoot: abducted supinated, 1st metatarsal may be adducted
- Talar head: displace medial, anterior, inferior
- Navicular: Dorso-lateral subluxation over talar head
- Calcaneum: Eversion, resulting in hindfoot valgus
- Medial Border: Appears longer than lateral border

### Types
1. **Flexible:** arch visible during NWB 
2. **Rigid**: Arch consistently absent

### Related Biomechanics
1. [[Alignment of Knee#Genu valgum/valgus|Knee valgus]]
2. [[Patellofemoral Joint#Bowstringing effect|Lateral Pull on patella]]
3. hip-knee-foot relationship:
	compensatory IR or hip
	Anterior pelvic tilt + lower back posture
4. [[Application to gait cycle]] 
	inefficient movement patterns
	improper distribution of forces ↑ risk of injury
5. Muscle imbalance
	weak hip abductors: ↑ IR ↓ underuse
	overactive hip adductor: excess IR ↑ strain
## Pes Cavus (high arch)
high plantar longitudinal arch


--- start-multi-column: ID_908l
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

### Features
- Longitudinal Arch: elevated
- first ray: plantarflexion due to strong pull on Fibularis longus
- forefoot: varus & adducted
- midfoot: high pitched structure
- hindfoot: varus alignment
- calcaneus: high calcaneal pitch
### Presentation
- visible high arch: elevates on WB
- frequent [[03d Ligamentous Injuries|Sprains]]: due to instability
- foot pain: in heel, lateral foot, first MT head
- Shoe fit issues
- calluses
- cock-up deformities at [[Distal tarsal Joints#Metatarsophalangeal Joints|MTP joints]]
