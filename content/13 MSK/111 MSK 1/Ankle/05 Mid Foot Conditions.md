# Lisfranc injury



--- start-multi-column: ID_v8wy
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

## overview
range of injuries along [[Distal tarsal Joints#Tarsometatarsal Joints|TMT Joints]] and Intercuneiform joints 

**Structures involved**
- medial, middle, lateral cuneiforms
- cuboid
- bases of 5th metatarsal
- transverse intermetatarsal ligament
- [[Lisfranc ligament]]
- interosseous ligaments
- dorsal, plantar ligaments

**MOI:** 
High energy trauma: 
- motor vehicle accidents
- falls
- crush injuries

Low energy trauma:
- sports injuries, axial rotational loads on plantarflexed position

--- column-break ---

## Presentation
- acute midfoot pain
- unable to WB
- pain, swelling, [[ecchymosis]] over midfoot
- gap between 1st and 2nd toes
- instability with stress testing [[Distal tarsal Joints#Tarsometatarsal Joints|TMT Joints]]

# Metatarsalgia


--- start-multi-column: ID_x4qv
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

## overview
pain under metatarsal heads in forefoot region

**Prevalence**
- athletes and individuals in high-impact sports
- more common in women, likely due to footwear
- associated with foot deformities e.g. hallux valgus

**Structures involved**
bones
- lesser metatarsal heads, 
- proximal phalanx
soft tissue:
- dorsal aspect: joint capsule, extensor digitorum longus & brevis tendons
- medial lateral walls: proper and accessory ligaments
- Plantar aspect: plantar plate, flexor digitorum longus & brevis tendons

**MOI:**
- Abnormal biomechanics: misalignment during [[Application to gait cycle|Gait cycle]] overload metatarsal heads
- Footwear: poor compatibility increase pressure on metatarsals
- high-impact activities



--- column-break ---

## Presentation
- aching/burning pain under metatarsal heads
- swelling, tenderness
- development of calluses or keratosis under affected areas
- altered gait 
- difficulty in toe-off

--- end-multi-column

# Cuboid syndrome


--- start-multi-column: ID_wsre
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

## Overview
commonly misdiagnosed lateral midfoot pain
disruption of **calcaneocuboid joint** integrity → ==impinged or displaced CC labrum==

**Structures involved:**
- **cuboid bone:** lateral midfoot, bordered by calcaneus, metatarsals, navicular and lateral cuneiform
- **CC joint:** medial/lateral rotation(pronation/supination or inversion/eversion)
- **Joint stabilizers:** articular surfaces, ligaments & tendons, peroneus longus tendon

--- column-break ---

## Presentation
report pain similar to [[03d Ligamentous Injuries#Lateral|lateral sprains]] 
- pain throughout ==foot and on WB==
- ==tenderness== along peroneus longus tendon, cuboid groove, dorsolateral/plantar cuboid
- slight ==sulcus over dorsum of cuboid== or slight prominence fullness on plantar surface
- Erythema, edema [[ecchymosis]]
- ↓ active/passive ankle ROM
- pain on resisted eversion/inversion
- antalgic gait

## Assessment
Definitive diagnostic tests lacking but assessments that can help

**midtarsal adduction test:** Manipulation of ==midtarsal joint== passively in ==transverse plane== while ==stabilizing the calcaneus== ^bbe2ec

**midtarsal supination test:** Adding ==inversion & plantarflexion== to [[#^bbe2ec|adduction tests]] Pain may also be elicited by ==passive translation of cuboid dorsally or plantarly==

--- column-break ---

## Risk factors
several factors for developing cuboid syndrome
- midtarsal instability
- high BMI
- footwear
- long and intense exercise
- insufficient recovery
- training on uneven surfaces
- [[03d Ligamentous Injuries|sprains]]
- excessive pronation or supination
- weak fibularis longus, brevis, tertius → affect CC joint stability
