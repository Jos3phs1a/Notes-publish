# [[Knee ligaments#^6e01d6|ACL]] Injury

Intraarticular → poor blood supply → bad healing
Most frequent in pivoting sports
Associated with other knee injuries, 
- forming **Unhappy triad** with [[#Knee ligaments 6e01d6 MCL Injury|MCL Injury]] and [[Meniscal Injury]] 
- or [[posterolateral corner injury]] 
## Clinical Presentation
### Mechanism of Injury
--- start-multi-column: ID_6dla
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

- ==strong quads== contraction in near/full extension
- ==valgus force== with foot firmly planted
- excess external rotation of tibia/internal rotation of femur

- pain and rapid/delayed swelling
- "pop" sound followed by instability
- Difficulty weight bearing

### Diagnostic Tests
| Tests           | Sensitivity | Specificity |
| --------------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| Lachmann's      | 85%         | 94%         |
| Anterior Drawer | 41%         | 95%         |
| Pivot Shaft     | 24%         | 94%         |
MRI most helpful
X-ray good for ruling out fractures

#### Lachmann's Test
Put knee at 20-30° flexion → highest tension
Place ACL under tension with least contribution from other ligaments and muscles
Positive: excess anterior translation + soft end feel
#### Anterior Drawer Test
Test for excessive anterior translation of tibia from femur
Positive: excess anterior translation + soft end feel

# [[Knee ligaments#^6e01d6|PCL]] Injury

Intra Articular Injury
less common than [[#Knee ligaments 6e01d6 ACL Injury|ACL Injuries]] 
## Clinical Presentation

--- start-multi-column: ID_orh1
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

### Mechanism of Injury
associated with high-energy trauma
e.g. 
- falling on fully flexed knee
- forceful posterior translation of tibia
- large varus force
- severe hyperextension

--- column-break ---

### Symptoms
- Posterior sag
- Posterior knee pain
- Swelling but minimal
- Instability

--- end-multi-column

### Diagnostic Tests
| Tests                    | Sensitivity | Specificity |
| ------------------------ | ----------- | ----------- |
| Posterior Sag            | 79%         | 100%        |
| Posterior Drawer         | 90%         | 99%         |
| Reverse Lachman Maneuver | 62%         | 89%         |
| Reverse pivot shift      | 26%         | 95%         |
| Quads active test        | 54%         | 97%         |
#### Posterior Drawer Test
Test for excessive posterior translation of tibia from femur

# [[Knee ligaments#^6e01d6|MCL]] Injury

Extra Articular injury, slightly better prognosis
can manage conservatively if not full rupture

## Clinical Presentation

--- start-multi-column: ID_cbhk
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

### Mechanism
- Valgus Stress to partially flexed knee
- Contact or non contact
- Associated with [[#Knee ligaments 6e01d6 ACL Injury|ACL]] and [[Meniscal Injury]] 

--- column-break ---

### Clinical Presentation
- Pain 
- swelling
- "pop" sound 
- Instability

--- end-multi-column
### Special tests
- Valgus stress test
