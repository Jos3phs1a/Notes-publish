### Type 1 strains
MOI: Sprinting, excess load beyond tolerance of [[stretch shortening cycle]]
location: long head bicep fem proximal MTU
management: more acute decline in function, but quicker recovery

### Type 2 strains
MOI: extensive lengthening due to knee extension and hip flexion e.g. kicking forward splits
location: ischial tuberosity, proximal tendon of semimembranosus 
management: closer the site of maximum pain palpation to the ischial tuberosity, the longer the rehabilitation period(generally longer than [[#Type 1 strains]])

## Tell tale signs
### Subjective
- discomfort at ischial tuberosity region
- sitting discomfort
- pain spreading down thigh
- pain on hamstring loading
### Objective
#### Physical Exam
- palpable tenderness/pain

#### Loading tests
- single leg bent knee bridge(low load)
- long lever bridge(mod load)
- arabesque movements to single leg deadlift(high load)
# Patellar

## pathomechanics
- pain at inferior pole of patella
- load-related pain increases with demand on knee extensors (quads)
- activities + repititions involving [[stretch shortening cycle|SSC]] aka jumping, squatting create pain
- tenocyte changes, swelling, matrix degradation
## Tell tale signs
### Subjective
- discomfort below knee
- Worsen during load e.g. jump/squat, prolonged knee flexion, driving, stair climbing
- pain post 24 hours of load tolerance
### Objective
#### Physical Exam
- single-leg decline squat
- small hop vs slow hop
- Maximal hop
- leg extension/leg press
