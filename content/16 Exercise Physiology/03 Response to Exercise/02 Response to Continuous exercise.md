# Low maximal work capacity

-  Short term, 5-10 min of continuous light-moderate exercise

## CVS
--- start-multi-column: ID_uzt9
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```


- immediate ↑ [[Metrics of measuring Heart#**Heart Rate** (HR)|HR]]  + [[02a Cardiovascular response to exercise#Rate pressure product|RPP]] due to ↑ [[Control of Cardiac Output#Influence of Autonomic Nervous System|Sympathetic Activity]] 
- ↑ [[Metrics of measuring Heart#Stroke Volume|SV]] immediately via ↑ [[Processes affected by ANS action#^883c6a|Venous Returns]] and via Preload & [[Control of Cardiac Output#Frank-Starling Law|Frank-Starling law]]
- ↑ [[Metrics of measuring Heart#Cardiac Output (CO)|CO]] achieves **Steady state**/Plateaus as O$_2$ demands met

#### Peripheral
- ↑ [[Blood Pressure#^4ad161|MAP]] due to elevated SBP
- ↓ [[Total peripheral resistance]] due to vasodilation, 
- Overall , when CO↑, BP will ↑ as response but TPR↓ will *offset, keeping body within ideal range*

## RS 
--- start-multi-column: ID_i11y
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: right
```


**Triphasic (3 phases) response** of [[Hypernea]] 
- sharp initial increase in Minute ventilation (V̇$_E$)
- slower exponential rise for 2 mins
- steady state by 3rd min

#### Ventilation
- Tidal Vol (V$_T$) ↑  & Dead Space Vol (V$_D$) remains unchanged
- V$_D$/ V$_T$ ↓ 
- Frequency of ventilation ↑, but minimal and gradual
- Bronchodilation - ↓ airway resistance 

**Pressure gradient** remains unchanged:
- **Alveolar Ventilation(V̇$_A$)** sufficient to maintain alveolar partial pressure levels (P$_A$O$_2$) 
- **Arteriole partial pressure levels**  (P$_a$O$_2$) remains unchanged due to muscles using stored O$_2$ instead of blood 
- overall gradient maintained for oxygen diffusion = ==maintain efficient exchange==

Right wards shift in [[oxygen dissociation curve]]
- due to ↓ PO$_2$ in muscles
-  ↑ PCO$_2$ 
- ↓ pH
- ↑ Temperature

Note slight decrease of PCO$_2$ after longer duration due to removal rate > production rate

# High maximal work capacity

- Long term, >30 mins of moderate to heavy exercise 
- Initial responses are similar to [[#Low maximal work capacity]] but will differ past the 30 min mark

## CVS

--- start-multi-column: ID_4a5a
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

#### Cardiovascular Drift
changes in observed cardiovascular metrics due to factors like ==rising body temperature==, causing competing demands for blood between skin and muscles
#### Effects on system
- ↓ [[Metrics of measuring Heart#Stroke Volume|SV]] due to plasma loss and redirection of blood to cutaneous vessels for blood flow for [[temperature regulation|Heat dissipation]] 
- ↑ [[Metrics of measuring Heart#**Heart Rate** (HR)|HR]] to compensate for reduced venous returns and ventricular filling time → ↑ [[02a Cardiovascular response to exercise#Rate pressure product|RPP]]
- Slight ↓ in [[Blood Pressure#^90fc31|SBP]] due to ↓ venous returns, ↓ [[Total peripheral resistance|TPR]] to compensate for less blood flow

Overall: [[Metrics of measuring Heart#Cardiac Output (CO)|CO]] remains constant

### Ventilation

Main response: [[Hypernea]] with hyperventilation
- slower exponential rise for 3 mins
	rapid ↑ in demand → Initial ↑ in V$_T$ larger due 
	1:  causing similar increase in (V̇$_E$)
	2:  while V$_D$ remains unchanged, V$_D$/V$_T$ ↓ larger than[[#Low maximal work capacity| in Low work capacity]] 
- steady state ~ 5 mins
	Body adapts to ↑ demand
- **ventilatory drift** after 30 mins
	Reason for drift is inconclusive, but may be attributed to ↑ [[Body Temperature]], ↑ demand on muscles 
	Body trying to ==match perfusion needs== - *f* ↑ 

### U shape response
- Small ↓ then ↑ in PaO2 levels resulting in inefficiency of gaseous exchange due to 
- ↑ demand on fatigued muscles + limited [[Metrics of measuring Heart#Cardiac Output (CO)|CO]] and perfusion,  O$_2$ transfer not sufficient to maintain PaO$_2$ 

### Internal respiration
- PaO2 relatively constant *Despite shallow u-shape response*
- However, PvO2 (venous blood O2) ↓ due to higher demand + shift in [[oxygen dissociation curve]]
- Resulting in ↑ arteriovenous (a-vO2) diff

# Interval vs Continuous Exercise

- Overall no significant difference between the two forms. both will provide similar benefits

**Prescription** will differ based on patients needs
e.g. Interval may be better for patients who cannot sustain moderate continuous exercise due to COPD or CAD and have [[Dyspnoea]] and skeletal muscle dysfunctions
