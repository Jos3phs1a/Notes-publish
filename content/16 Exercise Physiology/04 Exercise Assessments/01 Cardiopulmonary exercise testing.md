
## Utility
- determine peak oxygen consumption(VO$_2$) levels of disability
- identify factors contributing dyspnoea exercise limitation
- objectively evaluate the impact of therapeutic interventions
	- oxygen
	- bronchodilators
	- vasodilators
	- exercise training
- screen for coexistent ischemic heart disease, peripheral vascular disease arterial hypoxemia
- "prehab" to minimise surgical risk
- assist in [[Basic principles of exercise training|exercise training]]

## Types of assessments

### Maximal
- subjects required to exercise to volitional exhaustion 
	ie. plateau in VO$_2$ /HR reaches within 90% predicted/HRR is within 15 bpm/RPE 9/10
- Pros: very accurate, direct results
- Cons: Requires technical equipment e.g. ECG,BP, ventilators, very stressful on participant 
[[01a Standardised Treadmill Tests|Standardised Treadmill Tests]]  
[[01d bike test|Bike tests]]
### Sub-maximal
- subjects do not exercise until exhaustion
- pros: easy to administer, less stressful
- cons: results are estimates
