# Post exercise Hypotension

- Drop in [[Blood Pressure]] below resting values after exercise
- Lasting hours post exercise
- Persistent drop in systemic vascular resistance that is not completely offset by cardiac output

### Possible mechanisms
- Central baroreflex reset → ↓ [[Processes affected by ANS action|Sympathetic activity]]
- [[histamine]] (vasodilator) receptor activation → ↓[[Total peripheral resistance|TPR]]
- Thermoreflex reset → ↑ blood flow 

#### Situational
- Hyperthermia
- Fluid loss
- venous pooling in legs
### Implications
- Can be applied to patients with (borderline) [[Hypertension]]
- Post exercise syncope (fainting and light headedness)
- Compromised orthostatic tolerance
- predict poor prognosis or high-risk coronary artery disease
# Exercise induced bronchoconstriction

- transient, reversible condition post exercise
- occurs in people with or without asthma
	within 2-5 mins post exercise, peaks after 10 mins, resolves after 60mins
- Symptoms: dyspnea, wheezing, cough, chest tightness, excessive mucus production

### Possible mechanisms
contraction of smooth muscle in airways due to
- ↑ ventilation of cold air → water loss from airways 
- Mast cell degranulation → inflammatory mediators → irritation
- Epithelial injury due to air pollutants/ long term chemical exposure such as chloramines(pool water)

### Implications
- Asthma-equivalent effects or asthma inducing - severe difficulty in breathing

### Management
#### Medicinal related
- Albuterol: short acting B2-agonist(smooth muscle relaxant)
- Corticosteroids (symbicort, serevent): long acting B2-agonist
- daily intake of leukotriene receptor agonist
#### Activity related
- pre-exercise warmup → induce refractory period
- modify activities for low minute ventilation
