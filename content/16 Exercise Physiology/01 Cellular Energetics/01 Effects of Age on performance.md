# Muscle strength

- Muscle strength peaks at age 20-25
- Relatively unchanged up to age 49 then declines moderately up to 80 years old
- Strength declines about 10% between 20-50 y/o
- avg 65 y/o has 75-80% of strength of 25y/o
- by age 80, further decline to 60-80% *Cause is inconclusive* but normally due to lower physical activity or sarcopenia (loss of muscle mass)

# Neurological

- 40% decline in spinal cord axons
- 10% decline in [[Physiological effects of cryotherapy#Nerve conduction velocity(NCV)|NCV]] 
- Impact Reaction time + time taken for muscle contraction/relaxation


# Physiological function

Age causes:
- ↓ [[Control of Cardiac Output|cardiac output]]  
- ↓ [[Metrics of measuring Heart|heart rate]]
- ↓ vascular conductance
- ↑ [[Blood Pressure]]
- ↑ Ventilation/perfusion mismatch

Due to age causing structural changes in cardiovascular, respiratory and musculoskeletal systems, resulting in lower O$_2$ intake and subsequent responses 


# Heart
- ↑ Myocardial Fibrosis
- ↓ Myocardial complaince
- ↓ Capillary/fiber ratio
- ↑ Ventricular wall thickness
- Cardiac enlargement
# Vascular system

- ↓ Aortic Compliance
- ↑ Rigidity
- ↓ Elastin causing thicker arteriole walls
- ↑ Collagen


# Lungs

- ↓ Elastic Recoil
- ↑ Mean Pulmonary Artery Pressure
- ↓ PaO$_2$
- ↑ Increased V/Q mismatch
- ↓ Reduced number and size of alveoli
