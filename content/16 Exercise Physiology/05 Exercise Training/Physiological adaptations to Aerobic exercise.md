# CVS adaptations

**Cardiac hypertrophy:**
- ↑ left ventricle cavity size
- size of cardiac muscle fibers/ thickening of walls
- ↑ left ventricular end-diastolic vol

**Cardiac Output:**
- max CO ↑, but ↓ CO during submax exercise due to better efficiency and redistribution of blood + [[#^b87cb7|improved oxidative capacity]]
- ↑ SV: ↑ contractility, compliance and ventricular filling
- ↓ HR at rest during submax exercise: ↑ strain on heart over long duraction → body compensates during rest by ↑ vagal tone, leads to brachycardia 
- ↓ BP(SBP & DBP) at rest + during submax exercise

- plasma volume expansion
- ↑ hemoglobin concentration

# Ventilatory Adaptations

**At rest/submax exercise:**
- lung vol and capacity little change due to large reserves
- ↓ breathing frequency
- ↑ V$_T$ + ↓ V$_E$ → ↑ extraction of O$_2$ as it stays longer in body
- ==↓V$_E$ due to ↓ VCO$_2$ due to ↓ blood [[lactate]] concentration ==
- ↓ V$_E$/VO$_2$ (ventilatory equivalent for oxygen)
- ↓ Oxygen cost of ventilatory muscles due to ↑ [[#^9eb607|efficiency]] → more O$_2$ for active muscles
- ↓ V$_E$/MVV(max voluntary ventilation; more breathing reserve)
right shift in [[oxygen dissociation curve]] → Oxygen unloading ↑

**Maximal exercise**
- ↑ V$_E$ (↑f,↑V$_T$) = breathing faster + deeper
