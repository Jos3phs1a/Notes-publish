**Definition:** Increase in CSA of muscle fibers

## Mechanism

**triggered by:**
1) mechanical forces/tension that induce intracellular signaling
2) overloading forces that disrupt muscle fiber structures and promote cell damage
	overcompensation of protein synthesis during eccentric contraction, mediated by autophagy process
3) metabolic stress 
	lactate/oxygen reactive substrates

protein synthesis rate > protein degradation rate
- ↑ number of sarcomeres and myofibrils added in parallel
- ↑ size and amount of myofibrillar contractile proteins (actin & myosin)

satellite muscle cells: precursor to muscle cells that become active when met with sufficient stimulus on skeletal muscles
- proliferate and fuse to existing cells to create new myofibers

**Overtraining:**
- may lead to severe muscle inflammation/injury
- [[Rhabdomyolysis]] → potentially fatal
