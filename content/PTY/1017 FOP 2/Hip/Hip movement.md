## Lumbopelvic rhythm
As lumbar spine is firmly attached to pelvis,  rotation of pelvis will change its configuration

2 types:

### Ipsidirectional rhythm

--- start-multi-column: ID_qr60
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

Pelvis & lumbar spine rotate same direction
- maximizes angular displacement of entire trunk relative to lower limbs mostly for increasing reach

### Contradirectional rhythm

--- start-multi-column: ID_z51i
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

Lumbar spine simaltaneously rotates opposite direction to that of pelvis
- keeps the trunk and upper extremities nearly stationary 
- allow pelvis and supralumbar trunk to move independently → ↑ mobility 
- e.g. person with fused lumbar spine cannot perform this

# Arthrokinematics

During hip motion, femoral head remains securely seated within acetabulum 
-  steep walls and relative depth + [[Hip joint stability#Acetabular labrum|Acetabular labrum]] restrict translation between articular surfaces to 2mm or less
Hip arthrokinematics follow conventional [[Convex-Concave principle]]
