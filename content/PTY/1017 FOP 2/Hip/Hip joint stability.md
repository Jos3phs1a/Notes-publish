- Hip joint is [[types of joints#^071d00|Ball & Socket]] 
- Femoral head covered by articular cartilage, thickest area ~3.5mm thick, no cartilage over fovea

- Hemispheric acetabulum, gives incomplete socket ~60-70° wide opening aka *acetabular notch*

# Ligament Teres

### Structure
- tubular, synovial-lined connective tissue
- runs between transverse acetabular ligament and fovea of femoral head

### Functions
Note: ==not enough literature== but may serve these purposes:
- protects acetabular artery(minimal blood) and conduit for foveal artery(branch of obturator artery)
- limit excess hip internal/external rotation
- sling supporting inferior part of femoral head during hip flexion/abduction
- contribute to proprioception


# Lunate Surface


--- start-multi-column: ID_mauk
```column-settings
Number of Columns: 2
Largest Column: standard
```

- Femoral head articulates with acetabulum along horseshoe shape
- covered by articular cartilage, thickest ==~ 3.5m,== corresponding to area of highest joint forces encountered during *midstance of gait cycle*
- **Midswing phase** 
	- 13% body weight
	- 20% lunate surface
- **Midstance phase**
	- 300% body weight
	- 98% lunate surface
- Remaining area within the acetabulum aka *Acetabular fossa* without articular cartilage allows space for [[Hip joint stability#Ligament Teres|ligamentum teres]], fat, synovial membrane, blood vessels

# Acetabular labrum

- Fibrocartilage ring over outer circumference/rim of acetabulum, transverse acetabular ligament spans the acetabular notch to complete ring
- poorly vascularized but well supplied by afferent nerves

Function: deepen joint cavity and *grip* femoral head allows ==increase bony congruency and joint stability== 

### mechanism
- gripping femoral head → forms *mechanical/suction seal* via negative intra-articular pressure
	effective in resisting initial 1-2mm joint distraction(separation)
- forms fluid seal → maintain synovial fluid, enhancing lubrication and reducing contact stress


# Close - packed position 

- unlike conventional [[Joint Congruency#**Closed-packed joints**| Close packed position]] definition, hip cannot be at max congruency due to [[Hip joint stability#Hip Joint Ligaments|Hip joint ligaments]] being taut aka at greatest tension 
- Hip is most congruent in ==flexion, external rotation, abduction==
