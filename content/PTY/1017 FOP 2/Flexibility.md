
**Definition**: Ability to move a joint through ==pain/symptom free ROM==

Types:
	Joint flexibility → joint angle
	Muscle flexibility → muscle length

1. Dynamic
	Active muscle contraction effect on ROM
	Aka Active mobility/ROM

2. Passive
	Extensibility of soft tissues without contractions
	Via gravity/manual/mechanical

### Range of Motion(Full)

^713ebd

Definition: Movement of segment within ==unrestricted range==
Can be **joint** ROM, **muscle** flexibility, peripheral nerve mobility
e.g. elbow flexion → forearm, shoulder flexion → arm

1.Functional
	ROM for functional activities e.g. drinking water 100°

2.Passive
	Produced by ==external force==, no voluntary muscle contraction e.g. PT/gravity/machine

3.Active
	Produced by active contraction of muscle crossing particular joint
Recall [[Active vs Passive]]

4.Active-assisted
	Active contraction + Assistance by external force provided to help muscle to complete motion e.g. manual/mechanical

### Stretching ###

^9681dc

Definition: 
==Any movement== that aims to ==increase [[Mechanics of Soft Tissue#^90d32d|extensibility]]== (muscle length) or ROM (joint angle),
Includes soft tissue e.g. joint capsule, ligaments, tendons not just muscle

| Aspect         | [[ROM Exercises]]                                   | Stretching Exercises                                                            |
| :------------- | --------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Focus          | Joint mobility and function                         | Muscle flexibility and length                                                   |
| Intensity      | Typically low intensity                             | can be low to moderate intensity, depending on stretch                          |
| purpose        | Maintain/Improve joint motion and prevent stiffness | Prevent muscle tightness, improve flexibility, and prepare muscles for activity |
| Usage in rehab | Common for post-surgery or injury recovery          | Used to improve flexibility after the injury or reduce tightness                |

### Mobility

General term for movement
Ability of body to effect functional ROM
Hypermobility → excess
Hypomobility → lacking (more common)
	Tightness: Shortening of muscles, contractile or noncontractile tissue
