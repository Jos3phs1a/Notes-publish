# Arthrokinematics

- movement occurs in sagittal plane
both joints have similar arthrokinematics, but different contributions based on the movement itself
- **Wrist Flexion**: **midcarpal joint** contributes **more movement** (approximately **60%**)
- **Wrist Extension**: **radiocarpal joint** contributes **more movement** (approximately **60-66%**), 
*literature is mixed, some state that contributions are roughly equal*
## Referencing Extension

radiocarpal joint
- ==convex== surface of the ==lunate== rolls dorsally on the ==radius== and simultaneously slides in a palmar direction

Midcarpal joint
- ==head of the capitate== rolls dorsally on the ==lunate== and simultaneously slides in a palmar direction

NOTE: everything explain is flipped when referring to flexion movement

### Ligaments
- ==restrict movement== + ==ensure stability== in closed packed position
- dorsal radiocarpal ligaments slackened 
- palmar radiocarpal ligaments become taut.
