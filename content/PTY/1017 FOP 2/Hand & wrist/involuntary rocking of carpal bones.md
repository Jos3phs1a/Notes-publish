proximal row of carpal bones “rocks” slightly into flexion and extension and, to a much lesser extent, twists.

radial deviation: proximal row flexes slightly. At 20 degrees of radial deviation, scaphoid flexes beyond neutral about 15 degrees, shortened stature with its tubercle having approached the radius. allows a few more degrees of radial deviation before complete blockage against the styloid process of the radius occurs.

ulnar deviation: proximal row extends slightly. At 20 degrees of ulnar deviation, scaphoid rotated about 20 degrees into extension which projects its tubercle distally
