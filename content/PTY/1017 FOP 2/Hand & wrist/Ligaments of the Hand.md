#### **Scapholunate ligament**
limits the flexion(palmar)/extension(dorsal) during [[involuntary rocking of carpal bones]] 
experiences stress during such movement, normally not a problem → but is common site of injury
**if injured:**
- lunate will tend to extend/face dorsally
- lunate & scaphoid extend/flex and rotate(supinate/pronate) excessively → bones rub against each other → degeneration 
	→ a lot of compensation required by ECRL, ECRB , FCU. APB

#### Inverted double V ligaments
2 pairs of ligaments:
- distal portion: medial (capitate to triquetrum) & lateral(capitate to scaphoid) legs of **palmar intercarpal ligament**
- proximal portion: palmar ulnocarpal & palmar **radiocarpal ligaments**
==Assist in stabilizing + guiding the movements ==

In neutral, all ligaments are slightly stretched
During movements, ligaments diagonal will become taut or slack

#### Lunotriquetral ligament
stabilize ulnar side of lunate relative to the triquetrum
If injured:
	- lunate dislocates towards volar/palmar/flexion
	- ECU pronates and extends the triquetrum to compensate
