# Arthrokinematics

similar to that of [[Flexion & Extension of wrist joints]] but in frontal plane


## Referencing Ulnar Deviation
Radiocarpal joint: 
- Distal portion of joint "initiates" movement therefore ==convex carpal bones moving on concave radius== 
- Based on [[Convex-Concave principle]], Carpal bones ==slide and roll in opposite direction==
- Ulnar deviation: carpal bones role in ulnar deviation, slide radial 
- [[involuntary rocking of carpal bones]] to allow increased ROM

Midcarpal joint:
- capitate rolling in an ulnar direction and sliding slightly radially

## radial deviation
- movements are opposite to that of ulnar deviation
However, radiocarpal movement is lesser due to **carpus impinges** against **radial styloid process** 
Thus, 85% of radial deviation occurs at midcarpal joints


Ligamentous structures aid in **restricting and stabilizing movement**
