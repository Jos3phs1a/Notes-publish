characteristic triangular shape
Function:
- protects ulnar side as no bony articulation, vulnerable compared to radial side with joint structures
- Transfers compression force away from radius (~20%)
- primary stabilizer, binds forearm bones together while allowing radius to rotate about fixed ulna
# Structure

#### TFC disc
- fibrocartilaginous disc in wrist that cushions carpal bones, radius, ulna
lies on pole of distal ulna
- attachments: 
	1) base: distal radius + ulna styloid process 
	2) apex: triquetrum
	3) sides: reinforced by dorsal & [[Triangular Fibrocartilage Complex (TFCC)#Palmar radio-ulnar joint ligament|Palmar capsular ligaments]] of distal radio-ulnar joint
	
- attaches directly/indirectly to components of TFCC

#### Palmar radio-ulnar joint ligament
Connecting from ulna notch of radius to head of ulna
#### Palmar ulnocarpal ligament
2 parts:
- ulnotriquetral ligament
- ulnolunate ligament
Proximal attachment: palmar radio-ulnar joint ligaments
Distal attachment: palmar aspects of the lunate and triquetrum

attachments indirectly secure position of TFC
#### Ulnar collateral ligament
attaches ulna styloid processes to pisiform, triquetrum, articular disc
aided by flexor & extensor carpi ulnaris muscles, ulnotriquetral ligament
