Definition: normal movement brought about by passive tension of flexor and extensor muscles

## Scenario
Active wrist extension → passive tension of digital flexors → fingers & thumb pulled into flexion
Active wrist flexion → passive tension of digital extensors → pulls fingers into extension


## Spinal cord injury
C6 tetraplegia who has near or complete paralysis of his digital flexors and extensors, but well-innervated wrist extensors.
can control wrist movement but not fingers.
Tenodesis creates passive tension to help stabilize grip of items
