
Relating Linear to Angular Acceleration:

Relating Distance(S) and Angular Distance($\theta$);
	Recall eqn of Circumference of circle = 2$\pi$ r
	$\theta$ = 2$\pi$
	S = circumference of circle
	r = radius
	Therefore S = $\theta$ r 

Relating Linear Velocity,
	$V_{linear} = \frac{Distance(S)}{Time(T)}= \frac{\theta r}{t}$, and $\omega=\frac{\theta}{t}$
	Therefore $V_{linear}=r\omega$
	
Relating Radial/Centripetal Acceleration to radius of circle,
	$A_r=\frac{v^2}{r}=r\omega^2$

Finding total acceleration from linear(tangential) and Centripetal

Since line of linear and centripetal are perpendicular to each other, apply Pythagoras thm.

$a_{total}=\sqrt{a_{radial}^2+a_{linear}^2}$
