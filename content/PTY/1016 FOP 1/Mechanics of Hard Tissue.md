
[[Anatomy of Hard Tissue#^b5b70e|properties of bones]] result in mechanical properties:

1. Nonhomogeneous - material & properties throughout the bone is different, cells organic, inorganic substances
2. Anisotropic - properties differ based on direction acted upon

e.g. cortical bone can withstand load better when applied longitudinal vs transverse

1. Viscoelastic - bone resist rapid loading much better than slow load, stiffer and stronger at high strain rates

1. Bones fall between ductile and brittle, can deform slightly before failure(rupture)

- Size and geometry

- Direction, magnitude, rate of applied loads

Factors affecting bone integrity

- Osteoporosis - reduces strength and stiffness by reducing apparent density
- Surgery - alter bone geometry
- Bone defects
- Stress concentrations - screw holes for pins & bone plates
